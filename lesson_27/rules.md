# Lesson 27

## Summary
We learn how adjectives work — they go AFTER the noun, agree in gender, and pluralize like nouns. We meet several words for "car", the color *rojo*, and shortcuts like *no puedo encontrarlo* ("I can't find it") for conversational speed.

## Rules
1. **Adjectives go AFTER the noun in Spanish** (opposite of English). "Global situation" = *la situación global*; "digital camera" = *la cámara digital*; "red car" = *el auto rojo*. There's some flexibility for emphasis, but standard position is after.
2. **Articles match noun gender**:
   - *la* (the, fem) / *el* (the, masc)
   - *una* (a, fem) / *un* (a, masc)
3. ***-ión* is feminine** — *la situación, la opinión*.
4. **Words for "car" vary by region**:
   - *coche* (Spain) — masculine. From "coach". *El coche, un coche.*
   - *carro* — masculine. Latin America. Note the rolling double *r*.
   - *auto* — masculine. Short for *automóbil*.
   - *máquina* — feminine. Means "machine"; *q-u* gives /k/ — /makina/.
5. **Gender belongs to the WORD, not the object**: even though all of *coche/carro/auto/máquina* mean "car", their gender depends on the word, not the thing.
6. ***Grande* = big** — same root as English "grand".
7. **Adjectives also pluralize**: when the noun is plural, the adjective adds *-s* too. *La casa → las casas* (the houses); *grande → grandes* (big, plural). So *las casas grandes* = the big houses.
8. **Plural of nouns**: feminine *la → las*, masculine *el → los*. *El auto → los autos*; *el carro → los carros*.
9. **Helper-verb shortcut**: when you don't know a verb form, use a helper like *puedo* + the "to" form. It's slightly longer, but FAST to build because *puedo* is something you say all the time.
   - *No puedo encontrarlo* (I can't find it) — easy shortcut.
   - *No lo encuentro* (I don't find it) — more native, but harder to build on the fly.
10. **Helper-verb shortcuts to remember**: *puedo, quiero, tengo que, debo* + "to" form. These let you communicate quickly even when you haven't memorized the full conjugation.
11. **"Can you see…?" = *¿Ves…?*** Spanish often skips "can" because we overuse it in English. *¿Ves el auto?* = "Do you see the car?" is more natural than *¿Puedes ver el auto?*. But both work.
12. ***Encontrar* (to find / encounter)**: *encuentro, encuentras, encuentra, encontramos, encuentran*. Stem split o → ue.
13. **"In" = *en*** (single word, e-n).
14. **Color *rojo* (red)**:
    - Spelled *r-o-j-o*.
    - *J* in Spanish is /kh/ (like a German "ch") — *rojo* /rokho/.
    - Initial *r* is the rolling *r*.
15. **Adjectives keep matching gender**: with a feminine word use the *-a* version (*roja*), with a masculine word use *-o* (*rojo*). *La casa roja*, *el auto rojo*.
16. **Reflexive + adjective sentence**: *me quedo en el auto rojo* — "I'm staying in the red car". Build it piece by piece: *me quedo* (I stay) + *en* (in) + *el auto rojo* (the red car).
17. **Have-past + reflexive + adjective**: *me he quedado en el auto rojo* (I have stayed in the red car). Pieces: *me he quedado* + *en* + *el auto rojo*.
18. ***Comprar* = to buy***. *He comprado* = I have bought. *Me he comprado* = I have bought myself.
19. **Build the verb backbone first**: when constructing a sentence, find the verb piece first ("I remain myself" → *me quedo*), THEN add "in", "the", "red", "car". The verb is the backbone.
20. **Don't reorganize little words randomly**: if you start with the verb, the little word naturally lives next to it. If you start with the little word, you may freeze.

## Vocabulary
- *la situación* — the situation
- *global* — global
- *la cámara* — the camera (feminine)
- *digital* — digital
- *comprar* — to buy
- *coche* — car (Spain, masc)
- *carro* — car (Lat. Am., masc)
- *auto* — car (short for *automóbil*, masc)
- *automóbil* — automobile
- *máquina* — machine / car (feminine)
- *grande* — big
- *grandes* — big (plural)
- *la casa* — the house
- *las casas* — the houses
- *encontrar* — to find / encounter
- *encuentro* — I find
- *no lo encuentro* — I don't find it
- *no puedo encontrarlo* — I can't find it
- *quedar* — to remain
- *me quedo* — I stay (I remain myself)
- *en* — in
- *rojo / roja* — red (m/f)
- *el / la* — the (m/f)
- *un / una* — a (m/f)
- *los / las* — the (plural m/f)

## Examples / Sentences
- *La situación.* — The situation.
- *La situación global.* — The global situation.
- *La cámara digital.* — The digital camera.
- *Una cámara digital.* — A digital camera.
- *Quiero comprar una cámara digital.* — I want to buy a digital camera.
- *Quiero comprar la cámara digital.* — I want to buy the digital camera.
- *Quiero comprarla.* — I want to buy it.
- *El coche.* — The car.
- *Un coche.* — A car.
- *La máquina.* — The machine / car.
- *Una máquina.* — A machine / car.
- *El carro.* — The car.
- *Un carro.* — A car.
- *La máquina grande.* — The big machine/car.
- *Las casas.* — The houses.
- *Las casas grandes.* — The big houses.
- *El auto grande.* — The big car.
- *Los autos grandes.* / *Los carros grandes.* — The big cars.
- *¿Ves el coche grande?* — Do you see the big car?
- *¿Puedes ver el coche grande?* — Can you see the big car?
- *No puedo encontrarlo.* — I can't find it.
- *No lo encuentro.* — I don't find it.
- *Quedar.* — To remain.
- *Quedarme.* — To stay (myself).
- *Quedo.* — I remain.
- *Me quedo.* — I stay.
- *Me quedo en el auto rojo.* — I'm staying in the red car.
- *Me he quedado en el auto rojo.* — I have stayed in the red car.
- *He comprado un auto rojo.* — I have bought a red car.
- *Me he comprado un auto rojo.* — I have bought myself a red car.
