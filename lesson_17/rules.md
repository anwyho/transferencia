# Lesson 17

## Summary
We finish our practice of *e → ie* and *o → ue* splits, learn the *we* form (*-amos / -emos / -imos*), and see why the *we* form does NOT split (the stress isn't on the root). We also dive into Arabic *al-* loanwords in Spanish.

## Rules
1. Quick recap of *e → ie* splits: *querer → quiero, quiere, quieren, quieres*; *perder → pierdo, pierde, pierden, pierdes*; *pensar → pienso, piensa, piensan, piensas, pensamos*; *entender → entiendo, entiende, entienden, entendemos*; *cerrar → cierro, cierra, cerramos*.
2. Quick recap of *o → ue* splits: *poder → puedo, puede, pueden, podemos*; *dormir → duermo, duerme, duermen*; *encontrar → encuentro, encuentra, encuentran, encontramos*; *mostrar → muestro, muestra, muestran, mostramos*; *colgar → cuelgo, cuelga, cuelgan, colgamos* (note also adds *-go* in I).
3. Splits don't ALWAYS happen. Example: *comer* doesn't split → *como*. There's no rule for which ones split — most do, but you have to try and listen for feedback.
4. If a vowel-harmony slip happens (e.g. saying *cuelge* instead of *cuelga* because *ue* invites another *e*), STOP and run back to the "to" form (*colgar*) — that puts you back in control.
5. The *we* form: chop *-ar/-er/-ir*, add *-amos / -emos / -imos*. (We learn the difference between *-er* and *-ir* in Lesson 18.) Examples shown so far in *-ar*: *tomamos, hablamos, encontramos, mostramos, pensamos, cerramos*.
6. The *we* form has TWO syllables added (*-amos*), so the stress moves: *toma → tomamos*. Stress is still penultimate, but the penultimate syllable is now *-a-* not the root.
7. Because the stress moves OFF the root in the *we* form, the root vowel is no longer under pressure — so it does NOT split. *encontramos* (not "encuentramos"); *mostramos*; *pensamos*; *cerramos*; *podemos*; *entendemos*; *perdemos*.
8. Words in English starting with *al-* (and not just ending in *-al* like "normal") usually come from Arabic — Arabic *al-* means "the" and got glued onto the noun. So Arabic loanwords often carry a built-in "the": alcohol = "the alcohol" originally.
9. *Tomar* = to take (also commonly to drink). *Tomo, toma, toman, tomas, tomamos*. *¿Qué tomas?* can be asking what you want to drink.
10. *Beber* = to drink (exists, but less used than *tomar*). *Una bebida* / *la bebida* = a drink (noun, feminine).
11. Other Arabic-origin Spanish words: *azúcar* (sugar — the *l* in *al-* assimilated to the *z*; in Spain pronounced /athucar/), *arroz* (rice — *al-ruz* → *arroz*; the *l* assimilated to *r*, giving the double *rr*), *álgebra* (algebra), *aceite* (oil), *aceituna* (olive), *alfombra* (carpet), *almohada* (pillow).
12. Spanish writes ONE *s* where English writes two (*posible* not *possible*; *pasión* not *passion*) — Spanish doesn't allow double *s*. But it DOES allow double *r* (*rr*) — that's the rolling r, distinct from a single *r*.
13. The Spanish *z* is /th/ in most of Spain, /s/ in Latin America.
14. Recap of question/idiom: *Pienso que sí* = I think so (literally "I think that yes"). *Que* = that.

## Examples / Sentences
- *Cuelgo / Cuelga / Cuelgan / Lo cuelgan / Los cuelgan / Los cuelga* — I hang / he hangs / they hang / they hang it / they hang them / she hangs them
- *No tomo alcohol* — I don't drink alcohol
- *No tomo azúcar* — I don't take sugar
- *Quiero arroz* — I want rice
- *¿Qué tomas?* — What are you drinking? / What will you have?
- *Hablamos* — we speak; *Hablo / Habla / Hablan / Hablas* — I/he/they/you speak
- *Encontramos* — we find (no split — stress moved off the root)
- *Mostramos* — we show
- *Pensamos* — we think
- *Cerramos* — we close
- *Pienso que sí* — I think so
