# Lesson 41

## Summary
The future tense ("will") and the conditional ("would") are built the same way: take the FULL verb (the "to" form) and add little endings borrowed from *haber*. They both stress the ending — Spanish "pushes the accent into the future."

## Rules
1. Three ways to talk about the future in Spanish: (a) use present tense + a future word like *mañana* (tomorrow) or *más tarde* (later); (b) *ir a* + verb = "going to" (most common); (c) the proper future tense.
2. *Bailar* = to dance (related to *ballerina*). *Bailarín* = dancer. *Vamos a bailar* = we are going to dance.
3. *Más tarde* = later (literally "more late"). *Bailamos más tarde* = we dance later. Or *Vamos a bailar más tarde*.
4. The future tense uses the SOUNDS of *haber*: *he* /e/, *has* /as/, *ha* /a/, *hemos* /emos/, *han* /an/. The *h* is silent.
5. To form the future: take the WHOLE verb (the "to" form, like *bailar*) and ADD on these endings: *-é, -ás, -á, -emos, -án*. Note: the ending takes the accent (stress).
6. *Bailar* → *bailaré* (I will dance), *bailarás* (you), *bailará* (he/she/it/you formal), *bailaremos* (we), *bailarán* (they/you guys).
7. The accent goes on the ending — Spanish "pushes the accent into the future." (Compare: in Greek, past tense pulls the accent BACK; here we push it FORWARD.)
8. The endings *-é, -ás, -á, -emos, -án* work for ALL verbs — *-ar, -er, -ir* — same set, no difference.
9. The CONDITIONAL ("would") works the same way: full verb + endings. Key sound is *-ía*. Endings: *-ía, -ías, -ía, -íamos, -ían*. Accent stays on the *í*.
10. *-ía* covers I, he, she, it, you (formal) — so for "would," I jump in with the he/she/you-formal group.
11. *Bailar* → *bailaría* (I/he/she/it/you formal would dance), *bailarías* (you informal would), *bailaríamos* (we would — accent stays on *í*), *bailarían* (they/you guys would).
12. Conditional uses the same endings for all verb types (-ar, -er, -ir).
13. Both futures (will and would) share three traits: (a) added on the FULL verb, (b) stressed on the ending, (c) one set of endings for all verb types.
14. *Ir* (to go) → *iría* (I/he/she would go). *No iría* = she wouldn't go.
15. *Comer* (to eat) → *comería* (I would eat). *Comería, pero no tengo tiempo* = I would eat, but I don't have time.
16. *Tiempo* = time (and weather). Related to English "tempo."
17. *Transformar* = to transform. *Transformaría* = it/I would transform. *Transformaría la situación* = it would transform the situation.
18. To say "it would GET transformed / undergo transformation," Spanish uses *se*: *Se transformaría* = it would get transformed. (Spanish doesn't have "get" — it uses "itself.")
19. *Contaminar* = to contaminate. *Contamina* = it contaminates. *Se contamina* = it gets contaminated. *Se contaminaría* = it would get contaminated.
20. Word-cue: most words ending *-tion* in English become *-ción* in Spanish. *Imagination* → *imaginación*. To make the verb, take off the *-ción* and add *-ar*: *imaginar*. (Convert /j/ in English to /kh/ in Spanish for the *g*.)
21. *Imagino* = I imagine. *Me imagino* = I imagine (myself) — adding "myself" doesn't change the meaning much.
22. *Que* = that (the connector). Same word as *qué* (with accent) = what — they're really the same word in two roles. Compare English: that → th-at; what → wh-at; remove *t*, swap with *w* and you get the question version.
23. *Me imaginaría que no quiere ir* = I would imagine that he doesn't want to go.

## Vocabulary
- *bailar* — to dance
- *bailarín* — dancer
- *más tarde* — later
- *mañana* — tomorrow / morning
- *haber* — to have (auxiliary)
- *he, has, ha, hemos, han* — sound source for future endings
- *-é, -ás, -á, -emos, -án* — future endings (will)
- *-ía, -ías, -ía, -íamos, -ían* — conditional endings (would)
- *ir* — to go
- *iría* — I/he/she would go
- *comer* — to eat
- *comería* — I would eat
- *tiempo* — time, weather
- *transformación* — transformation
- *transformar* — to transform
- *contaminación* — contamination
- *contaminar* — to contaminate
- *imaginación* — imagination
- *imaginar* — to imagine
- *imagino, me imagino* — I imagine
- *que* — that (connector)
- *qué* — what (question word)

## Examples / Sentences
- *Vamos a bailar.* — We are going to dance.
- *Bailamos más tarde.* — We dance later.
- *Vamos a bailar más tarde.* — We're going to dance later.
- *Bailaré.* — I will dance.
- *Bailaremos.* — We will dance.
- *Bailará.* — He/she/it/you (formal) will dance.
- *Bailarán.* — They/you guys will dance.
- *Bailarás.* — You (informal) will dance.
- *Bailaría.* — I/he/she would dance.
- *Bailaríamos.* — We would dance.
- *Bailarían.* — They/you guys would dance.
- *Bailarías.* — You (informal) would dance.
- *Iría.* — I would go.
- *No iría.* — She wouldn't go.
- *Comería.* — I would eat.
- *Comería, pero no tengo tiempo.* — I'd eat, but I don't have time.
- *Transformaría la situación.* — It would transform the situation.
- *Se transformaría.* — It would get transformed.
- *Contamina.* — It contaminates.
- *Se contamina.* — It gets contaminated.
- *Se contaminaría.* — It would get contaminated.
- *Imagino. / Me imagino.* — I imagine.
- *Me imaginaría.* — I would imagine.
- *No quiere ir.* — He doesn't want to go.
- *Me imaginaría que no quiere ir.* — I would imagine that he doesn't want to go.
