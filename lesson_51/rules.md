# Lesson 51

## Summary
We push *para* vs *por* further: *estoy para ti* (I'm there for you), *estoy por ti* (I'm here because of you). We meet "false friends" like *discutir* (to argue, not discuss) and *actual* (current, not actual), and learn that prepositions are followed by the "to" form, not *-ing*.

## Rules
1. *Estoy* (from *estar*) = I am. To say "I'm there for you" you don't need the word "there" — *estar* already implies a location/state. Just say *estoy para ti* = I'm for you (= I'm there for you).
2. When knocking on a door / messaging on internet, you say *¿Estás?* — "Are you?" — not "Are you there?" Same with phone: *¿Está Juan?* / *¿Juan está?* = Is Juan there?
3. *Estoy por ti* = "I'm here because of you" (not "for you"). *Estoy por usted/ti* = formal/informal.
4. *He venido por ti* = "I have come because of you". Always avoid translating "because of" as *porque de* — use *por*.
5. Another use of *por*: when "for" means in EXCHANGE for one thing for another. *Cambiar este por ese* = "I would change this one for that one" (swapping).
6. *Cambiaría este por ese* — drop the *t* of *este* to get *ese*. Demonstratives match gender and number: *cambiaría estos por esos* (masc/mixed), *cambiaría estas por esas* (fem).
7. (Note) *Por* gets written as "x" online (since *por* is the multiplication word too: *ocho por ocho* = 8×8). *Gracias x venir*. Don't confuse with English "x = kiss".
8. THE RULE for prepositions: After Spanish prepositions (*para, por, de, en, con, a*), use the "to" form (infinitive), NEVER the *-ing* form. *Gracias por venir* (NOT *viniendo*). *Gracias por llamar* / *Gracias por llamarme*.
9. This is "negative language transfer" — don't drag English habits in. Spanish speakers learning English often say "thanks for to call" — that's the trace.
10. *Cansado* = tired (masc); *cansada* = tired (fem). Adjective agrees with you.
11. *Estoy cansado/cansada de trabajar* = I'm tired of working. After *de*, use the "to" form (*trabajar*), not -ing.
12. Past from *estar*: *estaba* = I/he/she was. Plus all the same endings: *estábamos* (we), *estaban* (they), *estabas* (you).
13. *Forma* = form, way. *Es una forma de hacerlo* = "it's a way of doing it" (after *de*, "to" form).
14. *Creer* = to believe (two e's). *Crear* = to create. They share the "I" form: *creo* can mean "I believe" OR "I create" — context decides.
15. *No creo en discutir* = I don't believe in arguing. After *en*, "to" form.
16. False friends: *discutir* sounds like discuss but mostly means TO ARGUE. For "discuss" Spanish usually uses *hablar*. *Argüir* = to argue a point (formal).
17. False friend: *actual* doesn't mean "actual" — it means CURRENT. *Mi trabajo actual* = my current job. *Actualmente* = currently, at the moment.
18. *De esta forma* = "in this way" (literally "of this way"). Spanish uses *de* not *en* here.
19. Most Latin-English vocabulary maps reliably to Spanish, but a handful are false friends — watch out.

## Vocabulary
- *estar / estoy / estás / está / estaba / estábamos* — to be (states/locations)
- *para ti / para usted* — for you (inf/formal)
- *por ti / por usted* — because of you
- *venir* — to come (*he venido* — I have come)
- *cambiar* — to change, to exchange
- *ocho* — eight
- *gracias por…* — thanks for…
- *llamar / llamarme* — to call / to call me
- *cansado / cansada* — tired (m/f)
- *de* — of, from (preposition)
- *trabajar* — to work
- *forma* — form, way
- *hacer / hacerlo* — to do/make / to do it
- *creer* — to believe
- *crear* — to create
- *creo* — I believe / I create (ambiguous)
- *creación* — creation
- *discutir* — to argue (false friend — NOT discuss)
- *hablar* — to speak (also used for "discuss")
- *argüir* — to argue (a point)
- *actual* — current (false friend)
- *actualmente* — currently
- *mi trabajo actual* — my current job

## Examples / Sentences
- *Estoy* — I am
- *Estoy para ti* — I'm there for you
- *¿Estás?* — Are you (there)?
- *¿Está Juan? / ¿Juan está?* — Is Juan there?
- *Estoy por usted / Estoy por ti* — I'm here because of you
- *He venido por ti* — I have come because of you
- *Es por ti que hemos venido* — It is because of you that we have come
- *Cambiaría este por ese* — I would change this one for that one
- *Cambiaría estos por esos* — I would change these for those (m)
- *Cambiaría estas por esas* — I would change these for those (f)
- *¿Cambiarías estas por esas?* — Would you change these for those?
- *¿No cambiarías estas por esas?* — Wouldn't you change these for those?
- *¿Por qué no cambiarías estas por esas?* — Why wouldn't you change these for those?
- *Gracias por llamar* — Thanks for calling
- *Gracias por llamarme* — Thanks for calling me
- *Estoy cansado / Estoy cansada* — I'm tired
- *Estoy cansada de trabajar* — I'm tired of working
- *Estaba cansada de trabajar* — I was tired of working
- *Estábamos cansados de trabajar* — We were tired of working
- *Es una forma de hacerlo* — It's a way of doing it
- *No creo* — I don't believe
- *No creo en discutir* — I don't believe in arguing
- *De esta forma* — In this way
- *No creo en discutir de esta forma* — I don't believe in arguing in this way
