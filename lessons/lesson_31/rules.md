# Lesson 31

## Summary
We learn the full present-tense conjugation of *ser* (the "characteristics" to-be), see why Spanish words add an extra *e-* to English *st-/sp-/s+consonant* words, and learn to ask "what is he like?" with *cómo es* vs "how is he doing?" with *cómo está*.

## Rules
1. Spanish words can't start with *st-*, *sp-*, or *s + consonant*. So Spanish slaps an *e* on the front. That's why *state* → *estar*, *Spanish* → *español*, *Spain* → *España*, *student* → *estudiante*, *school* → *escuela*, *special* → *especial*, *spatial* → *espacial*, *strict* → *estricto*, *spectacular* → *espectacular*.
2. *Escuela* shows another trick: the *o* in *school* splits into *ue* in Spanish.
3. *Estar* = states. *Ser* = characteristics. *Ser* by itself can mean "being" — *un ser humano* (a human being). When it follows the noun, *humano* is acting like an adjective. But you could also say *el humano* (the human) — there it's a noun.
4. *Ser* in present tense is more irregular than *estar*:
   - *soy* — I am (-oy ending, like *estoy*, *voy*, *doy*)
   - *eres* — you (informal) are (the weirdest one — relate it to *es*)
   - *es* — he / she / it / you (formal) is/are
   - *somos* — we are (work backwards from *son*: drop *n*, add *-mos*)
   - *son* — they / you guys are
5. There are only FOUR verbs in all of Spanish whose "I" form ends in *-oy* instead of *-o*: *voy* (go), *doy* (give, from *dar*), *estoy* (be-state), *soy* (be-characteristic).
6. *Ustedes* is the plural of *usted* (you formal), but in most of the Spanish-speaking world *ustedes* is just the everyday "you guys" — it doesn't sound formal anymore once it's plural. Only some parts of Spain treat it as formal. (More on Spain later.)
7. *Ellos / ellas* — like adjectives, you can make *ella* plural to get the feminine "they": *ellas*.
8. Use *ser* for "what is he like?" / "what is she like?" — that's a CHARACTERISTIC: *¿Cómo es?*
9. Use *estar* for "how is he?" / "how is he doing?" — that's a STATE: *¿Cómo está?*

## Vocabulary
- *español* — Spanish
- *España* — Spain
- *estudiante* — student
- *escuela* — school
- *especial* — special
- *espacial* — spatial
- *estricto* — strict
- *espectacular* — spectacular
- *ser* — to be (characteristic) / a being
- *humano / el humano* — human / the human
- *soy* — I am
- *eres* — you (inf) are
- *es* — he/she/it is, you (formal) are
- *somos* — we are
- *son* — they / you guys are
- *posible* — possible
- *yo, él, ella, usted, ustedes, nosotros / nosotras, tú* — subject pronouns

## Examples / Sentences
- *Soy estudiante.* — I am a student.
- *Es posible.* — It is possible.
- *Yo soy.* — I am (with emphasis).
- *Él es. / Ella es.* — He is. / She is.
- *Ellos son. / Ellas son.* — They are (m / f).
- *Ustedes son.* — You guys are.
- *Nosotros somos. / Nosotras somos.* — We are (m / f).
- *Tú eres.* — You are (informal).
- *¿Cómo está?* — How is he? (state — how is he doing)
- *¿Cómo es?* — What is he like? (characteristic)
