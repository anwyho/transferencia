# Lesson 71

## Summary
More mood-tense practice with *contar* (to count / tell), *esperar* (to hope), *gustar* (to like). We also learn the "personal *a*" — the little *a* you put before a person who's having something done to them.

## Rules
1. *Contar* = to count, and also to recount / tell (especially stories). It's a "splits" verb: *cuento* = I tell. *Un cuento* = a story.
2. After *quiero que*, mood tense: *Quiero que me cuentes* = I want you to tell me. Plural: *Quiero que me cuenten algo* = I want you all to tell me something.
3. *Contar con* = to count on (Spanish uses "with" where English uses "on"). *Quiero que cuentes conmigo* = I want you to count on me.
4. Tip: pay attention to errors native Spanish speakers make in English ("you can count *with* me") — they reveal how the phrase works in Spanish.
5. After *espero que* (I hope that), mood tense: *Espero que cuentes conmigo*, *Esperamos que cuenten con María*, *Esperamos que lo cuenten a María* (we hope they recount it to María).
6. SPECIAL CASE — *ver* in mood tense. The present is *veo, ves, ve, ven*. If we built mood tense from *ven*, we'd get *van*, which collides with "they go." So we build from the *I* form *veo* (the only irregular present form of *ver*) and keep the *e*: mood tense is *vea, veas, vea, vean*.
7. PERSONAL *A*: when the verb's action is done TO a specific person, Spanish puts an *a* before that person. *Espero que vean a María* = I hope they see María.
8. The personal *a* exists because Spanish word order is flexible — *María* could come before or after the verb. The *a* shows that María is being seen, not doing the seeing.
9. If there's already another preposition (like *con*), you don't need the personal *a*: *contar con María* — no extra *a*.
10. Examples: *Espero que comprendas a David / Espero que comprenda a David*. Without the *a*, *Espero que comprenda David* would sound like "I hope David understands" because of flexible word order.
11. *A* + *el* = *al* (always written as one word). *A el hombre → al hombre.*
12. *Quiero que vea al hombre.* = I want him to see the man.
13. If you put the person BEFORE the verb, you can sometimes drop the *a*: *Quiero que el hombre vea* = I want the man to see (he's the one seeing). Both word orders are okay because of personal *a* clarifying.
14. *Me gusta que…* (I like that…) is also subjective, so it triggers mood tense: *Me gusta que la profesora visite* = I like that the teacher visits.
15. Order is flexible: *Me gusta que la profesora visite* / *Me gusta que visite la profesora* — both mean "I like that the teacher visits."
16. To say "I like that he visits the teacher": *Me gusta que visite a la profesora* (with personal *a* — the teacher is being visited).
17. Personal *a* is about SPECIFIC people. "I need to find the plumber" (specific) — uses *a*. "I need to find a plumber" (any plumber) — no *a*.
18. The personal *a* can also personify pets, or in creative expressions: *escapar a la muerte* = to escape death.
19. Becoming our own teachers: by listening, analyzing, and asking why, we internalize details like the personal *a* — and that's how learning a language stops being painful.

## Vocabulary
- *contar* — to count / to tell, recount
- *cuento* — I tell (verb) / a story (noun)
- *contar con* — to count on (literally "count with")
- *conmigo* — with me
- *esperar* — to hope / to wait
- *espero que* — I hope that (triggers mood tense)
- *ver* — to see
- *veo / vea / vean* — I see / mood-tense versions
- *comprender* — to understand
- *hombre* — man (silent h)
- *al* — to the (a + el)
- *profesor / profesora* — teacher (m/f)
- *gustar* — to please / be liked
- *me gusta que* — I like that (triggers mood tense)
- *visitar* — to visit
- *muerte* — death
- *escapar* — to escape

## Examples / Sentences
- *Quiero que me cuentes.* — I want you to tell me.
- *Quiero que me cuenten algo.* — I want you-all to tell me something.
- *Quiero que cuentes conmigo.* — I want you to count on me.
- *Espero que cuentes conmigo.* — I hope you count on me.
- *Esperamos que cuenten con María.* — We hope they count on María.
- *Esperamos que lo cuenten a María.* — We hope they tell it to María.
- *Espero que vean a María.* — I hope they see María.
- *Espero que comprendas a David.* — I hope you (informal) understand David.
- *Espero que comprenda a David.* — I hope you (formal) understand David.
- *Quiero que vea al hombre.* — I want him to see the man.
- *Quiero que el hombre vea.* — I want the man to see.
- *Me gusta que la profesora visite.* — I like that the teacher visits.
- *Me gusta que visite a la profesora.* — I like that he/she visits the teacher.
- *Escapar a la muerte.* — To escape death (personifying death with *a*).
