# Lesson 26

## Summary
We drill *quedar* with all our helper verbs (*voy a, quiero, debo, tengo que, puedo, pienso*) plus negation and questions. We meet *llamar* (to call) and the everyday "*me llamo* / *¿cómo te llamas?*" introductions.

## Rules
1. **Reflexive + helper verbs**: when you add a "to" form to a helper, the reflexive word matches the SUBJECT.
   - *Voy a quedarme* — I'm going to stay (I'm going to remain myself).
   - *Quiero quedarme* — I want to stay.
   - *Debo quedarme* — I must stay.
   - *Tengo que quedarme* — I have to stay.
   - *Puedo quedarme* — I can stay.
   - *Pienso quedarme* — I'm planning to stay (literally "I think to stay").
2. **Reflexive matches the doer, not the helper-subject's gender**: *Quieres quedarte* (you want to stay) — *te*, not *me*, because YOU are doing the staying.
3. **Reflexive position flexibility**: *me/te/nos* can stick to the end of the "to" form OR sit before the changed verb. Both right.
   - *Debemos quedarnos.* / *Nos debemos quedar.* — We must stay.
4. **Negation goes before everything**: *no te quedas aquí* (you're not staying here). *No puedo quedarme* (I can't stay).
5. **Questions = just intonation + ¿…?**: *¿No te quedas aquí?* (Aren't you staying here?). *¿Por qué no te quedas aquí?* (Why aren't you staying here?).
6. **"Going to stay" review**: *voy a quedarme* (I'm going to stay), *voy a quedarme hasta las siete* (I'm going to stay until 7).
7. **Number drill**: *siete* (7) — like start of "set" with e-split.
8. ***Septiembre* / *Setiembre***: September has two valid spellings in Spanish, with or without the *p*. The "set"-piece links it to *siete*.
9. ***Pensar* (to think)**: *pienso, piensas, piensa, pensamos, piensan*. **Stem-vowel split: e → ie** when stressed (just like *quiere, viene*). NOT in *pensamos* — the stress shifts to the ending.
10. ***Pensar* + "to" form = "plan to / be thinking of"***: *¿Piensas quedarte aquí esta noche?* = Do you plan on staying here tonight?
11. **Have-past with reflexive — the *me* goes BEFORE *haber***: *me he quedado* (I have stayed). NOT *he me quedado*. NOT *he quedado me*.
12. **Position rule (sharper)**: little words go BEFORE the **changed verb**, not just "at the beginning". When nouns or other words are around, "before the changed verb" gives accurate placement.
13. **Build with logic, not feel**: when unsure where to place a word, work it out by rule — don't pick by what "sounds right". If your rule is wrong, the mistake will teach you.
14. **Negative + reflexive past**: *no nos hemos quedado* (we haven't stayed); *¿tú no te has quedado?* (haven't YOU stayed?).
15. ***Llamar* = to call / phone***. Double *l* sounds /y/ — *llamar* /yamar/.
16. **"My name is…" = *me llamo …*** ("I call myself …"). Spanish doesn't usually say "my name is" — it says "I call myself". *Me llamo Mihalis.*
17. **"What's your name?" = *¿Cómo te llamas?*** ("How do you call yourself?"). *Cómo* = how.
18. ***Llamar* set**: *llamo, llamas, llama, llamamos, llaman*. Reflexive: *me llamo, te llamas, se llama, nos llamamos, se llaman*.
19. ***Deber* = "to owe / to must"**. *Debo* = I must / I owe; *debemos* = we must.

## Vocabulary
- *quedar* — to remain
- *me quedo, te quedas, nos quedamos* — I/you/we stay
- *quedarme, quedarte, quedarnos* — to stay (for I/you/we)
- *aquí / acá* — here
- *siete* — seven
- *septiembre / setiembre* — September
- *deber* — to owe / to must
- *debo, debemos* — I must / we must
- *tener que* — to have to
- *tengo que, tienes que* — I/you have to
- *poder* — to be able
- *puedo, podemos, puedes* — can (I/we/you)
- *pensar* — to think
- *pienso, piensas, piensa, pensamos, piensan* — think (conjugated)
- *piensas quedarte* — you plan to stay
- *qué* — what
- *por qué* — why
- *no* — no / not
- *llamar* — to call / phone
- *me llamo* — my name is / I call myself
- *te llamas* — your name is / you call yourself
- *cómo* — how
- *esta noche* — tonight

## Examples / Sentences
- *Te quedas aquí.* — You're staying here.
- *No te quedas aquí.* — You're not staying here.
- *¿No te quedas aquí?* — Aren't you staying here?
- *¿Por qué no te quedas aquí?* — Why aren't you staying here?
- *Voy a quedarme.* — I'm going to stay.
- *Voy a quedarme hasta las siete.* — I'm going to stay until seven.
- *Quiero quedarme.* — I want to stay.
- *¿Quieres quedarte?* — Do you want to stay?
- *Debemos quedarnos.* / *Nos debemos quedar.* — We must stay.
- *Tengo que quedarme.* — I have to stay.
- *Tienes que quedarte.* — You have to stay.
- *Tienes que quedarte aquí.* — You have to stay here.
- *Quiero saber por qué tienes que quedarte aquí.* — I want to know why you have to stay here.
- *Puedo quedarme.* — I can stay.
- *No puedo quedarme.* — I can't stay.
- *No puedo quedarme esta noche.* — I can't stay tonight.
- *Podemos quedarnos.* — We can stay.
- *No podemos quedarnos esta noche.* — We can't stay tonight.
- *¿Qué piensas?* — What do you think?
- *¿Piensas quedarte aquí esta noche?* — Are you planning to stay here tonight?
- *Nos hemos quedado.* — We have stayed.
- *No nos hemos quedado.* — We haven't stayed.
- *Te has quedado.* — You have stayed.
- *¿Te has quedado?* — Have you stayed?
- *¿Tú te has quedado?* — Have YOU stayed?
- *¿Tú no te has quedado?* — Haven't YOU stayed?
- *Llamar.* — To call.
- *Llamo.* — I call.
- *Me llamo.* — I call myself / my name is.
- *Me llamo Mihalis.* — My name is Mihalis.
- *Te llamas.* — Your name is.
- *¿Cómo te llamas?* — What's your name?
