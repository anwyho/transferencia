# Lesson 09

## Summary
We meet *saber* ("to know"), the words *sí* (yes) and *si* (if), and we stitch big sentences together piece by piece. We also see one exception (explanation = *explicación*, not *explanación*).

## Rules
1. ***Saber* = "to know."*** Memory hook: British slang "he's got savvy." Savvy comes from Latin *sapere*. Spelled with *b*, but English kept the /v/ sound — another bridge between *v* and *b*.
2. ***Quiero saber* = "I want to know."*** Conjugation reminder: *quiero* (I), *quiere* (he/she/it/you-formal).
3. ***Sí* (with accent) = "yes."*** *Si* (without accent) = "if." Same letters, different word.
4. **Stitch long sentences slowly, block by block.*** "I want to know if you want to know" = *quiero saber* + *si* + *quiere saber* → *Quiero saber si quiere saber.*
5. **Once you've said a chunk, drop it from your head and focus on the next one.*** Don't try to hold the whole sentence at once.
6. **Recall is a feeling, not a fact.*** If you panic ("do I know it or not?"), game over. Stay relaxed and ask "what was that related to?" — let the word surface.
7. **Words of motion still take *a* before a following verb.*** *Quiere venir a la casa a visitarme* = "She wants to come to the house to visit me." Two *a*s — one is "to the house," one is "to visit me" (because of *venir*).
8. **You can stack: *quiero saber por qué no quiere venir a la casa a visitarme*** = "I want to know why she doesn't want to come to the house to visit me." Build it slowly.
9. **Confidence is the goal: try to say things you think you can't.*** Because we have so much shared Latin vocabulary, we know more Spanish than we realize — but only if we try.
10. ***Iniciar* = "to start" / "to initiate."*** From "initiation." So *quiero iniciarlo ahora* = "I want to start it now."
11. ***Justificación → justificar* (to justify).*** Reflexive: *no intento justificarme* = "I'm not trying to justify myself."
12. **Exception: "explanation" is *explicación*, not *explanación*.*** The verb is *explicar* (to explain). English has the same root in *inexplicable.*
13. **In Spanish you can just say "explain you something" — no "to" needed.*** *Quiero explicarte algo* = "I want to explain something to you" (literally "I want to explain-you something").
14. **/kh/ sound recap.*** *justificación* starts with /kh/ (j sound). Same throaty friction as *Argentina*.
15. ***Pero* = "but"** (recap). Useful for "I'm going to invite him, but I'm not going to invite him now."

## Vocabulary
- *saber* — to know
- *sí* — yes
- *si* — if
- *quiere* — he/she wants, you (formal) want
- *invitarme* — to invite me
- *invitarlo* — to invite him / it / you (formal)
- *imaginación* — imagination
- *imaginar* — to imagine
- *imaginarlo* — to imagine it
- *iniciar* — to start / initiate
- *iniciarlo* — to start it
- *informarme* — to inform me / to let me know
- *justificación* — justification
- *justificar* — to justify
- *justificarme* — to justify myself
- *explicación* — explanation (exception — not *explanación*)
- *explicar* — to explain
- *explicarte* — to explain to you
- *inexplicable* — inexplicable
- *intento* — I try / I'm trying
- *pero* — but
- *ahora* — now

## Examples / Sentences
- *Quiero saber.* — I want to know.
- *No quiero saber.* — I don't want to know.
- *Quiere saber.* — He/she wants to know.
- *No quiere saber.* — He/she doesn't want to know.
- *¿Por qué no quiere saber?* — Why doesn't he/she want to know?
- *Quiero saber por qué no quiere saber.* — I want to know why he/she doesn't want to know.
- *Quiero saber si quiere saber.* — I want to know if you want to know.
- *Quiero saber si quiere invitarme.* — I want to know if he/she wants to invite me.
- *No quiero imaginarlo.* — I don't want to imagine it.
- *Quiero saber si quiere venir a la casa a visitarme.* — I want to know if she wants to come to the house to visit me.
- *Quiero saber por qué no quiere venir a la casa a visitarme.* — I want to know why she doesn't want to come to the house to visit me.
- *Voy a invitarlo, pero no voy a invitarlo ahora.* — I'm going to invite him, but I'm not going to invite him now.
- *Quiere iniciarlo ahora.* — He/she wants to start it now.
- *¿Por qué no quiere informarme?* — Why doesn't he want to let me know?
- *No intento justificarme.* — I'm not trying to justify myself.
- *Pero quiero explicar.* — But I want to explain.
- *Pero quiero explicarte algo.* — But I want to explain something to you.
- *No intento justificarme, pero quiero explicarte algo.* — I'm not trying to justify myself, but I want to explain something to you.
