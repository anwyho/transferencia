# Lesson 89

## Summary
The teacher steps back and shows us how to **make our own rules** by analyzing patterns we hear in the wild. We don't need to be told everything — we have enough analytical tools by now to figure out the rest. Three little puzzles: when *demasiado* changes, when feminine words use *el*, and when *uno/alguno/ninguno* shorten to *un/algún/ningún*.

## Rules
1. The big idea: we've learned enough to **become our own rule-makers**. When you hear something you don't quite understand, break it apart, compare cases, and figure out the pattern. Don't memorize blindly — analyze.
2. *Demasiado* (too / too much / too many) breakdown: *de + más + -iado* → "of more, -iado." On its own, *de más* can mean "extra/spare": *Tengo cuatro de más* = "I have four extra / four too many."
3. **Rule for *demasiado* (puzzle 1):** when it goes with a NOUN, it changes to match (gender + number): *demasiadas casas, demasiados coches.* When it goes with an ADJECTIVE, it does NOT change: *demasiado agresivo, demasiado agresiva, demasiado agresivos, demasiado agresivas.* The adjective changes — *demasiado* stays put.
4. **Rule for *el* with feminine words (puzzle 2):** some feminine words take *el* in the singular: *el ala* (the wing), *el agua* (the water), *el aula* (the classroom). The pattern: they're feminine, but they have **two syllables AND begin and end in *a***. We say *el agua* to avoid the awkward *la-ala-ala* sound clash.
5. We know they're STILL feminine because the adjective agrees as feminine: *el agua fría* (cold water — *fría*, not *frío*). Don't be fooled by the *el*.
6. Compare: *la almohada* (the pillow) and *la alfombra* (the carpet) DO use *la* — even though they start with *a* — because they have more than two syllables. Only the short two-syllable feminine *a-...-a* words flip to *el*. Teacher says don't stress about this — you'll forget it; just have the analytical tool to figure it out when you meet it.
7. **Rule for *uno/alguno/ninguno* (puzzle 3):** these three words drop their final *-o* before a masculine noun.
   - *uno → un* (a / one) before masc. noun: *un hombre, un coche.*
   - *alguno → algún* (some) before masc. noun: *algún hombre, algún coche.*
   - *ninguno → ningún* (no/none) before masc. noun: *ningún hombre, ningún coche.*
8. By itself (no noun following), the full form stays: *Quiero uno.* (I want one.) *No quiero ninguno.* (I want none.)
9. The shortening **only happens with masculine**. Feminine keeps the full form: *una mujer, alguna mujer, ninguna mujer.*
10. Notice that *algún* and *ningún* take a written accent on the *u* (because the stress lands on that final syllable when the *o* drops).
11. Teacher's philosophy/closer: analyze the language to take CONTROL of it. Whatever you don't take control of will end up controlling you. Solve technical problems by understanding the *why*, not by memorizing what's correct. Once your technical problems are solved, you can live freely in the language and "become somebody else who is also you."

## Vocabulary
- *demasiado* — too / too much / too many
- *de más* — extra / spare ("of more")
- *casa / casas* — house / houses
- *coche / coches* — car / cars
- *agresivo / agresiva / agresivos / agresivas* — aggressive (m./f./pl. forms)
- *ala* — wing (fem., but takes *el*)
- *agua* — water (fem., but takes *el*)
- *aula* — classroom / hole (fem., but takes *el*)
- *frío / fría* — cold (m./f.)
- *almohada* — pillow (fem.)
- *alfombra* — carpet (fem.)
- *uno / un* — one / a (m.)
- *alguno / algún* — some (m.)
- *ninguno / ningún* — none / no (m.)
- *una / alguna / ninguna* — feminine forms (no shortening)
- *hombre* — man
- *mujer* — woman

## Examples / Sentences
- *Tengo cuatro de más.* — I have four too many / four spare.
- *Demasiadas casas, demasiados coches.* — Too many houses, too many cars.
- *Demasiado agresivo / agresiva / agresivos / agresivas.* — Too aggressive (m./f./pl.) — *demasiado* itself doesn't change.
- *El agua fría.* — The cold water.
- *El ala / el agua / el aula.* — The wing / the water / the classroom.
- *La almohada / la alfombra.* — The pillow / the carpet.
- *Un hombre / algún hombre / ningún hombre.* — A man / some man / no man.
- *Una mujer / alguna mujer / ninguna mujer.* — A woman / some woman / no woman.
- *Quiero uno. No quiero ninguno.* — I want one. I don't want any.
