# Lesson 07

## Summary
We learn the future-ish form *voy a + verb* ("I am going to ___"), meet the plural *los* ("them"), and learn a special quirk: verbs of motion connect to the next verb with an extra *a*.

## Rules
1. **For idioms English uses ("carry on"), find the Latin cousin.*** "Carry on" → continuation → *continuar* (to continue). Always look for the -tion word that matches the meaning.
2. **Spanish "I am going to ___" = *voy a + verb*** — exactly like English "I'm going to eat." Used to talk about the future.
3. **The extra *a* between *voy* and the next verb** is there because *voy* is a verb of motion. Verbs of motion (*voy*, *vengo*, *paso*) want an *a* before connecting to another verb — it shows the feeling of motion, even when you're not literally moving.
4. **Examples of the *voy a* pattern:** *Voy a confiscarlo, voy a examinarlo, voy a verlos, voy a intentar, voy a pasar.*
5. **Some verbs change a letter when becoming a verb.*** *Demostración → demostrar* (no n). Then drop the *de-* and you get *mostrar* (to show). Look for these little hooks.
6. ***Algo* = "something."*** Pronounced /AL-go/.
7. ***Ver* = "to see."*** Memory hook: English "view."
8. ***Te quiero* recap: te = you (informal).*** Stick it onto the verb: *quiero mostrarte algo* = "I want to show you something."
9. ***Lo* (it/him) → plural is *los* (them).*** Just add *s*. English hides this (him vs them look unrelated), but Spanish makes it obvious. *Quiero verlos* = "I want to see them." *Voy a verlos* = "I'm going to see them."
10. **No *a* needed when the first verb isn't a motion verb.*** *Quiero verlos* (I want to see them — no a). *Intento verlos* (I'm trying to see them — no a). *Voy a verlos* (I'm going to see them — yes a, because *voy* moves).
11. ***Intentar* = "to try" (the to-form).** So *voy a intentar* = "I'm going to try."
12. **You can stack motion verbs:** *voy a intentar verlos* — "I'm going to try to see them." Only the *voy* needs the *a*; *intentar* is not a motion verb.
13. ***Pasar* = "to pass / to pass by."*** Another verb of motion. *Voy a pasar a visitarte* = "I'm going to pass by to visit you" — note both *a*s: one for *voy* (motion), one for *pasar* (motion).
14. ***Lo* can also mean "you" formal.*** So *voy a pasar a visitarlo* = "I'm going to pass by to visit you (formal)" or "to visit him."
15. **General rule for motion-verb connecting *a*:** when a motion verb is followed by another verb (a "to" form), put *a* between them. Many important motion verbs do this.
16. **Be careful: *voy a intentar verlos* (no extra *a* before verlos), but *voy a pasar a visitarlo* (yes *a*).** *Intentar* isn't motion; *pasar* is.

## Vocabulary
- *continuación* — continuation
- *continuar* — to continue
- *continuarlo* — to continue it
- *demostración* — demonstration
- *demostrar* — to demonstrate (note: no n in Spanish version)
- *mostrar* — to show
- *mostrarte* — to show you
- *algo* — something
- *ver* — to see
- *verlos* — to see them
- *intentar* — to try
- *los* — them (plural of lo)
- *pasar* — to pass / pass by
- *confiscarlo* — to confiscate it
- *examinarlo* — to examine it
- *visitarte* — to visit you (informal)
- *visitarlo* — to visit him / it / you (formal)
- *formal* — formal
- *formalmente* — formally

## Examples / Sentences
- *Quiero continuar.* — I want to continue.
- *Quiero continuarlo.* — I want to continue it.
- *Intento continuar.* — I'm trying to continue.
- *Voy a continuar.* — I'm going to continue.
- *Voy a confiscarlo.* — I'm going to confiscate it.
- *Voy a examinarlo.* — I'm going to examine it.
- *Quiero verlos.* — I want to see them.
- *Voy a verlos.* — I'm going to see them.
- *Intento verlos.* — I'm trying to see them.
- *Voy a intentar verlos.* — I'm going to try to see them.
- *Voy a pasar a visitarte.* — I'm going to pass by to visit you.
- *Voy a intentar pasar a visitarte.* — I'm going to try to pass by to visit you.
- *Quiero mostrarte algo.* — I want to show you something.
- *Quiero pasar a mostrarte algo.* — I want to pass by to show you something.
