# Lesson 56

## Summary
Prepositions in Spanish never sit at the end of a sentence — they get moved to the front. We learn how to say "the girl I work with" by saying "the girl with which I work", and meet *cuál* (which) and *quién/quiénes* (who).

## Rules
1. Spanish prepositions (those little movement/position words) NEVER come at the end of a sentence. In English we say "Where are you from?" — in Spanish you flip it: *¿De dónde eres?* = "From where are you?"
2. Same for "Where are you going?" → *¿A dónde vas?* = "To where are you going?"
3. With country names we don't say "the". *Vamos a Estados Unidos* = "We go to United States" (no *los*).
4. "Working backwards" trick: if you see a Spanish word like *Unidos*, peel off the *-ido* ending to find the verb. Take off *-ido* → leaves *un-* → add back *-ir* or *-er*. Try both, the right one will sound right (*unir* = to unite). The more Spanish you hear, the better your gut gets.
5. To say "the girl I work with", flip it: "the girl with which/that/who I work" — *La chica con que trabajo*. The preposition goes BEFORE *que*, not at the end.
6. *Que* alone covers "that / which / who" in this structure — easy mode.
7. You also have fancier options that all work: *con cual*, *con la cual*, *con la que*, *con quien*. *Cual/cuales* and *quien/quienes* match number. *La cual / la que* match feminine. Don't worry about them now — *que* works for everything.
8. Don't say *yo* (I) unless you want to emphasize: *La chica con que trabajo* (no *yo* needed).
9. The line-in-the-past of *trabajar* is *trabajaba* ("I was working / used to work"), made with *-aba* for *-ar* verbs.
10. *Ir* (to go) is irregular in the line-in-the-past: *iba* ("I/he/she/it/you-formal was going"). Mnemonic: it's like *-aba* and *-ía* mashed together; or like the present *va* with an "i" before → *iba*.
11. *Cuál* (c-u-a-l) means "which". Use it for choosing one out of options: *¿Cuál quieres?* = "Which one do you want?" — no need for "one".
12. The temptation to repeat the preposition at the end (English habit) is wrong in Spanish. *No sé con cuál quiere hacerlo* — NOT "...con cual quiere hacerlo CON". Drop that habit.
13. *Quién* means "who". Use it with *he/she/it* verb form: *¿Quién va?* = "Who is going?"
14. Plural of *who* is *quiénes* — used when you know it's more than one person: *¿Quiénes van?* = "Who (plural) is going?" — and the verb matches.
15. With *estar* + location, the "here" can be dropped because *estar* already implies state/location. *¿Estás?* through a door = "Are you (there)?"
16. To get "have to" we use *tener que*: *tengo que* = "I have to". So *No sé con quién tengo que hacerlo* = "I don't know with whom I have to do it".
17. With *quién/quiénes*, all the prepositions float to the front: *con quién*, *para quién*, *por quién*, *de quién*.

## Vocabulary
- *para* — for (purpose/recipient)
- *por* — for (because of / through)
- *de* — from / of
- *con* — with
- *en* — in / on
- *eres* — you are (informal, *ser*)
- *vas* — you go (informal, *ir*)
- *Estados Unidos* — United States
- *unir* — to unite
- *trabajar* — to work
- *trabajo* — I work / a job
- *trabajaba* — I was working / used to work
- *la chica* — the girl (informal, common)
- *el chico* — the boy
- *que* — that / which / who (relative)
- *cual / cuales* — which (relative form, matches number)
- *la cual / el cual* — which (matches gender)
- *quien / quienes* — who / whom (matches number)
- *iba* — I/he/she/it was going (line past of *ir*)
- *fiesta* — party
- *cuál* — which? (question word)
- *quién / quiénes* — who? (question, sing/pl)
- *sé* — I know (very irregular *saber*)
- *aquí* — here
- *tengo que* — I have to
- *hacerlo* — to do it

## Examples / Sentences
- *¿De dónde eres?* — Where are you from?
- *¿A dónde vas?* — Where are you going?
- *Vamos a Estados Unidos.* — We're going to the United States.
- *La chica con que trabajo.* — The girl I work with.
- *La chica con la cual trabajo. / La chica con la que trabajo. / La chica con quien trabajo.* — Same idea, fancier.
- *Es la chica con que trabajaba.* — It's the girl I was working with.
- *La chica con que iba a la fiesta.* — The girl I was going to the party with.
- *¿Cuál quieres?* — Which one do you want?
- *No sé cuál quiere.* — I don't know which one he wants.
- *No sé cuál quiere ver.* — I don't know which one he wants to see.
- *No sé con cuál quiere hacerlo.* — I don't know which one he wants to do it with.
- *¿Quién va?* — Who is going?
- *¿Quiénes van?* — Who (plural) is going?
- *¿Quién está aquí? / ¿Quién está?* — Who is here? / Who is (here)?
- *No sé con quién tengo que hacerlo.* — I don't know who I have to do it with.
- *No sé con quiénes tengo que hacerlo.* — I don't know who (plural) I have to do it with.
- *No sé para quién tengo que hacerlo.* — I don't know who I have to do it for.
- *No sé por quién tengo que hacerlo.* — I don't know who I have to do it because of.
