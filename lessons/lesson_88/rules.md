# Lesson 88

## Summary
We finish up the irregular point-in-the-past family. Same recipe: irregular base + mixed *-ar*/*-er-ir* endings + no accent. We meet *puse, hice, vine, traje, fui*. Then we use the *they*-form to build the **past mood tense** for these irregulars (e.g. *fueron → fuera*, *tuvieron → tuviera*, *hicieron → hicieran*).

## Rules
1. *Poner* point-in-past: base is *pus-* (p-u-s). *Puse* (I put), *pusiste* (you put), *puso, pusimos, pusieron*. No accent on the endings: *puse* not *pusé*.
2. Now we can see why English has *-pose* in *compose, expose, oppose* — those words come from Latin *pon-* and the past *pus-*. *Componer = compose, exponer = expose, oponer = oppose.* You can hear the *-pose* sound in *puse, pusiste*.
3. *Hacer* point-in-past: base is *hic-* (h-i-c). *Hice* (I did/made), *hiciste, hicimos, hicieron*. EXCEPT for *he/she/you-formal*, where keeping a *c* would spell "hico" — so it changes to a *z*: *hizo* (he/she made/did). Spelling rule: *c* → *z* before *o* to keep the soft "s" sound.
4. *Venir* point-in-past: base is *vin-*. *Vine* (I came), *vino* (he came), *vinimos, vinieron*. Pun joke: *vino* also means "wine" — *Vino con vino* = "He came with wine."
5. *Traer* point-in-past: base is *traj-*. *Traje* (I brought), *trajo, trajimos, trajeron*. Bonus: *traje* also means "suit" (the kind you wear) — masculine. *Traje un traje* = "I brought a suit."
6. **Extra-irregular: *fui*.** The point-in-past *fui* is irregular AND it's the same form for BOTH *ser* AND *ir*. *Fui* = "I was" OR "I went." *Fue* = "he/she/it was" OR "he/she/it went." Context tells you which.
7. Full conjugation: *fui, fuiste, fue, fuimos, fueron*. So *No fui* = "I didn't go" (or "I wasn't"). *No fue* = "he didn't go / he wasn't."
8. **Sound rule: dropped *i* in *they*-forms.** It "should" be *fuieron*, *trajieron*, *dijieron* — but those are awkward to say, so the *i* falls away for efficiency: *fueron, trajeron, dijeron*.
9. **Building the past mood tense from irregulars.** Recipe stays the same: take the *they*-form of point-in-past, drop *-on*, you've got the past-mood base. Add normal mood-tense person endings. We go from *they* (not from *to*-form) precisely because that carries the irregularities over.
   - *fueron → fuera, fueras, fuera, fuéramos, fueran* — "if I were" type
   - *tuvieron → tuviera, tuvieras, tuviera, tuviéramos, tuvieran* — "if I had"
   - *hicieron → hiciera, hicieras, hiciera, hiciéramos, hicieran* — "that I did"
   - *trajeron → trajera* — "that I brought"
   - *dijeron → dijera* — "that I said"
10. Use *si fuera/fueras* for hypothetical-past "if I were / if you were." *Si yo fuera tú* = "If I were you." *Si fuera usted* = "If I were you (formal)."
11. Practical tip: if the irregular is too hard in the moment, swap to a more regular synonym. *Decir* is super irregular for "would" → use *contar* instead. *Te diría* (very irregular conditional) ↔ *Te contaría* (regular). *Si lo tuviera, te lo contaría* = "If I had it, I would tell you it."
12. Closing technique: Spanish loves to end with the object. *Si lo tuviera, te lo contaría.* Notice the *lo* tucks in nicely at the end.
13. We use *quería que* + past mood for "I wanted [someone] to do X." Example: *Quería que lo hicieran* = "I wanted them to do it." *Quería que lo hiciéramos* = "I wanted us to do it."
14. Teacher's philosophy: don't aim to install all this perfectly. Just expose yourself. Build verbs the regular way; if it sounds wrong, check with the person you're talking to. Don't be afraid of mistakes.

## Vocabulary
- *poner / ponía / puse / pusiste / puso / pusimos / pusieron* — to put (and past forms)
- *componer / exponer / oponer* — to compose / expose / oppose
- *hacer / hice / hiciste / hizo / hicimos / hicieron* — to do/make (and past forms)
- *venir / vine / vino / vinimos / vinieron* — to come
- *vino* — wine (also "he came")
- *traer / traje / trajo / trajimos / trajeron* — to bring
- *traje* — suit (clothing); also "I brought"
- *ser / fui / fue / fuimos / fuiste / fueron* — to be (point-in-past)
- *ir / fui / fue / fuimos / fuiste / fueron* — to go (point-in-past)
- *fuera / fueras / fuera / fuéramos / fueran* — past mood of *ser/ir*
- *tuviera / tuvieras / tuviéramos / tuvieran* — past mood of *tener*
- *hiciera / hicieran / hiciéramos* — past mood of *hacer*
- *trajera / dijera / pusiera / viniera* — past moods of *traer/decir/poner/venir*
- *contar / contaría / te contaría* — to tell / I would tell / I would tell you (regular alternative to *decir*)
- *quería que* — I wanted that... (followed by past mood)

## Examples / Sentences
- *¿Dónde lo pusiste?* — Where did you put it?
- *Hice / Hizo / Hicieron.* — I did / he did / they did.
- *Vino con vino.* — He came with wine.
- *Traje un traje.* — I brought a suit.
- *No fui.* — I didn't go. (or: I wasn't.)
- *No fue.* — He didn't go. (or: he wasn't.)
- *Si yo fuera tú.* / *Si fuera usted.* — If I were you (informal/formal).
- *Si lo tuviera, te lo contaría.* — If I had it, I would tell you (it).
- *Quería que lo hicieran.* — I wanted them to do it.
- *Quería que lo hiciéramos.* — I wanted us to do it.
