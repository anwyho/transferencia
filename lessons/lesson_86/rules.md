# Lesson 86

## Summary
Go-verbs are also weird in the future ("will") and conditional ("would") tenses — they all use a **contraction** of the infinitive as the base. Most replace the *-er/-ir* ending with *-dr-* (so *tener → tendré*, *poner → pondré*, *salir → saldré*, *venir → vendré*). *Hacer* and *decir* are extra-irregular: *haré*, *diré*. *Poder* drops its *e* too (*podré, podría*).

## Rules
1. Quick refresher on **will** endings (added to the full infinitive for regular verbs): *-é* (I), *-ás* (you-informal), *-á* (he/she/it/you-formal), *-emos* (we), *-án* (they/you-plural). These are the same as the very irregular present forms of *haber*.
2. Quick refresher on **would** endings: the key sound is *-ía* (remember it via *me gustaría* = "I would like"). Then add *-mos*, *-n*, *-s* as needed: *-ía, -ías, -ía, -íamos, -ían*.
3. **Go-verbs make a contraction in will/would.** They're not "te-ne-ré" (3 syllables) — they squeeze to 2 syllables. The last vowel before the ending changes/disappears, often becoming a *d*.
4. *Tener* → *tendré* (I will have), *tendría* (I would have). The final *e* of *tener* turns into a *d*.
5. *Venir* → *vendré* (I will come), *vendría* (I would come), *vendríamos* (we would come).
6. *Poner* → *pondré* (I will put), *pondría* (I would put), *pondríamos*, *pondremos*.
7. *Salir* → *saldré* (I will leave), *saldría* (I would leave), *saldrás*, *saldrías*.
8. **Will and would share the same irregular base** — once you know one, you know the other. Swap *-é* for *-ía*.
9. *Traer*, *atraer*, *distraer* DO NOT contract — they keep things normal: *traeré*, *atraeré*, *distraeré*.
10. *Hacer* and *decir* are EXTRA irregular. Don't get *hadré* or *didré* — instead:
    - *Hacer → haré* (I will do/make), *haría* (I would do/make), *haremos*, *harán*.
    - *Decir → diré* (I will say/tell), *diría* (I would say/tell), *te diré* (I will tell you).
11. Mnemonic for *haré/diré*'s irregularity: we already saw this verb being weird — past participles *hecho* (done) and *dicho* (said), and the informal command *haz* for *hacer*.
12. With future context (e.g. *más tarde* = later, *esta noche* = tonight) you can dodge the future tense and just use the present. *Te digo más tarde* = "I'll tell you later" (literally "I tell you later"). *¿Sales esta noche?* = "Are you going out tonight? / Will you go out tonight?"
13. *Poder* (to be able to) is irregular in will/would but NOT a go-verb. The *e* of *poder* falls out: *podré* (I will be able to), *podría* (I could / I would be able to). It used to be *podería*; many years of repetition wore the *e* away.
14. Subtle sound difference to listen for: *podría* (would be able to, from *poder*) vs *pondría* (would put, from *poner*). One has an *n*, one doesn't.

## Vocabulary
- *tener / tendré / tendría* — to have / I will have / I would have
- *venir / vendré / vendría / vendríamos* — to come / I will come / I would come / we would come
- *poner / pondré / pondría / pondríamos / pondremos* — to put + futures
- *salir / saldré / saldría / saldrás / saldrías* — to leave + futures
- *traer / traeré* — to bring / I will bring (no contraction)
- *atraer / atraeré* — to attract / I will attract
- *distraer / distraeré* — to distract / I will distract
- *hacer / haré / haría / haremos / harán / haríamos* — to do/make + futures
- *decir / diré / diría / te diré* — to say/tell + futures
- *poder / podré / podría / podríamos* — to be able to + futures
- *más tarde* — later
- *esta noche* — tonight
- *ocupados* — busy (plural masc.)
- *ahora* — now
- *pero* — but
- *tiempo* — time

## Examples / Sentences
- *Vendríamos más tarde, pero no tenemos tiempo.* — We would come later, but we don't have time.
- *Lo pondré aquí.* — I will put it here.
- *Lo pondría aquí.* — I would put it here.
- *Lo pondríamos aquí.* — We would put it here.
- *Lo pondremos aquí.* — We will put it here.
- *Saldría / Saldrías.* — I/you would leave (or go out).
- *¿Saldrás esta noche?* — Will you go out tonight?
- *¿Saldrías esta noche?* — Would you go out tonight?
- *¿Sales esta noche?* — Are you going out tonight? (present used for future)
- *Haré / Haremos / Harán.* — I will do / we will do / they will do.
- *Lo haríamos, pero estamos ocupados ahora.* — We would do it, but we're busy now.
- *Diría / Diré.* — I would say / I will say.
- *Te diré más tarde.* — I'll tell you later.
- *Te digo más tarde.* — I'll tell you later. (present-as-future)
- *Podría / Podríamos.* — I would be able to / we would be able to.
- *Pondría / Pondríamos.* — I would put / we would put.
