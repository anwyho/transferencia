# Lesson 20

## Summary
We use the present tense to talk about the future — either with *voy a* + verb ("I'm going to…") or with future context words like *mañana, pronto, la semana que viene*. We also drill the verb *ver* (to see) and place little words like *la, las, nos* before the verb.

## Rules
1. **Present can mean future** when there is future context (a word that signals time-to-come). Just like English: "I see her tomorrow."
2. **Future-context words**: *mañana* (tomorrow), *más tarde* (later, "more late"), *esta noche* (tonight), *pronto* (soon — like English "get here pronto").
3. **The verb *ver* (to see / to view)** is mostly regular: *ve* (he/she sees), *ven* (they/you guys), *ves* (you informal), *vemos* (we).
4. **The "I" form *veo* is slightly irregular**: it would be too short to just be /vo/, so we keep the /e/ — *v-e-o*, /beo/.
5. **Little person-words go BEFORE the changed verb**: *la veo* (I see her), not *veo la*. With the "to" form they can stick on the end (*verla*), but otherwise they sit in front.
6. **For "her" use *la***, for "him/it" use *lo*, for "us" use *nos*, for "me" use *me*, for "you" use *te*. Plural feminine "them" = *las*; plural masculine/mixed = *los*.
7. **No guessing rule**: when finding a verb form, don't guess. Reduce to the smallest piece, find that piece, then build up. The teacher says: "no guessing at all — it's the only rule."
8. **"Going to" future** with *ir a* + "to" form: *vamos a verla* = we are going to see her. The little word (*la*) sticks onto the end of the "to" form.
9. **Position flexibility**: little words like *nos* can either stick to the "to" form (*vas a vernos*) OR sit before the changed verb (*nos vas a ver*). Both are correct.
10. **Negative + question + why**: stack *no*, then put *¿…?* around it, then add *por qué*: *¿No las ves pronto?* / *¿Por qué no las ves pronto?*
11. **"Next week" = *la semana que viene*** — literally "the week that comes". *Que* here means "that/which".
12. ***Viene* (it comes) splits the *e***: *vene* would be the regular form, but the stressed *e* splits to *ie* → *viene*. (Same e-splitting we've seen before.)
13. **"Let's [verb]" uses the *we* form**: *hablamos* can mean "we speak" OR "let's speak". *Vamos* can mean "we go" OR "let's go".
14. **Building strategy: shrink the thought**: when stuck, reduce the sentence to the smallest piece you can think about, find that piece, then forget it and move on to the next. Don't try to memorize the whole sentence.
15. ***Nos* can mean "us", "ourselves", or "each other"** — all three. So *nos vemos* = "we see each other" → used as a goodbye ("see you later").
16. **Goodbye phrases**: *nos vemos* ("we see each other / see you"), often followed by *ciao*. The future feeling is implicit — we don't need a future-time word.

## Vocabulary
- *mañana* — tomorrow / morning
- *más tarde* — later (literally "more late")
- *esta noche* — tonight
- *pronto* — soon
- *ver* — to see / to view
- *veo* — I see (slightly irregular)
- *ves* — you (informal) see
- *ve* — he/she/it sees, you (formal) see
- *vemos* — we see / let's see
- *ven* — they see, you guys see
- *la* — her / it (feminine)
- *lo* — him / it (masculine/neuter)
- *las* — them (feminine)
- *los* — them (masculine/mixed)
- *me* — me
- *te* — you (informal)
- *nos* — us / ourselves / each other
- *la semana* — the week
- *la semana que viene* — next week ("the week that comes")
- *que* — that / which
- *viene* — it comes (e-split from *vene*)
- *hablar* — to speak
- *hablamos* — we speak / let's speak
- *vamos a* — we are going to / let's
- *nos vemos* — see you (we see each other)
- *ciao* — bye

## Examples / Sentences
- *La veo más tarde.* — I'm seeing her later.
- *La veo esta noche.* — I'm seeing her tonight.
- *La vemos pronto.* — We are seeing her soon.
- *Las vemos pronto.* — We're seeing them (females) soon.
- *Las ve pronto.* — He's seeing them (females) soon.
- *La ves pronto.* — You're seeing her soon.
- *Las ves pronto.* — You're seeing them (females) soon.
- *¿Las ves pronto?* — Are you seeing them soon?
- *¿No las ves pronto?* — Aren't you seeing them soon?
- *¿Por qué no las ves pronto?* — Why aren't you seeing them soon?
- *Vamos a verla.* — We're going to see her.
- *¿Vas a verlo pronto?* — Are you going to see him soon?
- *¿Vas a vernos pronto?* / *¿Nos vas a ver pronto?* — Are you going to see us soon?
- *¿Por qué no vas a vernos pronto?* — Why aren't you going to see us soon?
- *La semana que viene.* — Next week.
- *Hablamos la semana que viene.* — Let's speak next week.
- *Nos vemos.* — See you (literally "we see each other").
- *Nos vemos la semana que viene.* — See you next week.
