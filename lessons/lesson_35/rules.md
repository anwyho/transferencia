# Lesson 35

## Summary
We use *ser* with characteristic adjectives like *sincero* and *honesto*, learn how to say "I am being X" with *estoy siendo*, and meet *salir* (to leave) and a few new time words.

## Rules
1. *Sincerity* in Spanish is *sinceridad* (the *-ity → -idad* rule). From there, guess *sincere* = *sincero / sincera*.
2. To decide *ser* vs *estar*, Spanish looks at the CONCEPT of the adjective by itself, not how you happen to be using it. The concept of "sincere" is a characteristic — so *sincero* goes with *ser*. *Soy sincero / sincera* = "I am sincere".
3. *Somos sinceros / sinceras* = "we are sincere" (m / f).
4. To say "I am BEING sincere" (right now), build it the same way as any *-ing*: *estar* + *-iendo* form. *Ser* itself becomes *siendo*. So *estoy siendo sincero / sincera*.
5. *Honesto* = honest. *Estamos siendo honestos* = "we are being honest". The concept of being honest IS a characteristic, but Spanish lets you say "we are being honest" the same way English does — *estar* + *siendo* + the characteristic.
6. *Salir* = to leave / to go out. Irregular: the *I* form is *salgo*, not "salo". (Same trick as some other irregulars where the *yo* form ends in *-go*.)
7. *Pronto* = soon. Soon is NOT right now, so don't reach for the *-ing* form. *Salgo pronto* = "I'm leaving soon".
8. *Salgo mañana* = "I'm leaving tomorrow". Future context — use the plain present. NOT *estoy saliendo*.
9. *Estoy saliendo* = "I'm leaving right now (this moment)". That's the only time to use *-iendo* — for actions in progress at this very moment.
10. Resist the urge to drop *-ing* into Spanish wherever English uses it. That's negative language transfer. *I wait* and *I'm waiting* both = *espero* unless you really mean "right now".
11. *Comprar* = to buy. *Los compro* = "I buy them" or "I'm buying them".
12. "Next week" in Spanish is "the week that comes": *la semana que viene*. *Semana* = week. *Venir* = to come. *Viene* = he/she/it comes.
13. So *los compro la semana que viene* = "I'm buying them next week" — present tense, even though English uses *-ing*.
14. For "right now" only: *los estoy comprando ahora* / *estoy comprándolos ahora* = "I'm buying them right now". *Ahora* = now.

## Vocabulary
- *sinceridad* — sincerity
- *sincero / sincera* — sincere
- *honesto / honesta* — honest
- *siendo* — being
- *salir* — to leave / go out
- *salgo / saliendo* — I leave / leaving
- *pronto* — soon
- *mañana* — tomorrow
- *comprar* — to buy
- *los compro* — I buy them
- *semana* — week
- *la semana que viene* — next week (the week that comes)
- *venir* — to come
- *viene* — he/she/it comes
- *ahora* — now

## Examples / Sentences
- *Soy sincero / sincera.* — I am sincere.
- *Somos sinceras / sinceros.* — We are sincere (f / m).
- *Estoy siendo sincero / sincera.* — I am being sincere.
- *Estamos siendo honestos.* — We are being honest.
- *Salgo pronto.* — I'm leaving soon.
- *Salgo mañana.* — I'm leaving tomorrow.
- *Estoy saliendo.* — I'm leaving (right now).
- *Los compro.* — I buy them / I'm buying them.
- *Los compro mañana.* — I'm buying them tomorrow.
- *Los compro la semana que viene.* — I'm buying them next week.
- *Los estoy comprando ahora.* / *Estoy comprándolos ahora.* — I'm buying them now.
