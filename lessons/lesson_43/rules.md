# Lesson 43

## Summary
Big payoff lesson: with *habría* + past participle we build "I would have...", "should have...", "could have..." — long, fancy English sentences come out simply in Spanish, block by block.

## Rules
1. Like *poder* lost its *e* (*podría*), *haber* also contracts: *haber* + *-ía* → *habría* (would have), not "habería." It loses its last *e*.
2. *Habría* = I/he/she/it/you (formal) would have. *Habrías, habríamos, habrían* fill in the rest.
3. To say "I would have GONE / EATEN / SPOKEN," put the past participle (the *-ado/-ido* form) right after *habría*. Same structure as English.
4. Past participle rule (recap): for *-ar* verbs use *-ado*; for *-er/-ir* verbs use *-ido*. *Ir* (to go) → *ido* (gone). *Comer* → *comido* (eaten). *Hablar* → *hablado* (spoken).
5. *Habría ido* = I would have gone. *Habríamos comido* = we would have eaten.
6. Build complex sentences block by block. "We would have been able to go" = *Habríamos podido ir*. *Poder* → *podido* (been able). *Ir* stays as the "to" form because it follows a verb of motion / second verb.
7. *Habrían podido comer* = they would have been able to eat.
8. *Habrían venido a comer* = they would have come to eat. (Verb of motion + *a* + second verb.)
9. For "I would have liked it" — translate via Spanglish: "It would have pleased me" → *Me habría gustado*. The version of *gustar* after *habría* is *gustado* (past participle of *-ar*).
10. Tip: think "the version of the verb that comes AFTER *haber*," rather than chasing the literal English ("liked, gone, eaten" — they look so different in English you might miss they're all the same role).
11. *Nos habría gustado* = we would have liked it.
12. *Nos habrían gustado* = we would have liked them (literally "they would have pleased us"). Notice the verb is plural to match the things, not "us."
13. *Deber* = to owe, must (related to "debt"). It's an *-er* verb.
14. Conditional of *deber* + *-ía* = *debería* = "would owe" = SHOULD. *Debería* = I/he/she should.
15. *Deberías venir a mi casa* = You should come to my house. *Deberíamos hablar* = we should talk.
16. After *debería* (and *podría*, *querría*, etc.), the next verb is in the "to" form. English drops "to" after should/can/must, but Spanish keeps it: literal Spanish = "we should TO speak."
17. Build chain: *Deberíamos hablarlo* = We should speak about it (note: drop "about," use *lo*). *Deberíamos hablarle* = we should speak to him/her/you-formal. *Deberíamos hablarles* = we should speak to them/you guys.
18. To dodge ambiguity of *le* (him/her/you-formal), use *con*: *Deberíamos hablar con él / con ella* = we should speak with him/her — clearer.
19. *Dar* (to give) → *daría* (I would give), *darían* (they would give). *Me darían* = they would give me / to me. *Me lo darían* = they would give it to me.

## Vocabulary
- *haber* — auxiliary "have"
- *habría, habrías, habríamos, habrían* — I/you/we/they would have
- *ir* — to go; *ido* — gone
- *comer* — to eat; *comido* — eaten
- *hablar* — to speak; *hablado* — spoken
- *poder* — to be able; *podido* — been able
- *venir* — to come; *venido* — come
- *gustar* — to please; *gustado* — pleased
- *deber* — to owe, must
- *debería, deberías, deberíamos, deberían* — should (I/you/we/they)
- *con él / con ella* — with him / her
- *dar* — to give; *daría, darían* — would give

## Examples / Sentences
- *Habría.* — I/he/she would have.
- *Habría ido.* — I would have gone.
- *Habríamos comido.* — We would have eaten.
- *Habríamos.* — We would have.
- *Habríamos podido ir.* — We would have been able to go.
- *Habrían podido comer.* — They would have been able to eat.
- *Habrían venido a comer.* — They would have come to eat.
- *Me habría gustado.* — I would have liked it.
- *Nos habría gustado.* — We would have liked it.
- *Nos habrían gustado.* — We would have liked them.
- *Debería.* — I should.
- *Deberías venir a mi casa.* — You should come to my house.
- *Deberías venir a mi casa a comer.* — You should come to my house to eat.
- *Deberíamos.* — We should.
- *Deberíamos hablar.* — We should speak.
- *Deberíamos hablarlo.* — We should speak about it.
- *Deberíamos hablarle.* — We should speak to him/her/you (formal).
- *Deberíamos hablarles.* — We should speak to them/you guys.
- *Deberíamos hablar con él / con ella.* — We should speak with him / her.
- *Daría.* — I would give.
- *Darían.* — They would give.
- *Me darían.* — They would give me / to me.
- *Me lo darían.* — They would give it to me.
