# Lesson 73

## Summary
We keep practicing the mood tense (subjunctive) for orders to others, "let it/him/her" wishes, and for future events introduced by *cuando*.

## Rules
1. **Positive informal "tú" command shortcut**: take the *tú* present form and chop off the final *s*. *Esperas* (you wait) → *Espera* (wait!).
2. **"Let's" with -ar verbs**: use the *nosotros* mood-tense form ending in *-emos*. *Esperar* → *Esperemos* (let's wait).
3. *Esperemos* can also sound like a softer "shall we wait?" depending on the tone — *¿Esperemos?* feels less like a hard order.
4. **Negative "let's not"**: just put *no* in front. *No esperemos* (let's not wait).
5. **"Let him/her/them do X" (third-person commands)**: use *que* + the verb in the mood tense. *Que espere* = let him wait. *Que termine* = let it finish. *Que se calle* = let him shut up.
6. You can stack on extras like *ya* (already): *Que termine ya* = let it finish already / hurry up and finish.
7. *Callarse* = to quiet oneself / to shut up. *Que se calle* = let him shut himself up.
8. **Cuando + general truth → normal present**. *Cuando hablamos* = when we (generally) speak.
9. **Cuando + future event → mood tense**. Spanish is "superstitious" about treating an unhappened future as a fact, so it flips into the mood tense to mark that uncertainty. *Cuando hablemos* = when we (will) speak (later).
10. *Junta* = together (think of "military junta", or "junction/joint"). It must agree: *juntos* (mixed/male), *juntas* (all female).
11. **Future after *cuando* uses present, not future tense**, because *cuando* already gives the future flavor. *Cuando comamos juntos, hablamos* = when we eat together (future), we'll speak.
12. **Mood tense of *ir*** is irregular: root is *vaya* (v-a-y-a). Add the usual endings: *vaya, vayas, vaya, vayamos, vayan*.
13. With *cuando* + future *ir*: *La vemos cuando vayamos* = we'll see her when we go (future). General version stays normal: *La vemos cuando vamos*.

## Vocabulary
- *esperar* — to wait / to hope
- *espera* — wait! (informal command)
- *esperemos* — let's wait / shall we wait
- *callarse* — to be quiet / to shut up
- *que se calle* — let him shut up
- *terminar* — to finish
- *que termine* — let it finish
- *ya* — already
- *cuando* — when
- *junta / juntos / juntas* — together (also: a junction; military junta)
- *hablamos* — we speak / we'll speak
- *hablemos* — (mood) we speak
- *ir* — to go
- *voy / va / vamos* — I go / he goes / we go
- *vaya / vayas / vayamos / vayan* — mood-tense forms of *ir*
- *vemos* — we see
- *la vemos* — we see her

## Examples / Sentences
- *Espera.* — Wait. (informal)
- *Esperemos.* — Let's wait.
- *No esperemos.* — Let's not wait.
- *Que espere.* — Let him wait.
- *Que termine ya.* — Let it finish already.
- *Que se calle.* — Let him shut up.
- *Cuando hablamos.* — When we speak (generally).
- *Cuando hablemos.* — When we speak (in the future).
- *Cuando comamos juntos, hablamos.* — When we eat together, we'll speak.
- *La vemos cuando vamos.* — We see her when we go (generally).
- *La vemos cuando vayamos.* — We will see her when we go.

## Cross-references
- Builds on the earlier rule that positive informal commands = chop the *s* off the *tú* form.
- Builds on prior structures *quiero que*, *prefiero que* + mood tense.
- Recalls *callarse* (to shut up / quiet oneself) from earlier reflexive lessons.
