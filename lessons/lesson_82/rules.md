# Lesson 82

## Summary
We keep using the mood tense (subjunctive) after structures like *es relevante que* / *es importante que* / *es increíble que*, but we also learn handy tricks to AVOID the mood tense when we don't want to deal with it. The big trick: turn "the X thing" into *lo + adjective + es que*, which sidesteps the mood.

## Rules
1. After *ser/estar* + an adjective + *que* (like *es relevante que*, *está bien que*, *es importante que*), we use the mood tense (subjunctive) for the verb that follows. Example: *No es muy relevante que lo encontraran.* (It's not very relevant that they found it.)
2. We can use the **past mood tense** (encontraran) when we're talking about the past, and the **present mood tense** (encuentren) when we're talking about now/the future. Same structure, just match the time.
3. *Ser* in the line-in-the-past (was/used to be) is irregular. Mnemonic: think of the English word "era" — like "a line in the past is an *era*" — pronounce it Spanish-style and you get *era* (he/she/it was, I was). So *era muy relevante* = it was very relevant.
4. Sneaky trick to AVOID the mood tense: rephrase. Instead of *es importante que* (the important thing IS that...), say *lo importante es que* (the important thing is that...). After *es que* you don't need the mood tense — you're just stating a fact.
5. To make "the X thing" use *lo + adjective*. Don't say *la cosa importante* — just say *lo importante*. Works with any adjective: *lo importante* (the important thing), *lo increíble* (the incredible thing), *lo espectacular* (the spectacular thing).
6. *Increíble* literally means "unbelievable" — it comes from *creer* (to believe) → *creíble* (believable) → *increíble* (un-believable). No "d" sound like English "incredible."
7. If you say *es increíble que...* you DO need the mood tense. If you reword to *lo increíble es que...* you DON'T need it. Compare: *Es increíble que vuelvan.* vs *Lo increíble es que vuelven.*
8. *Volver a + infinitive* = "to do something again." So *vuelven a hacerlo* = "they do it again," *vuelvan a hacerlo* = mood-tense version (that they do it again).
9. To play the same trick we used earlier with *volví a llamarte para organizar* (instead of *para que organizáramos*), we can rework sentences to dodge the mood tense whenever it's getting in the way.

## Vocabulary
- *relevante* — relevant
- *encontrar* — to find
- *encontraron* — they found (point in the past)
- *encontraran* — that they found (past mood)
- *encuentren* — that they find (present mood)
- *era* — was/used to be (line-in-past from *ser*)
- *importante* — important
- *lo importante* — the important thing
- *increíble* — incredible / unbelievable
- *creer* — to believe
- *creíble* — believable
- *espectacular* — spectacular
- *volver* — to return / to do again (with *a*)
- *vuelven* — they return / they do (it) again
- *vuelvan* — that they return (mood)
- *hacer* — to do / to make
- *hacerlo* — to do it
- *volvieron* — they returned (point in past)
- *volvieran* — that they returned (past mood)

## Examples / Sentences
- *No es muy relevante.* — It's not very relevant.
- *No es muy relevante que lo encontraran.* — It's not very relevant that they found it.
- *No es muy relevante que lo encuentren.* — It's not very relevant that they (might) find it.
- *Era muy relevante.* — It was very relevant.
- *Era muy relevante que lo encontraran.* — It was very relevant that they found it.
- *Es increíble que vuelvan.* — It's incredible that they come back.
- *Lo increíble es que vuelven.* — The incredible thing is that they come back.
- *Lo increíble es que vuelven a hacerlo.* — The incredible thing is that they do it again.
- *Es increíble que vuelvan a hacerlo.* — It's incredible that they do it again.
- *Es increíble que volvieran a hacerlo.* — It's incredible that they did it again.
