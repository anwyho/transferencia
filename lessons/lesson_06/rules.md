# Lesson 06

## Summary
We meet *intento* ("I try / I'm trying"), learn that the *-o* ending = "I", and that Spanish *h* is silent. We also meet *voy* ("I go / I'm going") and discover that Spanish *v* sounds like a soft *b*.

## Rules
1. ***Intento* = "I try" / "I am trying."*** Same form covers both — Spanish doesn't split "try" vs "am trying."
2. **The `-o` at the end of a verb means "I."*** *Quier-o* = I want. *Intent-o* = I try.
3. **Even when there's no -ation rule, you can still hear and understand.*** From *intención* you can guess *intento* means something like "I try."
4. **Spanish *h* is completely silent.** Don't even *think* the H — it'll slip out. Imagine the word starts with the next vowel. *hospital* = /os-pi-TAL/, *horrible* = /o-RRI-ble/, *habitual* = /a-bi-TUAL/.
5. **Exception: *ch* is pronounced like English /ch/** (in "church"). That's the only time *h* makes a sound.
6. **Double *rr* inside a word rolls.** *horrible*. If you can't roll, just say a normal r and you'll be understood — no worries.
7. ***Ahora* = "now."** Spelled a-h-o-r-a, pronounced /a-O-ra/ (h is silent). Comes from *a* (to) + *hora* (hour) — literally "to the hour."
8. **So *a* = "to" (direction) and *hora* = "hour."** Both useful words for free.
9. ***Voy* = "I go" / "I am going."*** Spelled v-o-y. Memory hook: *voyage* — going somewhere.
10. **Spanish *v* sounds like a soft *b* (/b/ with no burst of air).** In most of the Spanish-speaking world, *v* and *b* sound the same — soft. In Argentina and Chile (the southern cone) you hear more of an English /v/. Use whichever you like; you'll pick up the local accent.
11. **A "plosive" *b* (English-style, pops air on a piece of paper) is hard. The Spanish soft *b* is gentler — no air burst.** Don't stress about it.
12. ***Vamos* = "let's go" (literally "we are going").*** That *v* is usually heard as soft b: /BA-mos/.
13. **Don't put the English /z/ in *visible*.*** Pronounce a clean /s/: /vi-SI-ble/, not /vi-ZI-ble/.
14. **Etymology hook for memory:** *visitation* exists in English (visitation rights), so *visit → visitar* makes sense.

## Examples / Sentences
- *Intento publicarlo.* — I'm trying to publish it.
- *Intento administrarlo.* — I'm trying to administrate it.
- *Intento imitarlo.* — I'm trying to imitate him / it.
- *Voy.* — I go / I'm going.
- *Voy ahora.* — I'm going now.
- *No voy ahora.* — I'm not going now.
- *Quiero visitarlo.* — I want to visit him.
- *Quiero cancelarlo, pero no quiero cancelarlo ahora.* — I want to cancel it, but I don't want to cancel it now.
