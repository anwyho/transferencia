# Lesson 47

## Summary
We keep practicing the *-aba* line-past for *-ar* verbs, and learn how Spanish uses *se* ("itself") to say things like "the dinner was burning" or "it breaks all the time" — the trick for the missing English word "get".

## Rules
1. *-ar* verbs in the line-past: chop *-ar*, add *-aba* (I/he/she/it/you-formal). Add *-s* for you-informal, *-n* for they/you-guys, *-amos* (with accent: *-ábamos*) for we.
2. *Cenaba* (one form) covers "I was having dinner / he was / she was / you-formal were / I used to dine" etc.
3. To make a verb out of a noun, often just attach the *-ar/-er/-ir* ending. *La cena* (dinner) → *cenar* (to dine).
4. In Spain, *c* before *e/i* sounds like /th/. So *cena* sounds like /thena/.
5. If a verb's action isn't done TO something else (it just happens), use *se* + the verb to mean "itself". This is how Spanish covers English "get" (get burnt, get broken, get changed).
6. Word order is flexible because of *se*: *La cena se quemaba* = *Se quemaba la cena* — both mean "the dinner was burning (itself)". Word order matters less in Spanish than English because Spanish has more grammar tools.
7. Without *se*, the sentence feels incomplete — Spanish is waiting to hear WHAT got burnt. *Quemaba la cena* = he was burning the dinner. *La cena se quemaba* = the dinner was burning.
8. To say "I get burnt in the sun": *me quemo en el sol* (literally "I burn myself in the sun"). Don't worry — you're not literally burning yourself; *se/me* just means it happened to you.
9. You can also flip it: *El sol me quema* — "The sun burns me" — no *se* needed because now the sentence is complete (sun burns + me).
10. *Romper* = to break. *Lo rompo* = I break it. *Se rompe* = it breaks (gets broken). *Se rompen todo el tiempo* = they break all the time.
11. *Todo el tiempo* = "all the time" (literally "all the time"). *Todo* = all.
12. When you add *se* to a verb, FIRST be clear what type of verb it is — go back to the "to" form so you don't mess up the ending.
13. *Este* = this (masculine), *esta* = this (feminine). The *e* in *este* echoes the *e* of *el*; the *a* in *esta* echoes the *a* of *la*.
14. Plural: *estos* = these (masc), *estas* = these (fem). *Estos* echoes the *-os* of *los*. (Not *estes* — this is a special pattern.)
15. To say "that/those" instead of "this/these", just pluck out the *t*: *este* → *ese* (that), *estos* → *esos* (those).
16. To say "those ones" without naming the thing, just use *esos / esas*. Default to *esos* for masculine or mixed groups.
17. Verbs that show their action isn't on something else need *se*: *se transforma* (it transforms), *se rompe* (it breaks). But *cambia* (it changes, from *cambiar*) doesn't need it. You'll build intuition with practice.

## Vocabulary
- *la cena* — the dinner
- *cenar* — to dine, to have dinner
- *quemar* — to burn
- *se* — itself / oneself (reflexive marker)
- *el sol* — the sun (masculine)
- *en* — in
- *romper* — to break (related to "rupture")
- *todo el tiempo* — all the time
- *todo* — all
- *el tiempo* — the time
- *este / esta / estos / estas* — this / these (m/f singular/plural)
- *ese / esa / esos / esas* — that / those (m/f singular/plural)
- *el auto / el coche* — the car
- *la noche* — the night
- *transformar(se)* — to transform
- *cambiar* — to change

## Examples / Sentences
- *La cena* — the dinner
- *Cenaba* — I was having dinner / used to dine
- *Quemaba la cena* — he was burning the dinner
- *Él quemaba la cena* — HE was burning the dinner (with emphasis)
- *Usted quemaba la cena* — You (formal) were burning the dinner
- *La cena se quemaba* / *Se quemaba la cena* — the dinner was burning
- *Me quemo en el sol* — I burn (myself) in the sun
- *El sol me quema* — the sun burns me
- *Lo rompo* — I break it
- *Se rompe todo el tiempo* — it breaks all the time
- *Se rompen todo el tiempo* — they break all the time
- *Esta noche* — this night/tonight
- *Este auto / Este coche* — this car
- *Estos autos* — these cars
- *Ese auto* — that car
- *Esos autos* — those cars
- *Esos se rompen todo el tiempo* — those ones break all the time
