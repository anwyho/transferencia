# Lesson 63

## Summary
We learn *acordarse* (to remember), see how the *o* splits into *ue* when stressed, and we keep clarifying who-likes-what with *gustar*. The mid-translation (*it pleases him*) saves us from messing it up.

## Rules
1. *Acordarse* = to remember. The *-se* on the end means we always need a pronoun: *me acuerdo* = "I remember (myself)".
2. The *o* in *acordar* splits into *ue* when it's STRESSED. So *me acuerdo* (stressed o → *ue*) but *nos acordamos* (stress is elsewhere → *o* stays).
3. So:
   - I remember = *me acuerdo*
   - we remember = *nos acordamos*
   - I don't remember = *no me acuerdo*
   - we don't remember = *no nos acordamos*
4. Keep the *se* visible — it changes to match: *me*, *te*, *se*, *nos*, *se*. Don't drop it.
5. To say "I gave it to him" we need the L-L fix: *le lo di* → *se lo di*. The *se* here is the phonetic fix, NOT the reflexive *se*.
6. To clarify "him or her" or "them", add *a él*, *a ella*, *a ellos*, *a ellas* at the end — always repeat the little word in this case.
7. *Gustar* mind-flip drill: ALWAYS take the mid road "it pleases him / her / you". Don't translate directly to English "he likes". The mid-step prevents mistakes.
8. *Le gusta* = "it is pleasing to him / her / you-formal / it". Could mean any of those — context or clarification needed.
9. Clarify *le gusta* with *a*: *Le gusta a ella* = "She likes it" (literally "it is pleasing to her"). *A ella le gusta* (same, with the clarification at front).
10. Without the *a*, saying *ella le gusta* would mean "she is pleasing to" someone (whoever *le* points to). The *a* is essential to clarify direction.
11. Past form of *gustar*: *le gustó* = "it pleased him / her / you (formal) / it". Same ambiguity as *le gusta*. Clarify with *a ella*: *Le gustó a ella* = "She liked it".
12. With these "indirect" structures, when the *le* group (he, she, it, you-formal) all sound alike, clarification with *a + person* is very common.

## Vocabulary
- *acordarse* — to remember
- *me acuerdo / no me acuerdo* — I remember / I don't remember
- *nos acordamos / no nos acordamos* — we remember / we don't remember
- *si* — if
- *o* — or
- *dar / di / dio / dimos* — to give / I gave / he gave / we gave
- *se lo di / se lo dimos* — I/we gave it to him/her/them
- *a él / a ella / a ellos / a ellas / a usted* — to him/her/them/you-formal
- *gustar* — to please (= "like" mind-flipped)
- *le gusta / le gustó* — it is pleasing / pleased to him/her/you-formal
- *le gusta a él / a ella / a usted* — he/she/you-formal likes it (clarified)
- *le gustó a ella* — she liked it (it pleased to her)

## Examples / Sentences
- *Me acuerdo.* — I remember.
- *No me acuerdo.* — I don't remember.
- *No me acuerdo si se lo di a él o a ella.* — I don't remember if I gave it to him or her.
- *No nos acordamos.* — We don't remember.
- *No nos acordamos si se lo dimos a él o a ellas.* — We don't remember if we gave it to him or to them (fem).
- *Le gusta.* — He / she / you-formal / it likes it (lit. it is pleasing to him etc.).
- *Le gusta a ella. / A ella le gusta.* — She likes it.
- *Le gustó.* — He / she / you-formal liked it.
- *Le gustó a ella.* — She liked it.
