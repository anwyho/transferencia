# Lesson 66

## Summary
Spanish doesn't lean on "my, your, his" as much as English does — for things like body parts and pain, it uses *me/te/le* instead. We also see how to say "X's house" and the words *solo* (only) and *nadie más* (nobody else).

## Rules
1. Spanish uses possessive words (*my, your, his*) much less than English. Watch for places where English wants "my" but Spanish wants *me/te/le*.
2. With body parts, Spanish doesn't say "my leg hurts" — it says "it hurts me the leg": *Me duele la pierna.*
3. *Doler* = to hurt. The verb is "o-splits": *duele*. So *me duele* = it hurts me.
4. To say "doesn't hurt anymore," just use *más* (more) for "anymore": *No me duele la pierna más* = my leg doesn't hurt anymore.
5. The noun "pain" is *dolor*. *Dolor de cabeza* = headache (literally "pain of head").
6. "I have a headache" = *Tengo dolor de cabeza.* Past-line: *Tenía dolor de cabeza.* You can also say *me duele la cabeza* / *me dolía la cabeza*.
7. To say "X's house" in Spanish, you say "the house of X": *la casa de José* = José's house. *La fiesta de María* = Maria's party.
8. *Solo* = only / just. *Hablé contigo solo* or *solo hablé contigo* = I only spoke with you. Putting *solo* later in the sentence sounds more emphatic.
9. With double negatives, just keep stacking: *No hablé con nadie más en la fiesta de María, solo hablé contigo.* = I didn't speak with anybody else at Maria's party, I only spoke with you.

## Vocabulary
- *doler* — to hurt
- *duele* — it hurts (o splits to ue)
- *me duele* — it hurts me
- *pierna* — leg (feminine)
- *más* — more / anymore (with negation)
- *dolor* — pain (noun)
- *cabeza* — head
- *dolor de cabeza* — headache
- *tener* — to have
- *tengo / tenía* — I have / I had (was having)
- *casa* — house
- *fiesta* — party
- *nadie* — nobody
- *nadie más* — nobody else
- *solo* — only / just
- *contigo* — with you (informal)

## Examples / Sentences
- *Me duele.* — It hurts me.
- *Me duele la pierna.* — My leg hurts.
- *No me duele la pierna más.* — My leg doesn't hurt anymore.
- *Tengo dolor de cabeza.* — I have a headache.
- *Tenía dolor de cabeza.* — I had / I was having a headache.
- *Me duele la cabeza / me dolía la cabeza.* — Same idea, line in past.
- *La casa de José.* — José's house.
- *Vamos a la casa de José.* — We're going to José's house.
- *Vamos a la fiesta de María.* — We're going to Maria's party.
- *No hablé con nadie más en la fiesta de María, solo hablé contigo.* — I didn't speak with anybody else at Maria's party, I only spoke with you.
- *Hablé contigo solo / solo hablé contigo.* — I only spoke with you.
