# Lesson 44

## Summary
Gender exceptions! Some words ending in *-a* are masculine (Greek loan words: *el problema, el sistema*), and a few words ending in *-o* are feminine (*la mano, la foto, la moto*). We also meet "this/that/these/those" (*este, ese, esta, esa, estos, esos*).

## Rules
1. Most words ending in *-o* are masculine and most ending in *-a* are feminine — but exceptions exist.
2. Words ending in *-ma* often come from Greek through Spanish. In Greek they were neuter but looked masculine, so they entered Spanish as masculine words even though they end in *-a*: *el problema, el sistema, el paradigma, el esquema, el diagrama, el planeta*.
3. *Esquema* = outline/diagram. Reminder: Spanish can't start a word with *s* + another consonant — so it adds *e-* up front: *España* (Spain), *espagueti* (spaghetti), *esquema* (scheme).
4. *Planeta* (planet) is masculine even though it ends in *-a*: *el planeta, un planeta, los planetas*.
5. *El auto* = the car. *Los autos* = the cars.
6. *Este* / *esta* = this. *Este auto* = this car (masc). *Esta noche* = this night / tonight (fem).
7. The trick: if you take the *t* out of *este/esta*, you get *ese/esa* = "that." So *este auto* (this car) → *ese auto* (that car). *Esta noche* → *esa noche* (that night).
8. Plurals: *estos, estas* (these), *esos, esas* (those). *Esas noches* = those nights.
9. A few words ending in *-o* are feminine. The most common is *la mano* (hand): *la mano, las manos, esta mano, esa mano, estas manos*.
10. *Mano* connects to English "manuscript, manual, maintain, manage" — *man-* is "hand." *Manejar* (to manage / to handle) historically meant handling horses; in some Latin American countries it now means "to drive."
11. *Foto* and *moto* are also feminine — but only because they're SHORT for *la fotografía* and *la motocicleta* (both feminine).
12. *Tomar* = to take. *Estoy tomando una foto* = I'm taking a photo (right now). *Tomaría una foto* = I would take a photo.
13. *Sacar* is also used for "take a photo": *Estoy sacando una foto*.
14. *Manejar* example: *Manejo el problema* = I manage the problem. *Estoy manejando el problema* = I'm managing the problem (right now).
15. Spanish gerund recap: for *-ar* verbs, drop *-ar*, add *-ando*. For *-er/-ir* verbs, drop *-er/-ir*, add *-iendo*. (*Manejar* → *manejando*; *comer* → *comiendo*; *ir* → *iendo*.)
16. "Give me a hand" works in Spanish too (literal idiom): *¿Me puedes dar una mano?* = Can you give me a hand? (Note: *una* mano, since *mano* is feminine!)
17. Stacking conditionals: *¿Me podrías dar una mano?* = Could you give me a hand? *Me deberías dar una mano* = You should give me a hand.
18. Plural builds: *Deberían darme una mano* = They should give me a hand. *Nos deberían dar una mano* = They should give us a hand.
19. *Pero* = but. *Mi cámara* = my camera. *No tengo mi cámara* = I don't have my camera.
20. *Me gustaría tomar una foto, pero no tengo mi cámara* = I would like to take a photo, but I don't have my camera.

- *grande* — big
- *muy* — very
- *porque* — because
- *la mano, las manos* — the hand(s) (fem ending in -o)

- *la moto* (← *motocicleta*) — the motorbike

- *tomar* — to take
- *sacar* — to take (out / a photo)

- *pero* — but

## Examples / Sentences
- *El problema, el sistema, el paradigma, el esquema, el diagrama, el planeta.* — All masc despite ending in -a.
- *Un planeta. Los planetas.* — A planet. The planets.
- *El auto. Los autos.* — The car. The cars.
- *Este auto.* — This car.
- *Esta noche.* — This night / tonight.
- *Ese auto.* — That car.
- *Esa noche.* — That night.
- *Esas noches.* — Those nights.
- *Estas manos.* — These hands.
- *Quiero ese auto.* — I want that car.
- *No quiero ese auto, porque es muy grande.* — I don't want that car because it's very big.
- *La mano, las manos.* — The hand, the hands.
- *Esta mano. Esa mano.* — This hand. That hand.
- *¿Me puedes dar una mano?* — Can you give me a hand?
- *¿Me podrías dar una mano?* — Could you give me a hand?
- *Me deberías dar una mano.* / *Deberías darme una mano.* — You should give me a hand.
- *Deberían darme una mano.* — They should give me a hand.
- *Nos deberían dar una mano.* — They should give us a hand.
- *Manejo el problema.* — I manage the problem.
- *Estoy manejando el problema.* — I'm managing the problem.
- *Estoy tomando una foto.* — I'm taking a photo.
- *Tomaría una foto.* — I would take a photo.
- *Me gustaría tomar una foto.* — I would like to take a photo.
- *Pero no tengo mi cámara.* — But I don't have my camera.
- *Me gustaría tomar una foto, pero no tengo mi cámara.* — I'd like to take a photo, but I don't have my camera.
