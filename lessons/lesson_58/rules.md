# Lesson 58

## Summary
We meet the "point in the past" — the one-shot, finished past (like English "I spoke", "I bought"). For *-ar* verbs the endings are *-é* (I) and *-ó* (he/she/it/you-formal), and the accent really matters: *hablé* vs *habló*.

## Rules
1. There's ONE more verb time to learn: the "point in the past" — used for finished, one-shot actions. English shows it with *-ed* (walked, wanted) or irregulars (ran, ate). It's messier than other tenses but has logic.
2. Hooks to remember: *hablar* for *-ar* verbs, *comer* for *-er/-ir* verbs.
3. Point-in-the-past for *-ar* verbs:
   - I = *-é* (accent on the e). *Hablé* = I spoke.
   - he/she/it/you-formal = *-ó* (accent on the o). *Habló* = he/she/you-formal spoke.
4. The accent is critical. Same letters, different meaning. *HÁ-blo* (present, "I speak") vs *ha-BLÓ* (past, "he spoke"). Stress wrong = wrong meaning.
5. Common slip: English-speakers want to say *habló* for "I spoke" because /o/ feels like "I". WRONG — for "I" it's /é/ in this past. *No hablé* = "I didn't speak". *No habló* = "he/she/you-formal didn't speak".
6. Sanity-check trick for unfamiliar verbs: try the ending that sounds Spanish. If "comprir" feels off, try "comprer", "comprar" — the one you've heard before will jump out.
7. Building "I bought it for me": go literally — *Me lo compró* (he bought it for me, no need for "for"). Add *para mí* only to emphasize: *Lo compró para mí*.
8. *Enviar* = to send. Mnemonic: English "envoy" comes from this; possibly "envelope" too. Curious about word histories? etymonline.com is the teacher's recommendation.
9. *Te lo envié* = "I sent it to you" (informal). Stack the little words: indirect (te) + direct (lo) + verb.
10. *Todavía* = "still / yet". Cool breakdown: *toda* (all, feminine) + *vía* (way) = "all the way". Native speakers don't hear that — but breaking long words into pieces helps you internalize them.
11. The "it sent itself" trick: where English says "it was sent", Spanish says *se envió* (it sent itself). Don't add *lo* — you don't need it because *envió* already means "it sent". *Se envió ayer* = "It was sent yesterday".
12. The *I* / *me* trap from English. Resist saying *me* when you mean *I*. After *que* (than), use *yo* not *mí*: *Comió más que yo* = "He ate more than I (ate)".
13. *Ayer* = yesterday.
14. *Olvidar* = to forget. The noun *el olvido* ("the forgetting") doesn't exist in English; common in Spanish songs/tangos.
15. To say "I was going to do it but I forgot" you mix the line and the dot in the past: *Lo iba a hacer pero lo olvidé* — line in the past for "was going" (*iba*), point in the past for "forgot" (*olvidé*).
16. *Iba* (was going) is one of the three irregulars in the line-in-the-past.

## Vocabulary
- *hablar* — to speak
- *hablé* — I spoke
- *habló* — he/she/it/you-formal spoke
- *no hablé / no habló* — I didn't / he didn't speak
- *comprar* — to buy
- *compré* — I bought
- *compró* — he/she/it/you-formal bought
- *enviar* — to send (related to envoy)
- *envié* — I sent
- *envió* — he/she/it/you-formal sent
- *te* — you (object, informal)
- *me* — me
- *lo* — it
- *se* — itself / oneself / etc.
- *para* — for
- *para mí* — for me (emphasis)
- *todavía* — still / yet (= "all the way")
- *toda* — all (feminine)
- *vía* — way
- *ayer* — yesterday
- *conmigo* — with me
- *por qué* — why
- *no sé* — I don't know
- *olvidar* — to forget
- *olvidé* — I forgot
- *olvidó* — he/she/it/you-formal forgot
- *el olvido* — the forgetting (no English equivalent)
- *iba* — I/he/she was going (irregular line-past of *ir*)
- *hacer* — to do
- *pero* — but

## Examples / Sentences
- *Hablé.* — I spoke.
- *Habló.* — He / she / you-formal spoke.
- *No hablé.* — I didn't speak.
- *No habló.* — He didn't speak.
- *Compré.* — I bought.
- *Lo compré.* — I bought it.
- *No lo compré.* — I didn't buy it.
- *Lo compró.* — He bought it.
- *Me lo compró.* — He bought it for me.
- *Lo compró para mí.* — He bought it for me (emphasized).
- *Envié. / Lo envié. / Te lo envié.* — I sent / sent it / sent it to you.
- *No te lo envié.* — I didn't send it to you.
- *No te lo envié todavía.* — I didn't send it to you yet.
- *Envió. / Lo envió ayer.* — He sent. / He sent it yesterday.
- *Se envió. / Se envió ayer.* — It was sent. / It was sent yesterday.
- *Habló conmigo.* — He spoke with me.
- *No sé por qué no habló conmigo.* — I don't know why he didn't speak with me.
- *Olvidé. / Olvidó.* — I forgot. / He forgot.
- *Lo iba a hacer pero lo olvidé.* — I was going to do it but I forgot.
