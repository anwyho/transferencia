# Lesson 02

## Summary
English is half-Latin, and Spanish is basically modern Latin — so we already know thousands of Spanish words hiding in English. This lesson teaches the first conversion rule (English `-al` words) and how to pronounce Spanish vowels purely.

## Rules
1. **Spanish is "modern Latin."** Whoever called Latin a dead language lied — it became Spanish. About 3,000 English words can be flipped into Spanish with simple rules.
2. **First conversion rule: words ending in `-al` are usually the same in Spanish.** *normal → normal, legal → legal, natural → natural.*
3. **Move the stress to the end.** English stresses the start (NOR-mal); Spanish stresses the end (nor-MAL). The accent (where the stress lives) is part of the word — listen for it.
4. **Spanish has only 5 pure vowel sounds, always the same:**
   - a = /ah/ (like "apple" but pure)
   - e = /eh/ (like "elephant")
   - i = /ee/ (like "ink" → /ee/)
   - o = /oh/
   - u = /oo/
   English vowels mush around; Spanish vowels never do.
5. **Spanish is phonetic.** Letters always make the same sound. So always ask "how is it written?" then say each letter.
6. **The Spanish *s* is a real /s/, not /z/.** English speakers slip a /z/ into "is" — don't. *Es* = /es/, never /ez/.
7. **The word *es* means "is / he is / she is / it is / you are (formal)."** One little word, four meanings. *Es normal* = "It's normal."
8. **You don't need to say "he/she/it"** if context makes it clear — *es* alone covers it.
9. **The word for "no/not/don't" is *no*** — same spelling as English, but pronounced /noh/. Stick it before *es* to negate: *No es normal.*
10. **Cut English's hidden /y/ sound.** English "ann-yu-al" has a /y/ Spanish doesn't have — write it: *anual.* Pronounce only what's written.
11. **Vocabulary trick: relate words to their English cousin.** *Diente* (tooth) → think "dental." Don't memorize randomly; hook it to a Latin English word.
12. **With vocabulary you can guess freely.** Stick on -o, -e, or -a if there's no rule (verb → *verbo*). With sentence structure, take your time. With words, experiment.
