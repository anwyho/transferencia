# Lesson 21

## Summary
We learn the verb *haber* ("to have" only in the sense of "I have eaten / I have gone") and how to build the past with it: *he/has/ha/hemos/han* + verb-with-*-ado* ending. This is *Spanish*'s favorite past tense.

## Rules
1. **Two "haves" in Spanish**:
   - *tener* = to possess (I have a car). Add *que* → *tengo que* = "have to".
   - *haber* = to have in the sense of "I have eaten / I have gone". Used to make past tense.
2. ***Haber* is super irregular** but easy to remember: just look at its two vowels, /a/ and /e/, and you'll find every form.
3. **Silent *h***: *haber* starts with a silent *h*, so *he* sounds like /e/, *ha* like /a/, etc.
4. **The /e/ side (I and we)**:
   - *he* = I have (just like *sé* from *saber* — only two verbs in Spanish make the I-form this way).
   - *hemos* = we have. (I and we are connected: we = plural of I.)
5. **The /a/ side (everyone else)**:
   - *ha* = he/she/it has, you (formal) have.
   - *han* = they have, you guys have. (Add *-n* for plural.)
   - *has* = you (informal) have. (Add *-s* for informal you.)
6. **Full *haber* set**: *he, has, ha, hemos, han*.
7. **Verbs use *-s* for plural? NO**: nouns and adjectives use *-s* for plural. Verbs use *-mos* (*-amos, -emos, -imos*) for "we". Don't mix them up.
8. **Building "eaten/spoken/gone" (past participle)**: take an *-ar* verb, drop *-ar*, add *-ado*.
   - *hablar* → *hablado* (spoken)
   - *anticipar* → *anticipado* (anticipated)
   - *celebrar* → *celebrado* (celebrated)
   - *tomar* → *tomado* (taken)
9. **"I have spoken" = *he hablado***: think of the two pieces separately — first *he* (I have), then *hablado* (spoken).
10. **Negation goes BEFORE the *haber* part**: *no he hablado* = I haven't spoken.
11. **Little words (*lo, la, me, te, nos*) go BEFORE *haber***, not between *haber* and the *-ado* verb. *Lo hemos anticipado* — we've anticipated it. NOT *hemos lo anticipado*. (The *haber*+participle pair is never split.)
12. **This past is more useful than English's**: Spanish uses "have-past" (he hablado) much more often than English does. When in doubt, use it.
13. **"For (a period of time)" = *por***. Like in *por favor* ("for a favor" = please). *Por mucho tiempo* = for a long time.
14. ***Tiempo* (time) is the e-split version of Italian *tempo*** (which English borrowed for music). Same splitting pattern.
15. **Building a question past**: *¿Has celebrado?* — Have you celebrated? *¿No has celebrado?* — Haven't you? *¿Por qué no has celebrado?* — Why haven't you?

## Vocabulary
- *haber* — to have (auxiliary, for "I have eaten")
- *he* — I have
- *has* — you (informal) have
- *ha* — he/she/it has, you (formal) have
- *hemos* — we have
- *han* — they have, you guys have
- *saber* — to know
- *sé* — I know
- *hablar* — to speak
- *hablado* — spoken
- *anticipar* — to anticipate
- *anticipado* — anticipated
- *celebrar* — to celebrate
- *celebrado* — celebrated
- *tomar* — to take / drink
- *tomado* — taken
- *por* — for (period of time) / per
- *por favor* — please ("for a favor")
- *mucho* — a lot / much
- *tiempo* — time (e-split from *tempo*)
- *dónde* — where
- *anticipación* — anticipation
- *celebración* — celebration

## Examples / Sentences
- *He hablado.* — I have spoken.
- *No he hablado.* — I haven't spoken.
- *Hemos hablado.* — We have spoken.
- *Ha hablado.* — He/she has spoken.
- *Han hablado.* — They have spoken.
- *Has hablado.* — You have spoken.
- *Lo hemos anticipado.* — We have anticipated it.
- *Lo hemos anticipado por mucho tiempo.* — We have anticipated it for a long time.
- *¿Has celebrado?* — Have you celebrated?
- *¿No has celebrado?* — Haven't you celebrated?
- *¿Por qué no has celebrado?* — Why haven't you celebrated?
- *¿Dónde has celebrado?* — Where have you celebrated?
- *¿Dónde lo has celebrado?* — Where have you celebrated it?
- *He tomado.* — I have taken.
- *Hemos tomado.* — We have taken.
- *¿Lo ha tomado?* — Has she taken it?
