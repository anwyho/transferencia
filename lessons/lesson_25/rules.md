# Lesson 25

## Summary
We see that *me, te, nos* cover not just "me / you / us" but also "to me, for me, from me" — Spanish doesn't usually need extra "to" or "for" words. We drill *cocinar* (to cook), times o'clock (*a las seis, a las ocho*), and meet *quedar* (to stay/remain) with the reflexive *me quedo*.

## Rules
1. **Don't translate "to" or "for" in front of *me, te, nos***: Spanish *me* by itself can mean "me", "to me", OR "for me". Same with *te* (you, to you, for you) and *nos* (us, to us, for us).
   - *Me habla.* — He speaks to me.
   - *Me lo habla.* — He's speaking to me about it.
   - *Me lo compra.* — He buys it for me. (No *para* needed.)
   - *Te lo compro.* — I buy it for you / from you (context decides).
2. **Position is the same as before**: little words still go before the changed verb. Don't let the new meaning move them. *Me habla*, NOT *habla me*.
3. **Asking a question is just intonation**: *¿Te lo compro?* with rising voice = "Shall I buy it for you?" — no special "shall" word, just question marks and tone.
4. **"Buy me!" idiom**: street vendors in Spanish-speaking countries say "*¡cómprame algo!*" meaning "buy me something" but really meaning "buy something from me". Context.
5. **The verb *hacer* (to do/make)** is irregular — the teacher says we don't need to dwell on that yet. *¿Quieres hacer?* = "Do you want to do?". *¿Quieres hacerlo?* = "Do you want to do it?". *¿Me lo quieres hacer?* / *¿Quieres hacérmelo?* = "Do you want to do it for me?".
6. ***Cocina*** — kitchen. Pronounced /cosina/ in Latin America, /cothina/ in most of Spain.
7. **To cook = *cocinar***. Just turn the noun into a verb with *-ar*. (English "concoction" comes from the same root — "with cooking".)
8. ***Cocinar* full set**: *cocino, cocinas, cocina, cocinamos, cocinan*.
9. ***Poder* (to be able / can)**: *puedo, puedes, puede, podemos, pueden*. Note the **o → ue split** when stressed.
10. **"Can you cook something for me?" = *¿Me puedes cocinar algo?*** — *me* before the changed verb (*puedes*).
11. **"At [time] o'clock" = *a las [number]***. Literally "at the [number] hours" (*a las seis horas*), but you don't say *horas*. *A las seis* = at six o'clock.
12. **Numbers introduced**:
    - *seis* (6)
    - *ocho* (8) — like *octa*gon, *octo*pus, *Oct*ober (which used to be the 8th month).
    - *nueve* (9) — like *Nov*ember (used to be 9th). The /o/ splits to /ue/: *nove → nueve*.
    - *diez* (10) — /dieth/ in Spain, /dies/ in Latin America. Like *Dec*ember (used to be 10th).
    - *siete* (7) — like beginning of "set". E-split.
13. **Q-U is always /k/ in Spanish** — never /kw/. *Quedar* = /kedar/, NOT /kwedar/. Same in *que, quiero, máquina*.
14. ***Quedar* = "to remain" / "to be left over"** (it's flexible — has more meanings later). Spelled *q-u-e-d-a-r*.
15. **"I stay" = *me quedo*** ("I remain myself"). Spanish doesn't really say "I stay"; it says "I remain myself". *Me* before the verb makes it reflexive.
16. ***Quedar* set**: *me quedo, te quedas, se queda, nos quedamos, se quedan*. (Doesn't split — even though *e* is stressed, *quedo* stays as is.)
17. **"Until" = *hasta*** (h-a-s-t-a, silent *h*). *Hasta la vista* = "until the view / until we see each other". *Hasta las diez* = until ten o'clock.
18. **"Here" = *aquí* (Spain) or *acá* (Latin America)**. Both work.
19. **Emphasis with reflexive verbs**: the *me/te/nos* is NOT optional and NOT emphatic — you must include it for "stay" to mean stay. To add emphasis, also put the subject pronoun: *yo me quedo, tú te quedas, nosotros nos quedamos*.

## Vocabulary
- *hablar* — to speak
- *me habla* — he speaks to me
- *me lo habla* — he speaks to me about it
- *comprar* — to buy
- *me lo compra* — he buys it for me
- *te lo compro* — I buy it for you
- *hacer* — to do / to make
- *hacerlo* — to do it
- *la cocina* — the kitchen
- *cocinar* — to cook
- *cocino, cocinas, cocina, cocinamos, cocinan* — cook (conjugated)
- *poder* — to be able / can
- *puedo, puedes, puede, podemos, pueden* — can (conjugated)
- *algo* — something
- *seis* — six
- *siete* — seven
- *ocho* — eight
- *nueve* — nine
- *diez* — ten
- *quedar* — to remain / be left over
- *me quedo* — I stay (I remain myself)
- *te quedas* — you stay
- *nos quedamos* — we stay
- *aquí / acá* — here
- *hasta* — until
- *hasta la vista* — until we see each other / see you

## Examples / Sentences
- *Me habla.* — He speaks to me.
- *Me hablan.* — They speak to me.
- *Me lo habla.* — He's speaking to me about it.
- *Me lo compra.* — He buys it for me.
- *Te lo compro.* — I buy it for you.
- *¿Te lo compro?* — Shall I buy it for you?
- *¿Quieres hacer?* — Do you want to do?
- *¿Quieres hacerlo?* — Do you want to do it?
- *¿Me lo quieres hacer?* / *¿Quieres hacérmelo?* — Do you want to do it for me?
- *Cocino.* — I cook.
- *Tú cocinas.* — You cook.
- *Nosotros cocinamos.* — We cook.
- *¿Puedes cocinar?* — Can you cook?
- *¿Me puedes cocinar algo?* — Can you cook something for me?
- *He cocinado.* — I have cooked.
- *He cocinado a las seis.* — I have cooked at 6 o'clock.
- *Hemos cocinado a las ocho.* — We have cooked at 8 o'clock.
- *Me quedo.* — I stay.
- *Me quedo aquí.* — I'm staying here.
- *Me quedo aquí hasta las diez.* — I'm staying here until 10.
- *Nos quedamos hasta las nueve.* — We're staying until 9.
- *Te quedas.* — You stay.
- *Te quedas aquí.* — You're staying here.
- *¿Te quedas aquí?* — Are you staying here?
- *Yo me quedo.* — I'm staying (emphatic).
- *Nosotros nos quedamos.* — WE are staying.
