# Lesson 37

## Summary
We learn the small "people words" that go with verbs (*me*, *te*, *nos*, *lo/la/los/las*, *le/les*) — how they change, what order they go in, and how *lo/la/los/las* turn into *le/les* when they mean "to/for him/her/you/them."

## Rules
1. *Dar* (to give) takes the regular future endings: *daré, darás, dará, daremos, darán*. (Future = the "to" form + *-é, -ás, -á, -emos, -án*.)
2. The little word *me* can mean "me" or "to me" — same word covers both. Same for *te* (you/to you) and *nos* (us/to us).
3. The word *lo* means "it" (masculine) when referring to a thing.
4. When you stack two little words, the personal one comes first: *¿Me lo darán?* = "Will they give it to me?" (person *me*, then thing *lo*).
5. If the "it" is a feminine thing, use *la* instead: *¿Me la darán?*
6. *¿Me lo darán?* in Spanish is a literal question ("Are you guys going to give it to me?"), not a polite request. For "Can you give it to me?" use *poder* (can).
7. *Algo* = "something."
8. Order to build a verb: first ask "Who is doing the verb?" Build the verb form first, then add the little words. Example: *Te darán algo* (they will give you something).
9. *Me*, *te*, *nos* can also mean "myself, yourself, ourselves" (or "each other"). Example: *me doy* = I give myself.
10. *Me doy cuenta* = "I realize" (literally "I give myself account"). *Cuenta* is like "account." *Te das cuenta* = you realize. *¿Te das cuenta?* = "Do you know what I mean?" (very common).
11. Big rule: *lo, la, los, las* CHANGE to *le* and *les* when the meaning is "to him / to her / to you (formal) / to them / for him / etc." (4 options collapse to 2.)
12. *Le doy* = I give (to) him/her/you-formal. *Les doy* = I give to them.
13. *Me, te, nos* do NOT change for these meanings — they already cover everything (me, to me, myself, etc.).
14. To find the right form, listen for the FEELING: "Am I being cooked? Am I being spoken (out of someone's mouth)?" → direct (*lo/la*). "Am I being cooked FOR? Am I being given TO?" → indirect (*le/les*). Don't translate "to/for" word-by-word.
15. With *me, te, nos* it doesn't matter anyway — they don't change. So *me cocina algo* covers both "he's cooking me something" and "he's cooking something for me."
16. For he/she/them, you DO have to choose: *le cocina algo* (he cooks him/her/you-formal something) vs *los cocina* (he's cooking them — the things).
17. When you have both little words, drop *algo* — the *lo/la* already says "it": *Nos las cocina* = "He's cooking them for us."
18. Past participle of *hablar* is *hablado* (spoken). So *he hablado* = I have spoken; *le he hablado* = I have spoken to him/her.
19. *Veo* (I see) is slightly irregular — it's *veo*, not *vo*.
20. *Cocinar* (to cook) comes from *la cocina* (the kitchen) — just stick *-r* on the end.

## Vocabulary
- *dar* — to give
- *daré, darás, dará, daremos, darán* — I/you/he/we/they will give
- *algo* — something
- *lo, la, los, las* — it/them (things, direct)
- *le, les* — to/for him, her, you (formal), them (indirect)
- *me, te, nos* — me/you/us (and to me, myself, etc. — they don't change)
- *cuenta* — account
- *darse cuenta* — to realize ("give oneself account")
- *hablar* — to speak
- *hablado* — spoken (past participle)
- *cocinar* — to cook
- *la cocina* — the kitchen
- *las tartas* — the tarts
- *ver* — to see
- *veo* — I see

## Examples / Sentences
- *¿Me lo darán?* — Will they give it to me?
- *Te darán algo.* — They will give you something.
- *Te lo darán.* — They will give it to you.
- *Nos lo darán.* — They will give it to us.
- *No nos lo darán.* — They won't give it to us.
- *Me doy cuenta.* — I realize.
- *¿Te das cuenta?* — Do you realize? / Do you know what I mean?
- *Nos damos cuenta.* — We realize.
- *Lo doy.* — I give it.
- *Los doy.* — I give them (things).
- *Les doy algo.* — I give them something.
- *He hablado.* — I have spoken.
- *Le he hablado.* — I have spoken to him/her.
- *Le hemos hablado.* — We have spoken to him/her/you (formal).
- *Han hablado.* — They have spoken.
- *Lo han hablado.* — They have spoken about it.
- *Les han hablado.* — They have spoken to them.
- *Lo veo.* — I see it/him.
- *La veo.* — I see her/it (fem).
- *Las veo.* — I see them (fem).
- *Me cocina algo.* — He's cooking me something.
- *Le cocina algo.* — He's cooking him/her something.
- *Los cocina.* — He's cooking them (the things).
- *Nos las cocina.* — He's cooking them (fem) for us.
- *Le hablo.* — I speak to him.
- *Les hablan.* — They speak to them.
