# Lesson 64

## Summary
We learn how to say "ago" in Spanish using *hace* + a time amount, and we finish off the past-point tense by adding the *you (informal)* and *they* endings. We also see why *-er/-ir* verbs flip their vowel pattern in the past.

## Rules
1. To say "X time ago," Spanish says "it makes X time": *hace* + amount of time. Example: *hace dos semanas* = two weeks ago. (*Hace* comes from *hacer*, to do/make.)
2. You stick *hace* phrases right onto a sentence, no extra words. *Lo vi hace dos semanas.* = I saw him two weeks ago.
3. When you say "I responded to her something" (like "I responded to her the mail"), Spanish prefers "I responded *her* the mail" — drop the "to." This becomes *le respondí el mail*, and if the mail is already known, *se lo respondí* (I responded it to her). Spanish doesn't lean on possessive words as hard as English does.
4. *Le* turns into *se* whenever it bumps into *lo/la/los/las* — that's why "I responded it to her" is *se lo respondí* (not "le lo").
5. *Mes* (month) is masculine: *el mes* = the month, *un mes* = a month / one month. So "one month ago" = *hace un mes*.
6. Past-point endings so far: *-é* (I), *-ó* (he/she/it/you formal), *-amos* (we) for *-ar* verbs; *-í*, *-ió*, *-imos* for *-er/-ir* verbs. Example with *responder*: *respondí, respondió, respondimos*.
7. New past-point endings for *you (informal)*: *-aste* for *-ar* verbs, *-iste* for *-er/-ir* verbs. Examples: *hablaste*, *comiste*.
8. New past-point endings for *they / you-all*: *-aron* for *-ar* verbs, *-ieron* for *-er/-ir* verbs. Examples: *hablaron, esperaron, comieron, salieron, vendieron*.
9. To make a question, just put question marks around the same sentence. *¿Comiste?* = Did you eat? *¿No comiste?* = Didn't you eat? *¿No lo comiste?* = Didn't you eat it?
10. *Last night* is *anoche* — careful, don't translate "tonight" as *anoche*. *Tonight* is *esta noche* ("this night").
11. Whether to use the past-point or the past-line is often interchangeable: ¿Dónde viviste? (closed time, point) vs ¿Dónde vivías? (ongoing, line). Both can work — it just shows which idea is in the speaker's mind.
12. Big sound trick: in the present tense, *-er/-ir* verbs are full of /e/ (*come, comen, comes, comemos*) with only one tiny /i/ popping in (*comimos*… wait, that's past — in the present it's *vivimos/salimos* etc., the one /i/). In the past-point it flips: *-er/-ir* is full of /i/ (*comí, comió, comiste, comimos, comieron*), with only one /e/ peeking in at *-ieron*.
13. That little /e/ in *-ieron* is there to remind us this is the *-er/-ir* group, not just *-ir*. (Mirror image of what /i/ does in the present.)

## Vocabulary
- *hacer* — to do / to make
- *hace* — it does / it makes (also "ago" with time)
- *semana* — week (feminine)
- *dos* — two
- *responder* — to respond
- *siete* — seven (related to September)
- *mes* — month (masculine)
- *discriminación* — discrimination (note: with an *i*, not *e*)
- *discriminar* — to discriminate
- *salir* — to leave / to go out
- *anoche* — last night
- *esta noche* — tonight ("this night")
- *vivir* — to live
- *dónde* — where
- *esperar* — to wait / to hope
- *llegar* — to arrive
- *cuándo* — when
- *vender* — to sell
- *preparar* — to prepare
- *comer* — to eat
- *hablar* — to speak

## Examples / Sentences
- *Hace dos semanas.* — Two weeks ago.
- *Lo vi hace dos semanas.* — I saw him two weeks ago.
- *Le respondí.* — I responded to her.
- *Le respondí hace siete semanas.* — I responded to her seven weeks ago.
- *Se lo respondí.* — I responded it to her.
- *Se lo respondí hace un mes.* — I responded it to her a month ago.
- *Respondí / respondió / respondimos.* — I / he-she-it / we responded.
- *¿Comiste?* — Did you eat? *¿No comiste?* — Didn't you eat? *¿No lo comiste?* — Didn't you eat it?
- *¿Saliste anoche?* — Did you go out last night? *¿No saliste anoche?* — Didn't you go out last night?
- *¿Dónde viviste? / ¿Dónde vivías?* — Where did you live? / Where were you living?
- *¿Me esperaste? / ¿No me esperaste? / ¿Por qué no me esperaste?* — Did you wait for me? / Didn't you wait for me? / Why didn't you wait for me?
- *¿Cuándo llegaste?* — When did you arrive?
- *Hablaron / esperaron / lo prepararon / comieron / salieron / los vendieron.*
- Full panorama (hablar): *hablé, habló, hablamos, hablaste, hablaron*.
- Full panorama (comer): *comí, comió, comimos, comiste, comieron*.
