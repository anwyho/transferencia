# Lesson 04

## Summary
We learn *quiero* ("I want") and the powerhouse rule: English `-tion` words become Spanish `-ción` words, and dropping the `-ción` and adding `-r` gives you the verb. Suddenly we know about 1,000 verbs.

## Rules
1. ***Te quiero* literally means "I want you"** and is the everyday "I love you" between friends/lovers. *Te amo* exists but is much more serious.
2. **Strip *te* (you) off and you're left with *quiero* = "I want."**
3. **To say "I don't want" just put *no* in front: *no quiero*.** Same word order as English.
4. **English `-ion` → Spanish `-ión` (same word, accent on the end).** Pronounce syllable by syllable: o-pi-NIÓN, pa-SIÓN, pre-pa-ra-CIÓN.
5. **The `-tion` ending → `-ción` in Spanish.** Spelled c-i-o-n.
6. **The c-i-o-n sound:** in most of the Spanish-speaking world it's /sión/ (like /s/); in most of Spain it's /thión/ (like English /th/). Use /s/ — that's the majority and it's easier. *organización* = /or-ga-ni-sa-CIÓN/ for most, /or-ga-ni-tha-THIÓN/ in Spain.
7. **The letter *z* follows the same /s/ vs /th/ split.** *cerveza* (beer) = /cervesa/ or /cervetha/.
8. **Don't pronounce English *sh*** — you said /pasión/ with a clean /s/, perfect.
9. **The killer rule: take an `-ación` word, strip the `-ción`, add `-r` → you get the verb (the "to ___" form).** *preparación → preparar* (to prepare), *generación → generar* (to generate), *confirmación → confirmar* (to confirm), *complicación → complicar* (to complicate).
10. **"Negative language transfer" vs "positive language transfer."** Things English drags into Spanish that don't belong = negative (e.g. saying *confermación* because of "confirm"). Things English gives us that *do* work (Latin words) = positive. Notice the difference and lean into the positive.
11. **The "long route" trick: don't jump from English to verb directly.** Go through the noun: *generation → generación → generar.* That installs a clean process.
12. **Etymology hooks help memory.** *Confirmación* = "with signing" (con + firma = signature). *Conversación* = "with verses." *Confrontación* = "with the forehead" (frente = forehead — you go forward face-first). *Complicación* = "with folding" (Latin *plicare* = to fold).
13. **com- becomes con- in Spanish before most consonants, but stays com- before *p* (because of how the lips move).** *complicación.*

## Vocabulary
- *urgente* — urgent
- *urgentemente* — urgently
- *te quiero* — I love you (everyday); literally "I want you"
- *te amo* — I love you (very serious)
- *quiero* — I want
- *no quiero* — I don't want
- *te* — you (informal, object)
- *opinión* — opinion
- *pasión* — passion
- *preparación* — preparation
- *preparar* — to prepare
- *organización* — organization
- *generación* — generation
- *generar* — to generate
- *confirmación* — confirmation
- *confirmar* — to confirm
- *firma* — signature
- *firmar* — to sign
- *con* — with
- *conversación* — conversation
- *confrontación* — confrontation
- *frente* — forehead / front
- *complicación* — complication
- *complicar* — to complicate

## Examples / Sentences
- *Quiero preparar.* — I want to prepare.
- *No quiero.* — I don't want.
