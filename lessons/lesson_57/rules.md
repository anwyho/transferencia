# Lesson 57

## Summary
We learn the one tiny place where *-er* and *-ir* verbs split apart in the present (the "we" form), and we discover cool tricks with *lo* — like *lo interesante* (the interesting thing) and *lo de Juan* (Juan's business / the thing about Juan).

## Rules
1. In the WHOLE Spanish language, there is just ONE moment where *-er* and *-ir* verbs are different: it's the "we" form in the present. *-er* uses *-emos* (*comemos*), *-ir* uses *-imos* (*vivimos*). Everywhere else they share endings.
2. Quick recap of present endings: he/she/it eats = *come* (uses /e/); he/she/it lives = *vive* (uses /e/); they/you-guys eat = *comen*; they/you-guys live = *viven*.
3. *Lo + adjective* = "the X thing". *Lo interesante* = "the interesting thing". *Lo complicado* = "the complicated thing". *Lo bueno / lo malo* = "the good thing / the bad thing". *Lo fácil* = "the easy thing".
4. *Junto* means "together". Singular masculine. Mnemonic: it's spelled with a J — connects to "junction" / "join" in English.
5. *Junto* agrees with the people: *juntos* (mixed/masc), *juntas* (all feminine).
6. *Fácil* means "easy" — see the link with English "facilitate" (to make easy). Mnemonic stuck.
7. *Venir* (to come) is irregular: *I come* = *vengo*, but *they come* = *vienen*. The teacher's pep talk: don't try to remember everything at once, just build what's in front of you. Comfort with uncertainty is essential — if you wait until you know everything, you'll never speak.
8. *Así* (a-s-í) = "like this / like that". It's a wonderful shortcut instead of saying *de esta forma* / *de esta manera*. Very common.
9. *Lo de* + something = "the thing about" / "the business about". *Lo de la fiesta* = "the thing about the party". *Lo de Juan* = "Juan's business" / "that thing about Juan".
10. With *lo de*, we treat "lo de" as a unit. You can swap or stack: *hablamos de lo de la fiesta*, *hablamos sobre lo de la fiesta*, *hablamos lo de la fiesta*.
11. *Mañana* = both "morning" and "tomorrow". With *la* (*la mañana*) it usually means morning; without it usually means tomorrow.
12. *De esta manera* / *de esta forma* are emphatic versions of *así*. *Así* is the everyday word.
13. Word order: little word (*lo*) before unchanged verb, OR stuck on the end. *No quiero hablar de lo de Juan así* / *No quiero hablarlo así* — both fine.
14. Build forms by going DIRECTLY from the "to"-form to the form you want. Don't take detours through other forms (you'll get lost). Then check if it feels right; if not, it might be irregular.

## Examples / Sentences
- *Hablamos. / Comemos. / Vivimos.* — We speak / eat / live.
- *¿Dónde viven?* — Where do you guys live?
- *Lo interesante es que viven aquí.* — The interesting thing is that they live here.
- *Lo interesante es que viven juntos / juntas.* — The interesting thing is that they live together.
- *Lo fácil es que vienen juntos.* — The easy thing is that they come together.
- *Lo complicado es que vienen juntos.* — The complicated thing is that they come together.
- *No hablamos así.* — We don't speak like that.
- *No comemos así.* — We don't eat like that.
- *No vivimos así.* — We don't live like that.
- *Hablamos de lo de la fiesta. / Hablamos sobre lo de la fiesta. / Hablamos lo de la fiesta.* — We talk about the thing about the party.
- *Lo de Juan.* — Juan's business / the thing about Juan.
- *No quiero hablar de lo de Juan así.* — I don't want to talk about Juan's business like this.
- *Quiero organizar lo de mañana.* — I want to organize the thing about tomorrow.
- *Pero no quiero organizarlo así.* — But I don't want to organize it like this.
