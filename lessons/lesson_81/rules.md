# Lesson 81

## Summary
We finish the mood tense by combining "on me" *se me* with the mood tense, learning *volver a* = "again", and building the **past mood tense** (works the same as present, just back-shifted).

## Rules
1. *No quiero que la puerta se me cierre* = I don't want the door to close on me. *Cerrar* (e→ie) flips to mood form *cierre*. After *no quiero que* we always use the mood tense.
2. *Otra vez* = again / another time.
3. *No quiero que la puerta se me cierre otra vez* = I don't want the door to close on me again.
4. **Volver a + verb = "to do (verb) again"**. Literally "to return to (do)". *Vuelve a cerrarse* = it closes again (returns to close itself).
5. *Volver* needs the connector *a* because it's a verb of movement.
6. *No quiero que la puerta vuelva a cerrarse* = I don't want the door to close again.
7. **"To happen" verbs**: *pasar* (easiest, "to pass"), *ocurrir* (occur), *suceder* (succession). All mean "to happen".
8. *No quiero que vuelva a pasar* = I don't want it to happen again.
9. **Mood tense exists only in present and past.** No future mood tense exists in modern Spanish (just a couple of fossilized fixed expressions).
10. **Building the past mood tense** (one form for both line and point — same form covers both):
    - Step 1: Take the *they* form of the **point in the past**. *Hablar* → *hablaron*; *comer* → *comieron*; *organizar* → *organizaron*.
    - Step 2: Change the final *-on* to *-an* → that's the *they* mood-past. *Hablaran*, *comieran*, *organizaran*.
    - Step 3: Drop the *n* for the **I/he/she/it/you (formal)** form: *hablara*, *comiera*.
    - Step 4: Add *-s* for **you (informal)**: *hablaras*, *comieras*.
    - Step 5: For **we**, add *-mos* with an accent on the *a* before it (accent stays put): *habláramos*, *comiéramos*, *organizáramos*.
11. The line in past + mood works the same: *quería que* (I wanted that) + past mood tense.
12. *Quería que comieran con nosotros* = I wanted them to eat with us.
13. *Esperaba que me llamara* = I was hoping that he called me. (English "would call me" → Spanish past mood tense, no separate "would" word.)
14. *Necesitábamos hablar con él* = we needed to speak with him (no mood tense, no *que*).
15. *Necesitábamos que hablara con nosotros* = we needed him to speak with us. (*que* signals the mood tense.)
16. **Volver a (review) as "again"**: *volví a llamarte* = I called you again. Nothing to do with literal returning.
17. You can dodge the mood tense by rephrasing with *para + to-form*. *Volví a llamarte para organizar* = I called you again in order to organize (no mood tense needed).
18. But if you say "so that we organized" you must use *para que + past mood tense*: *Volví a llamarte para que organizáramos*.
19. **Big tip from teacher**: *que* in a sentence is most often the alarm bell that you might want the mood tense.

## Vocabulary
- *cerrar* — to close
- *cierre / cierres / cierre / cerremos / cierren* — mood forms (present)
- *otra vez* — again
- *volver* — to return / come back
- *volver a + verb* — to do (verb) again
- *vuelva* — (mood) returns
- *cerrarse* — to close itself
- *pasar* — to pass / happen
- *ocurrir* — to occur / happen
- *suceder* — to happen / succession
- *hablar / hablaron / hablaran / hablara / hablaras / habláramos* — speak forms (past mood)
- *comer / comieron / comieran / comiera / comieras / comiéramos* — eat forms (past mood)
- *organizar / organizaron / organizaran / organizáramos* — organize forms
- *querer* — to want
- *quería* — I wanted (line past)
- *esperar* — to hope / wait
- *esperaba* — I was hoping (line past)
- *llamar* — to call
- *llamara* — (mood past) he called
- *llámame* — call me
- *necesitar* — to need
- *necesitábamos* — we needed
- *volví* — I returned (point past, comer-style)
- *volví a llamarte* — I called you again
- *con él* — with him

- *No quiero que la puerta vuelva a cerrarse.* — I don't want the door to close again.
- *No quiero que vuelva a pasar.* — I don't want it to happen again.
- *Hablar → hablaron → hablaran → hablara → hablaras → habláramos.* — Past mood ladder.
- *Comer → comieron → comieran → comiera → comieras → comiéramos.* — Past mood ladder.

- *Necesitábamos hablar con él.* — We needed to speak with him.
