# Lesson 45

## Summary
More about *gustar*-style verbs that flip the sentence: *interesar* (to interest), *parecer* (to seem), and others. We also learn *poner* (to put) and how *com-/sup-/op-/im-pose* in English become *componer/suponer/oponer/imponer* in Spanish.

## Rules
1. Translate *gustar* mentally as "to be pleasing" or "to please" (NOT "to like") — the literal angle keeps you in control.
2. With *gustar*, always start with the verb. The verb agrees with the THING that pleases, not with the person who likes it. *Le gusta* (it pleases him/her/you-formal); *Les gusta* (it pleases them/you guys); *Le gustan* (they please him); *Les gustan* (they please them).
3. With *gustar*-type verbs, you use *le/les* (not *lo/la/los/las*). Reason: it's "pleasing TO him/TO her" — the "to" hides inside *le*.
4. *Interesar* = to interest / to be interesting to. Looks like a verb (ends in *-ar*). *Interesante* = interesting. Better stored as "to be interesting to" — same flip as *gustar*.
5. *Me interesa* = it interests me / it's interesting to me. Build all the forms: *te interesa, le interesa, nos interesa, les interesa* (with the verb agreeing with the thing).
6. Question/negative: *¿Te interesa?, ¿No te interesa?, ¿Por qué no te interesa?*
7. *Bailar esta noche* = to dance tonight. *¿Le interesa bailar esta noche?* = Does it interest her to dance tonight?
8. *Salir* = to leave / to go out. *Salir a bailar* = to go out to dance (a verb of motion + *a* + another verb). *¿Le interesa salir a bailar esta noche?*
9. Conditional: *Te interesaría* = It would interest you. (*Interesar* → *interesaría*.)
10. *Parecer* = to seem (already has "to" feeling included in English). *Me parece* = it seems to me. WATCH OUT: *parecer* is *-er*, not *-ar*. Always check the verb type before changing it!
11. The "to" form is your TAKEOFF POINT — go back, check whether it's *-ar/-er/-ir*, then change.
12. *Me parece que...* = It seems to me that... In Spanish, the *que* (that) is OBLIGATORY (English makes it optional). Putting *que* always also helps you think out the sentence.
13. *Me parece que no quiere salir esta noche* = It seems to me that he doesn't want to go out tonight.
14. *Que* (no accent) = that (connector). *Qué* (with accent) = what (question). Same root word.
15. *Bien* = well (not "good"). *Me parece bien* = it seems well to me, sounds fine to me. There's a fuzzy line between *bueno* (good) and *bien* (well) — you'll absorb it from listening.
16. *No me parece bien* = it doesn't seem right to me. *No le parece bien* = it doesn't seem right to him/her. *¿No le parece bien?* = Doesn't it seem right to you (formal)?
17. *Me interesaría verlo* = It would interest me to see it.
18. *Poner* = to put. The English chunk *-pose* (compose, suppose, oppose, impose) maps to *poner* in Spanish: *componer, suponer, oponer, imponer*.
19. *Componer* literally = "with-putting" (con- becomes com- before *p*). It's "putting together" → "to compose."
20. With *poner* + small word: stick the small word on the end of the "to" form. *Quiero ponerlo aquí* = I want to put it here. *Me gustaría ponerlo aquí* = I'd like to put it here.
21. To say "put salt ON it," in Spanish you don't need "on." Use *le* (which carries on/for/from): *Quiero ponerle sal* = I want to put salt on it. (Not *lo*, because *lo* = it/him directly; *le* covers "on it / to it / for it / from it.")
22. *Sal* = salt. *Me gustaría ponerle sal* = I'd like to put salt on it. *Un poco* = a little. *Más* = more.
23. *Me gustaría ponerle un poco más sal* = I'd like to put a little more salt on it.
24. *Me gustaría ponerles un poco más sal* = I'd like to put a little more salt on them (e.g., *las papas fritas* = the chips/fries).

## Vocabulary
- *gustar* — to be pleasing (to like)
- *gusta, gustan* — it/they pleases
- *le gusta, les gusta, le gustan, les gustan* — he/she/they likes / likes them
- *interesar* — to interest / to be interesting to
- *interesante* — interesting
- *me/te/le/nos/les interesa* — interests me/you/him/us/them
- *interesaría* — would interest
- *salir* — to leave, to go out
- *salir a bailar* — to go out to dance
- *bailar esta noche* — to dance tonight
- *parecer* — to seem
- *me parece, le parece* — it seems to me / to him
- *que* — that (connector)
- *qué* — what (question)
- *bien* — well
- *bueno* — good
- *poner* — to put
- *componer* — to compose
- *suponer* — to suppose
- *oponer* — to oppose
- *imponer* — to impose
- *ponerlo, ponerle* — to put it / to put on it
- *aquí* — here
- *sal* — salt
- *un poco* — a little
- *más* — more
- *las papas fritas* — chips / french fries

## Examples / Sentences
- *Me gusta.* — I like it.
- *Nos gusta.* — We like it. (it is pleasing to us)
- *Le gusta.* — He/she/you (formal) likes it.
- *Les gusta.* — They/you guys like it.
- *Le gustan.* — He likes them.
- *Les gustan.* — They like them.
- *Me interesa.* — It interests me.
- *¿Te interesa?* — Does it interest you?
- *¿No te interesa?* — Doesn't it interest you?
- *¿Por qué no te interesa?* — Why doesn't it interest you?
- *Le interesa.* — It interests him/her/you (formal).
- *Les interesa.* — It interests them.
- *¿Le interesa bailar esta noche?* — Does it interest her to dance tonight?
- *¿Le interesa salir a bailar esta noche?* — Does it interest her to go out to dance tonight?
- *Salir a bailar esta noche.* — To go out to dance tonight.
- *¿Te interesaría?* — Would it interest you?
- *Me interesaría verlo.* — It would interest me to see it.
- *Me parece.* — It seems to me.
- *Me parece que...* — It seems to me that...
- *Me parece que no quiere salir esta noche.* — It seems he doesn't want to go out tonight.
- *Me parece que no le gusta.* — It seems to me he doesn't like it.
- *Me parece bien.* — Seems good/fine to me.
- *No me parece bien.* — It doesn't seem right to me.
- *No le parece bien.* — It doesn't seem right to him/her/you (formal).
- *¿No le parece bien?* — Doesn't it seem right to you (formal)?
- *Componer / suponer / oponer / imponer.* — To compose / suppose / oppose / impose.
- *Quiero ponerlo aquí.* — I want to put it here.
- *Me gustaría ponerlo aquí.* — I'd like to put it here.
- *Quiero ponerle sal.* — I want to put salt on it.
- *Me gustaría ponerle sal.* — I'd like to put salt on it.
- *Me gustaría ponerle un poco más sal.* — I'd like to put a little more salt on it.
- *Me gustaría ponerles un poco más sal.* — I'd like to put a little more salt on them.
