# Lesson 39

## Summary
Meet prepositions: little words like *con* (with), *para* (for), *de* (of/from). After them, Spanish uses he/she/they (not him/her/them) — except *me* and *you* get special forms *mí* and *ti*.

## Rules
1. *Con* = with. We have it inside English words like *con*venient, *con*firm, *con*sequence (literally "with-sequence").
2. After a preposition in Spanish, you don't say "with him/her/them" — you literally say "with he, with she, with they": *con él, con ella, con ellos / ellas, con nosotros / nosotras, con usted, con ustedes*.
3. Prepositions are little words showing movement, position, or direction. They're in a group together: *con, para, de*, etc.
4. *Para* = for. *Para él, para ella, para ellos/ellas, para nosotros/nosotras*.
5. EXCEPTION: after a preposition, "me" becomes *mí* (m-í) and "you informal" becomes *ti* (t-i). So: *para mí* (for me), *para ti* (for you).
6. *De* = of, from. *De él, de ella, de mí, de ti, de ellos, de ellas, de nosotros, de ustedes*.
7. To make a yes/no question, just put *¿...?* with intonation: *¿Es de ustedes?* = Is it from you guys?
8. Use *es* (from *ser*) for "is" when describing characteristic things ("it's from you guys, it's for you guys" = belonging-style). Use *está* (from *estar*) when speaking about a state/location ("it is with you guys" — location of something).
9. *Hacer* = to do or to make. *Hace* = he/she does or makes. (Not from *haber* even though it sounds similar.)
10. *Un favor* = a favor. *Me hace un favor* = he's doing me a favor.
11. Watch out: *La hace un favor* could sound like "he's making it (a feminine thing)." So if you mean "he's doing HER a favor," use *le*: *Le hace un favor*. (Reminder of the *lo/la → le/les* rule.)
12. *Decir* = to tell or to say. Pronounced /dethir/ in most of Spain (the *c* is the "th" sound).
13. With a second verb (in the "to" form), you can put the little word EITHER before the first verb OR stuck on the end of the second verb: *Le quiero decir* OR *Quiero decirle* — both fine.
14. But for "I want to say IT" (the thing being said), it's *Lo quiero decir* (the *lo* refers to a thing, no preposition replacing).
15. *Le* and *les* are kind of imaginary prepositions — they replace "to, for, from" all wrapped up in one little word.
16. *Comprar* = to buy. *Compro* = I buy. *Los compro* = I buy them (things). *Les compro* = I buy for them. *Compro para ellos* is technically possible but less natural — Spanish prefers *les compro*.
17. Most natural Spanish drops the explicit prepositions in favor of *me, te, nos, le, les*. Example: "He buys it for me" = *Me lo compra*. "They buy them for me" = *Me los compran*.
18. *Me, te, nos, le, les* mostly replace "to" but can also replace "for" or even "from": *Me lo compra* could mean "He buys it for me" OR "He buys it from me" — context decides.
19. Big takeaway: English uses prepositions WAY more than Spanish — listen and you'll naturally start dropping them.

## Vocabulary
- *con* — with
- *para* — for
- *de* — of, from
- *en* — in, on
- *mí* — me (after a preposition)
- *ti* — you informal (after a preposition)
- *él, ella, ellos, ellas* — he, she, they (m), they (f)
- *nosotros, nosotras* — we (m), we (f)
- *usted, ustedes* — you (formal), you guys
- *hacer* — to do, to make
- *hace* — he/she/it does/makes
- *un favor* — a favor
- *decir* — to tell, to say
- *comprar* — to buy
- *compro, compra, compran* — I/he-she/they buy

## Examples / Sentences
- *Con él / con ella / con ellos / con ellas.* — With him / her / them (m/f).
- *Con nosotros / con nosotras.* — With us (m/f).
- *Con usted / con ustedes.* — With you (formal) / with you guys.
- *Para él / para ella / para ellos / para ellas.* — For him / her / them.
- *Para mí.* — For me.
- *Para ti.* — For you (informal).
- *De él, de ella, de mí, de ti, de ellos, de ellas, de nosotros, de ustedes.* — Of/from him/her/me/you/them/us/you guys.
- *¿Es de ustedes?* — Is it from you guys?
- *Es para ustedes.* — It's for you guys.
- *Está con ustedes.* — It is (located) with you guys.
- *Me hace un favor.* — He's doing me a favor.
- *Le hace un favor.* — He's doing her/him/you (formal) a favor.
- *Quiero decir.* — I want to say.
- *Le quiero decir.* / *Quiero decirle.* — I want to tell him.
- *Lo quiero decir.* — I want to say it.
- *Compro.* — I buy.
- *Los compro.* — I buy them.
- *Les compro.* — I buy for them.
- *Compro para ellos.* — I buy for them (literal).
- *Me compra. / Me lo compra.* — He buys me / he buys it for me.
- *Me lo compran.* — They buy it for me / from me.
- *Me los compran.* — They buy them for me.
