# Lesson 83

## Summary
We practice the past mood tense after *que* and after *si* (when "if" goes hypothetical-past, like English "if I were"). We also meet two extra tools: the conditional ("would have" from *haber*) for the second half of *si* sentences, and a heads-up that an alternative past-subjunctive form exists (*-ase/-iese*).

## Rules
1. After *que* we can put the verb in the past mood tense to get the feeling "the fact that you may have done X." We're doing the same trick English does with "may have." Example: *Que lo compraras no es mi problema.* — That you bought it / The fact that you (may have) bought it is not my problem.
2. To form the past mood tense: take the *they* form of the point-in-the-past, drop *-on*, and you get the *I/he/she/you-formal* mood form. *compraron → comprara*. Add *-s* for *you informal* (*compraras*), etc.
3. After *si* (if), when we're being **hypothetical-about-the-past** (English "if I were"), use the past mood tense. *Si pudiera cocinar* = "If I could cook." We do NOT use the mood after *si* in normal present "if."
4. English mnemonic: in English we change "was" to "were" for hypothetical past ("if I were you"). Spanish does the same job with the past mood tense.
5. *Poder* (to be able to) goes irregular in the point-in-the-past: the *pod-* base turns into *pud-*. So *pudieron* (they were able to) → past mood base *pudiera* (if I could).
6. Conditional ("would") = take the FULL infinitive and add *-ía* endings. Memory hook: *me gustaría* (I would like) shows you the *-ía* sound. *Cocinaría* = I would cook. *Cancelaría* = I would cancel.
7. The classic "if" sentence: *Si pudiera + infinitive, lo + conditional.* Example: *Si pudiera cancelarlo, lo cancelaría.* (If I could cancel it, I would cancel it.)
8. *Haber* (the helper "to have") in the point-in-the-past: *hab-* turns into *hub-*. So *hubieron* (they had). Past mood base = *hubiera*. *Si me lo hubieras contado* = "If you had told me it."
9. "Would have" = conditional of *haber* = *habría* (the *e* falls out: it's not *habería*). Add the past participle: *habría ido* (I would have gone), *habríamos ido* (we would have gone).
10. The big "if-had" sentence: *Si + hubiera/hubieras... + past participle, habría + past participle.* Example: *Si me lo hubieras contado, habría ido.* (If you had told me it, I would have gone.)
11. *Tu casa* = your house (informal). *Su casa* = his/her/your-formal/their house — *su* covers all four meanings, context tells you which.
12. *Ver* (to see): the *I* form keeps an extra *e* (*veo*). Past mood base = *viera*. *Si lo viera, lo comprendería / entendería.* (If I saw it, I would understand it.)
13. *Comprender* and *entender* both = "to understand." Conditional: *comprendería*, *entendería*.
14. There's a second flavor of the past mood tense with *-ase/-iese* endings (*hablase* instead of *hablara*, *comiese* instead of *comiera*). Both forms mean exactly the same thing — just different dialects/styles. You don't need to USE this form, but you should RECOGNIZE it when you hear or read it.

## Vocabulary
- *que* — what / that
- *comprar* — to buy
- *compraron* — they bought
- *comprara/compraras* — that I/you bought (past mood)
- *problema* — problem (masculine, despite the -a)
- *decir* — to say / to tell
- *contar* — to count / to recount / to tell
- *contaras* — that you told (past mood)
- *poder* — to be able to / can
- *pudieron* — they were able to
- *pudiera* — could (past mood, "if I could")
- *cocinar* — to cook (lit. "to kitchen")
- *cocinaría* — I would cook
- *gustaría* — would like (memory hook for *-ía*)
- *cancelar* — to cancel
- *cancelarlo* — to cancel it
- *cancelaría* — I would cancel
- *haber* — to have (helper)
- *hubieron* — they had
- *hubieras* — that you had (past mood)
- *habría* — I/he/she/you-formal would have
- *habríamos* — we would have
- *ido* — gone (past participle of *ir*)
- *sabido* — known (past participle of *saber*)
- *hubiéramos* — that we had (past mood)
- *casa* — house
- *mi/tu/su* — my / your-informal / his-her-your-formal-their
- *ver* — to see
- *vieron* — they saw
- *viera/vieras* — that I/you saw (past mood)
- *comprender* — to understand
- *comprendería* — I/he would understand
- *entender* — to understand
- *entendería* — I/he would understand
- *hablase, entendiese, comiese, comiésemos* — alternate past-subjunctive forms (recognize only)

## Examples / Sentences
- *Que lo compraras no es mi problema.* — That you bought it is not my problem.
- *Que le contaras no es mi problema.* — That you told him is not my problem.
- *Si pudiera cocinar, cocinaría.* — If I could cook, I would cook.
- *Si pudiera cancelarlo, lo cancelaría.* — If I could cancel it, I would cancel it.
- *Si me lo hubieras contado, habría ido.* — If you had told me it, I would have gone.
- *Si hubiéramos sabido, habríamos ido.* — If we had known, we would have gone.
- *Si vieras su casa.* — If you saw their house.
- *Si lo viera, lo comprendería / entendería.* — If I saw it, I would understand it.
