# Lesson 72

## Summary
We use the mood tense to give COMMANDS — both negative ("don't do this") and most positive ones too. The one big exception: positive informal commands ("do this" to a friend) take a shortcut — just drop the *s* off the *you* form.

## Rules
1. To give COMMANDS, Spanish uses the mood tense — both negative and positive — with one exception (positive informal, see below).
2. NEGATIVE INFORMAL COMMAND: take the *you* form and put it in mood tense. *No hables* = don't speak. *No te quedes* = don't stay. *No lo des* = don't give it.
3. Don't confuse *no te quedas* (statement: "you don't stay") with *no te quedes* (command: "don't stay!"). The mood tense ending makes it a command.
4. *Ahí* (a-h-í) and *allí* (a-l-l-í) both mean "there" — barely any difference in sound or meaning.
5. With *no lo des*, you can clarify *a él / a ella* if needed: *No se lo des a él.* = Don't give it to him.
6. POSITIVE COMMAND TO MORE THAN ONE PERSON: also mood tense. *Coman* = eat (you all). *Esperen* = wait (you all).
7. POSITIVE COMMANDS attach the little words (me, lo, la, te, se) to the END of the verb, written as one word: *Espérenme* = wait for me (you all). *Espérenlo* = wait for him/it. *Espérenlos* = wait for them.
8. NEGATIVE COMMANDS keep little words BEFORE the verb (the normal position): *No los esperen* = don't wait for them. *No lo coma* = don't eat it. *No te olvides* = don't forget.
9. Why this attaching trick? Because the only thing distinguishing a command from a statement might be just one vowel change — sticking *me/lo/la* on the end gives your ear another clue that it's a command.
10. FORMAL COMMANDS (singular and plural) also use mood tense: *Coma* = eat (formal). *Cómalo* = eat it (formal). *No lo coma* = don't eat it (formal). *Cómanlo / no lo coman* = eat it / don't eat it (you all formal).
11. *Olvidar* = to forget (related to "oblivion"). It's a verb that doesn't like to be alone — you need to say WHAT is forgotten or use the reflexive: *lo olvido* (I forget it) or *me olvido* (I forget myself). So:
    - *Olvídelo* = forget it (formal).
    - *Olvídese* = forget yourself / forget (formal, no object).
    - *No lo olvides / No te olvides / No te lo olvides* = don't forget it (informal).
    - *No se olviden / No lo olviden / No se lo olviden* = don't forget (you all).
12. "Let's" commands also use mood tense: *No lo olvidemos* = let's not forget it. *No nos olvidemos* = let's not forget. (Reflexive *nos* added because *olvidar* doesn't like being alone.)
13. *Comprar* = to buy. *Compren lo que falta* = (you all) buy what is missing. Or *compren lo que hace falta* — same idea.
14. THE BIG EXCEPTION — POSITIVE INFORMAL COMMAND: just take the *you* form of the present and DROP THE *s*. *Compras → compra* = buy! *Comes → come* = eat! (Yes, this looks identical to the he/she/it form, but tone of voice and the small words attached to the end make it clear.)
15. Why this shortcut? Positive informal commands are what we use ALL DAY ("come here, wait, buy this, do that"), so Spanish made it the easiest form.
16. With positive informal commands, little words still go on the end: *Cómpralo* = buy it. *Cómpramelo* = buy it for me. *Cómelo* = eat it. *Olvídalo* = forget it. *Olvídate* = forget (yourself).
17. As soon as the same command goes negative, you switch back to mood tense: *Cómelo / no lo comas.* *Véndelo / no lo vendas.*
18. *Callar* = to quiet. *Callarse* = to shut up (reflexive). *Cállate* = shut up! (informal, not very polite). *Cállense* = shut up, you all (mood tense, plural).

## Quick reference table
- Positive informal (you): drop *s* from present *you* form. *Compra. Cómelo. Olvídate.*
- Positive informal (you all): mood tense. *Compren. Coman. Olviden.*
- Positive formal: mood tense. *Compre. Coma.*
- Negative (any flavor): mood tense, with little words BEFORE the verb. *No compres. No coma. No lo coman. No te olvides.*
- Positive commands attach little words at the end (*cómpralo, cómpramelo, espérenme*).

## Vocabulary
- *hablar* — to speak
- *quedarse* — to stay
- *dar* — to give
- *des / dé* — give (mood tense informal / formal)
- *ahí / allí* — there (tiny difference)
- *comer* — to eat
- *esperar* — to wait
- *olvidar / olvidarse* — to forget (related to "oblivion")
- *comprar* — to buy
- *vender* — to sell
- *callar / callarse* — to quiet / to shut up
- *faltar* — to be missing
- *hace falta* — it's necessary

## Examples / Sentences
- *No hables.* — Don't speak.
- *No te quedes (aquí / ahí).* — Don't stay (here / there).
- *No lo des. / No se lo des. / No se lo des a él.* — Don't give it. / Don't give it to him.
- *Coman. / Esperen.* — Eat (you all). / Wait (you all).
- *Espérenme. / Espérenlo. / Espérenlos.* — Wait for me / for him / for them (you all).
- *No los esperen.* — Don't wait for them.
- *Coma. / Cómalo. / No lo coma.* — Eat / eat it / don't eat it (formal).
- *No lo comas. / No lo coman.* — Don't eat it (informal / plural).
- *Cómanlo.* — Eat it (you all).
- *Olvídelo. / Olvídese.* — Forget it / forget (formal).
- *No lo olvides. / No te olvides. / No te lo olvides.* — Don't forget it (informal).
- *No se olviden. / No lo olviden. / No se lo olviden.* — Don't forget (you all).
- *No lo olvidemos. / No nos olvidemos.* — Let's not forget it / let's not forget.
- *Compren lo que falta / lo que hace falta.* — Buy what's missing.
- *Compra. / Cómpralo. / Cómpramelo.* — Buy. / Buy it. / Buy it for me. (informal positive)
- *Olvida. / Olvídalo. / Olvídate.* — Forget. / Forget it. / Forget yourself.
- *Cómelo. / No lo comas.* — Eat it / don't eat it (informal).
- *Véndelo. / No lo vendas.* — Sell it / don't sell it (informal).
- *Cállate. / Cállense.* — Shut up (informal) / Shut up (you all).
