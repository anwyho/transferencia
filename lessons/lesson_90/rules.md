# Lesson 90

## Summary
A short tour of dialect variations you'll meet (Spain's *leísmo*, *vosotros*, aspirated *s*, and *vos* in the Río de la Plata region) and a closing pep talk: keep practicing, enjoy the journey, listen actively, and don't worry about not understanding everything at first.

## Rules
1. **Spain "leísmo" dialect:** in most of Spain, when referring to PEOPLE (him/her/you-formal/you-all/them), they use *le* and *les* instead of *lo/la/los/las*. So *Le veo* = "I see him/her/you-formal" (in Spain). *Lo veo* = "I see it." In most of Latin America this isn't the case — they use *lo/la* even for people.
2. ***Vosotros* (you-plural informal)** is used in most of Spain only — basically nowhere in Latin America. Worth listening for if you're going to practice Spain Spanish.
3. **Aspirated *s* in many accents:** the *s* gets breathy or disappears. You'll hear */ehtá/* instead of *está*, */cómo ehtá/* instead of *cómo está*, */ehpero/* instead of *espero*. Common in Caribbean / southern Spain / parts of Latin America.
4. ***Vos* in the Río de la Plata** (around Montevideo and Buenos Aires, and roughly 30% of the Spanish-speaking world): another word for *tú*. Used exclusively or mixed with *tú*.
5. **Verb conjugations with *vos* shift slightly** because the accent lands on the END, so the diphthong (vowel split like *e→ie*, *o→ue*) doesn't happen.
   - *Tú puedes → Vos podés* (you can)
   - *Tú quieres → Vos querés* (you want)
6. The differences between dialects are minor; you now have the know-how to identify and understand them. If you want, adopt the dialect of the people you practice with — it's advisable, because the more you sound like what you're hearing, the more you'll understand back.
7. **Pan-Hispanic accent**: many native speakers who travel between Spanish-speaking countries develop a mixed accent. You may even find yourself switching accents depending on who you're talking to — that's normal, even native speakers do it.
8. **Listening is a separate skill from speaking.** You'll hear things you could *say* yourself but not catch by ear. Don't be discouraged. Insist on analyzing what you hear, breaking it apart the same way we built sentences up — but in reverse. The more you do this, the more Spanish will "slow down" for you.
9. **Final advice:** practice as much as possible. Repeat the course later — like watching a great movie a second time, you'll catch new things. Enjoy literature, music, cinema in Spanish. Watch films with subtitles, match English to Spanish bits and pieces. Don't obsess about the destination — enjoy the journey. You're already a Spanish speaker.

## Vocabulary
- *le / les* — him/her/you-formal/them (used as direct object in Spain's leísmo)
- *lo / la / los / las* — direct objects (standard)
- *vosotros* — you (plural informal, Spain only)
- *vos* — you (Río de la Plata, alternative to *tú*)
- *podés* — you can (vos form of *poder*)
- *querés* — you want (vos form of *querer*)
- *está / espero* — to be (state) / I wait — examples for aspirated *s*
- *hispanohablante* — Spanish-speaker
- *practicar* — to practice
- *hablar* — to speak
- *analizar* — to analyze
- *perfeccionar* — to perfect
- *español* — Spanish

## Examples / Sentences
- *Le veo.* — I see him/her/you-formal. (Spain)
- *Lo veo.* — I see it. (Spain) / I see him/it. (Latin America)
- */ehtá/, /cómo ehtá/, /ehpero/* — aspirated-s pronunciations of *está, cómo está, espero.*
- *Vos podés.* — You can. (Río de la Plata)
- *Vos querés.* — You want. (Río de la Plata)
- *Ahora hablamos español. Ahora somos hispanohablantes.* — Now we speak Spanish. Now we are Spanish-speakers.
- *Somos hispanohablantes y sólo tenemos que practicar, hablar y analizar para perfeccionar nuestro español.* — We are Spanish-speakers and we only have to practice, speak, and analyze to perfect our Spanish.
