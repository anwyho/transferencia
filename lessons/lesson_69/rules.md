# Lesson 69

## Summary
We meet the "mood tense" (subjunctive) — Spanish's special tense for showing your feelings, wishes, and subjectivity about the world. Building it is easy: just swap verb groups. *-ar* verbs use *-er/-ir* endings, and *-er/-ir* verbs use *-ar* endings.

## Rules
1. The "mood tense" (called the *subjunctive* in grammar books) is a tense that just shows mood — your subjectivity about the world.
2. We sort of have it in English too. Compare "It's important that you ARE here" (everyday) vs "It's important that you BE here" (more serious, more imperative). That switch from "are" to "be" is English's mood. Also: *Had I gone, I would know* / *Not only is it a problem, it's the biggest problem.*
3. To form the mood tense in Spanish: SWAP THE VERB GROUPS. *-ar* verbs use the *-er/-ir* endings, and *-er/-ir* verbs use the *-ar* endings.
4. Build the mood tense from the present forms: *habla → hable, hablas → hables, hablan → hablen, hablo → hable, hablamos → hablemos*.
5. Same for *-er/-ir*: *come → coma, comes → comas, comen → coman, como → coma, comemos → comamos*.
6. Just like in regular present tense, the *I* form and the *he/she/it/you-formal* form are the same in mood tense (*hable, coma*).
7. For "we" mood tense, *-ar* verbs go to *-emos* (*hablemos*), and *-er/-ir* verbs go to *-amos* (*comamos*). We use the /e/ for *-ar*, NOT the odd /i/ that pops up in *-ir* present (*vivimos, salimos*).
8. Mood tense uses come in two flavors: OBLIGATORY (you must use it or it sounds wrong) and OPTIONAL (you use it to add a flavor or feeling).
9. OBLIGATORY USE — after expressions of wanting/needing/etc., Spanish doesn't say "I want you to wait" — it says "I want THAT you wait": *Quiero que esperes.* The verb after *que* must be in mood tense.
10. The structure: [subjective verb] + *que* + [mood tense verb]. Example: *Quiero que lo esperes.* = I want you to wait for him.
11. To clarify ambiguous *te/me*: you can add *yo, tú, él, ella* etc. *¿Quieres que yo te espere?* = Do you want me to wait for you? (clarifies it's "I" doing the waiting).

## Vocabulary
- *hablar* — to speak
- *habla / hable* — he speaks / mood-tense version
- *hablan / hablen* — they speak / mood-tense version
- *hablo / hable* — I speak / mood-tense version
- *hablamos / hablemos* — we speak / mood-tense version
- *comer* — to eat
- *como / coma* — I eat / mood-tense (also "he/she/it eats" mood)
- *comen / coman* — they eat / mood-tense
- *querer* — to want
- *quiero* — I want
- *que* — that (linker, no accent)
- *esperar* — to wait / to hope
- *esperes / espere* — you wait / he waits (mood tense)

## Examples / Sentences
- *Habla → hable.* — Mood-tense swap for "speaks."
- *Hablan → hablen.*
- *Hablo → hable.* (Going from -ar to -er/-ir keeps the *o* if we changed o for o, so we don't — *hablo → hable*.)
- *Hablamos → hablemos.* — We speak (mood).
- *Como → coma.* / *comen → coman.* — Mood swaps for "eat."
- *Quiero que esperes.* — I want you to wait. (Literally: I want that you wait.)
- *Quiero que lo esperes.* — I want you to wait for him / for it.
- *¿Quieres que te espere?* — Do you want me to wait for you?
- *¿Quieres que yo te espere?* — Do you want ME to wait for you? (clarified)
