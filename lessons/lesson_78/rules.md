# Lesson 78

## Summary
After certain verbs (*seguir*, *empezar/comenzar*, *terminar*) Spanish picks the *to* form or the *-ing* form — and the choice changes the meaning a little. Plus more mood-tense practice in those slots.

## Rules
1. **After *seguir* use the *-ing* form** to mean "continue doing X". *Sigo hablando* = I continue speaking.
2. *Sigue hablando* = he/she/you (formal) continues speaking.
3. *Es probable que siga hablando* = it's probable that he continues speaking. (Mood tense after *es probable que*.)
4. **English "he'll probably X" ≈ Spanish "es probable que X-mood"**.
5. *Ser* in -ing form = *siendo*.
6. *Gratis* = free (no money). Source of English "gratuitous" = unnecessary, extra.
7. *Es imperativo que siga siendo gratis* = it's imperative that it continues being free.
8. **Two ways to say "to start"**: *comenzar* (think "commence") and *empezar* — *empezar* is more common, both work.
9. **Verbs needing a preposition to connect to the *to* form**: most verbs of movement (*ir*, *volver*, *salir*) need *a*. *Empezar* and *comenzar* also need *a*. Examples: *empezar a hablar* = to start to speak.
10. *Es espectacular que comiencen a hablar español* = it's spectacular that they begin to speak Spanish.
11. **Empezar/comenzar + -ing** = to start *out* doing X (right from the very beginning). *Es espectacular que comiencen hablando español* = it's spectacular that they start out speaking Spanish.
12. In English the difference between "begin to speak" and "begin speaking" is tiny; in Spanish, the *-ing* version really feels like "from the very beginning".
13. *Terminar* = to finish / to terminate.
14. **Terminar + de + *to* form** = to finish doing X. *Terminar de vender* = to finish selling.
15. **Terminar + -ing form** = to *end up* doing X. *Terminar vendiéndolos* = to end up selling them.
16. The fact that both *empezar a* and *terminar de* take a preposition is a clue that they also take an *-ing* form afterward with a slightly different meaning.
17. *Cuando + future* still triggers mood tense: *cuando terminen* = when (in future) you finish.
18. To internalize the mood tense, listen — chat online is great because the spelled-out vowels (*-e* vs *-a*) jump off the page.

## Vocabulary
- *seguir* — to follow / continue
- *sigo / sigue* — I continue / he continues
- *es probable que* — it's probable that
- *ser* — to be (characteristics)
- *siendo* — being
- *gratis* — free (no cost)
- *gratuitous* (English) — from *gratis*: unnecessary, extra
- *imperativo* — imperative
- *comenzar* — to start (commence)
- *empezar* — to start
- *empezar a + verb* — to start to (do)
- *comenzar a + verb* — to begin to (do)
- *empezar/comenzar + -ing* — to start out (doing)
- *espectacular* — spectacular
- *terminar* — to finish / terminate
- *terminar de + verb* — to finish (doing)
- *terminar + -ing* — to end up (doing)
- *vender* — to sell
- *vendiéndolos* — selling them (gerund + lo)
- *conmigo* — with me
- *hablen* — (mood) they speak

## Examples / Sentences
- *Sigo hablando.* — I continue speaking.
- *Sigue hablando.* — He continues speaking.
- *Es probable que siga hablando.* — It's probable that he continues speaking.
- *Es imperativo que siga siendo gratis.* — It's imperative that it continues being free.
- *Es espectacular que comiencen a hablar español.* — It's spectacular that they begin to speak Spanish.
- *Es espectacular que comiencen hablando español.* — It's spectacular that they start out speaking Spanish.
- *Quiero que hablen conmigo cuando terminen de venderlos.* — I want you (guys) to speak with me when you finish selling them.
- *Quiero que hablen conmigo cuando terminen vendiéndolos.* — I want you (guys) to speak with me when you end up selling them.
