# Lesson 42

## Summary
We practice the conditional ("would") more, and meet *gustar* — the famous "to be pleasing" verb that flips your sentence around. *Me gusta* = "it pleases me," not "I like."

## Rules
1. The conditional key sound is *-ía*: add it to the full verb to get "would." This *-ía* covers I, he, she, it, you (formal) — I jumps in with that group.
2. *Hablar* → *hablaría* (I would speak), *hablaríamos* (we would speak), *hablarían* (they/you guys would speak), *hablarías* (you informal would).
3. *No hablarían* = they wouldn't speak.
4. *Gustar* = to like — but BETTER thought of as "to be pleasing" or "to please." Whenever you want to say "I like X," in Spanish you say "X is pleasing TO ME."
5. *Gusta* = it pleases / it is pleasing. *Me gusta* = "it pleases me" = I like it.
6. WARNING: don't say *me gusto* — that means "I please myself / I like myself." (Common learner error!)
7. To say "I like THEM," think literally: "they please me" → *Me gustan* (the verb agrees with the THING that pleases, not with "me").
8. The "Spanglish step": before saying it, mentally translate "I like X" into "X is pleasing to me" — then build it. This keeps you in control.
9. Conditional of *gustar* = *gustaría* (it would please). *Me gustaría* = I would like (literally "it would please me").
10. *Me gustaría comer ahora* = I would like to eat now.
11. For "we would like": think "it would please US" first, not the plural verb. Start with the verb (*gustaría*), THEN attach *nos*: *Nos gustaría comer ahora*. Don't get pulled into making *gustarían* — that would mean "they would please."
12. Rule of thumb: ALWAYS start with the verb. When you're in control of the verb, you're in control of the sentence.
13. *Me gustaría verte* = I would like to see you.
14. Some common verbs CONTRACT when adding future/conditional endings — they lose a letter to make pronunciation easier.
15. *Poder* (to be able / can) → expected *podería*, but the /e/ falls away after years of speaking → *podría* = I/he/she/you (formal) could. Common verbs are more likely to contract.
16. Build all forms of *podría*: *podrías* (you informal), *podríamos* (we), *podrían* (they/you guys).
17. *Venir* = to come. *Mi casa* = my house. *¿Podrías venir a mi casa?* = Could you come to my house? Note: when one verb follows another, the second stays in the "to" form (*venir*).
18. *Decir* = to say or tell. *¿Me podrías decir algo?* OR *¿Podrías decirme algo?* = Could you tell me something? (The little word can go before the changed verb or stuck on the "to" form.)
19. Reminder: *si* (no accent) = if; *sí* (with accent) = yes. *¿Podrías decirme si quieres venir?* = Could you tell me if you want to come?

## Vocabulary
- *hablar* — to speak
- *hablaría, hablaríamos, hablarían, hablarías* — would speak (I/we/they/you)
- *gustar* — to be pleasing (to like)
- *gusta, gustan* — it pleases / they please
- *me gusta, me gustan* — I like it / I like them
- *me gustaría* — I would like
- *nos gustaría* — we would like
- *poder* — to be able, can
- *podría, podrías, podríamos, podrían* — I/you/we/they could
- *ver* — to see
- *verte* — to see you
- *venir* — to come
- *mi casa* — my house
- *decir* — to say, to tell
- *algo* — something
- *si* — if
- *sí* — yes

## Examples / Sentences
- *Hablaría.* — I would speak.
- *Hablaríamos.* — We would speak.
- *Hablarían.* — They/you guys would speak.
- *No hablarían.* — They wouldn't speak.
- *Gusta.* — It pleases / it's pleasing.
- *Me gusta.* — I like it. (literally: it pleases me)
- *Me gustan.* — I like them. (they please me)
- *Me gustaría.* — I would like. (it would please me)
- *Me gustaría comer ahora.* — I would like to eat now.
- *Nos gustaría comer ahora.* — We would like to eat now.
- *Me gustaría verte.* — I would like to see you.
- *Podría.* — I/he/she could.
- *Podrías.* — You (informal) could.
- *Podríamos.* — We could.
- *Podrían.* — They/you guys could.
- *¿Podrías venir a mi casa?* — Could you come to my house?
- *¿Me podrías decir algo?* / *¿Podrías decirme algo?* — Could you tell me something?
- *¿Podrías decirme si quieres venir?* — Could you tell me if you want to come?
