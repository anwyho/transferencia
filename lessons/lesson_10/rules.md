# Lesson 10

## Summary
Verbs in Spanish are the words that end in *-r* (the "to" form). To say "I do it," chop off the *-ar/-er/-ir* ending and put *-o*, and shift the stress to the second-to-last syllable. We also meet the *tener* ("-tain") family of verbs.

## Rules
1. Every "to" form of a Spanish verb ends in *r*. It will be one of three flavors: *-ar*, *-er*, or *-ir* (e.g. *separar*, *saber*, *venir*).
2. The *-r* ending is the easy way to spot a verb. Even "is" or "to be" is a verb, because in English you can put "to" in front of it.
3. To make the "I" form of a regular verb: chop off *-ar/-er/-ir* and add *-o* (e.g. *intentar* → *intento*, *donar* → *dono*, *crear* → *creo*, *comer* → *como*).
4. In the present tense, the stress (the "loud part") goes on the second-to-last syllable: *inTENto*, not *intenTO*. If you stress the last *-o*, you accidentally say "he tried" (past tense — we'll learn that later).
5. The *"to"* form has no tense at all — it is just the name of the verb.
6. Some verbs are irregular and won't follow rule 3. We already know one: *voy* (I am going) doesn't end in *-o*.
7. With a future-time word like *mañana* (tomorrow), Spanish is happy to use the plain present — "I eat tomorrow" is perfect Spanish (e.g. *Como mañana* = I'm eating tomorrow).
8. *Voy a* + "to"-form means "I'm going to ___" (e.g. *Voy a comer* = I'm going to eat).
9. *Más* means "more" (related to English *massive*), so *más tarde* = "later" (literally "more late").
10. *Tener* (to have) is special: it also translates as "-tain" inside other verbs. Swap the English *-tain* for Spanish *-tener* (e.g. contain → *contener*, obtain → *obtener*, maintain → *mantener*, sustain → *sostener* — note *sus-* becomes *sos-*; entertain → *entretener*).
11. *Tener* is irregular in the I-form: it should be *teno* but it pops a *g* in and becomes *tengo*.
12. *Venir* (to come) follows the same *-go* trick as *tener*: I come = *vengo*. Spanish irregular verbs come in little families that all break the same way.
13. Spanish hints at a "symbiotic" view of having: when you own something, you also belong to it (you must maintain it, sustain it, contain it).
14. The Spanish *z* is pronounced /th/ in Spain (e.g. *organizo* sounds like /organitho/).

## Vocabulary
- *ventilar* — to ventilate
- *separar* — to separate
- *saber* — to know (savvy); written with a *b*
- *venir* — to come (think "convenient" = "with-coming")
- *quiero* — I want
- *debo* — I owe / I must
- *intentar* — to try; *intento* — I try
- *voy* — I am going (irregular)
- *segregar* — to segregate
- *simular* — to simulate
- *revelar* — to reveal
- *respirar* — to breathe (respiration)
- *donar* — to donate; *dono* — I donate
- *crear* — to create; *creo* — I create / I believe
- *comer* — to eat; *como* — I eat / how / like
- *tarde* — late (English slang "tardy")
- *mañana* — tomorrow / morning
- *con* — with (from *confirmar* = "with-signature")
- *firma* — signature
- *organizar* — to organize; *organizo* — I organize
- *tener* — to have / -tain
- *tenerlo* — to have it
- *más* — more (related to "massive")
- *contener* — to contain
- *obtener* — to obtain
- *sostener* — to sustain
- *mantener* — to maintain
- *entretener* — to entertain
- *entre* — between (e.g. *entre tú y yo* = between you and I)
- *tengo* — I have (irregular, has a *g*)
- *vengo* — I come (irregular, has a *g*)

## Examples / Sentences
- *Quiero* — I want
- *Debo* — I must / I owe
- *Voy a comer* — I'm going to eat
- *Voy a comer tarde* — I'm going to eat late
- *Voy a comer mañana* — I'm going to eat tomorrow
- *Como mañana* — I'm eating tomorrow (literally "I eat tomorrow")
- *Como con Pablo mañana* — I'm eating with Pablo tomorrow
- *Voy a comer con Pablo mañana* — I'm going to eat with Pablo tomorrow
- *No dono* — I don't donate / I'm not donating
- *Quiero tenerlo* — I want to have it
- *Voy a tenerlo* — I'm going to have it
- *Voy a tenerlo más tarde* — I'm going to have it later
- *Vengo mañana* — I'm coming tomorrow
