# Lesson 33

## Summary
More adjectives that change meaning with *ser* vs *estar* — *listo* (ready/smart), *bueno* (good/attractive), *feliz* (happy) — and the key idea that Spanish forces you to think "characteristic vs state" in a way English doesn't.

## Rules
1. Some adjectives go ONLY with *estar*, some go ONLY with *ser*, and some can go with either and change meaning. *Aburrido* (lesson 32) is one. *Listo* is another.
2. *Listo* with *estar* = ready (state). *Listo* with *ser* = smart / clever (characteristic).
   - *Estoy listo / lista* — I'm ready.
   - *Soy listo / lista* — I am smart.
   - *Es listo / lista* — He / she is smart.
3. To make a "we are ready" emphatic, add *nosotros*: *nosotros estamos listos*. Adding the pronoun gives it more emphasis.
4. Memory trigger: if you forget *eres* (you-informal-are), think of *¿De dónde eres?* — that sentence is everywhere.
5. *Tan* = "so". *T-a-n*. Use it like English "so": *estoy tan cansado* (I am so tired), *estoy tan aburrido* (I'm so bored), *es tan aburrido* (it is so boring).
6. *Bueno* = good (we saw it before in *bon appétit* — the French *o* splits to *ue* in Spanish). With people, the *ser/estar* swap shifts the meaning:
   - *Es bueno* (with *ser*) = he is a good person OR he is good at something.
   - *Está bueno* (with *estar*) = he is attractive / in good form / looks hot.
7. *Son buenos* = "they are good people" (or good at something).
8. Don't view *ser/estar* as an obstacle — it's a tool English doesn't give us. Sometimes English forces a single "is" when Spanish would let you nail the nuance.
9. *Quiero saber* = "I want to know". Combine with *cómo*:
   - *Quiero saber cómo está* — I want to know how he is doing (state).
   - *Quiero saber cómo es* — I want to know what he's like (characteristic).
10. *Feliz* = happy. You can guess it from English *felicity* / *felicidad* (happiness), or recognize *Feliz Navidad* (Happy Christmas).
11. *Feliz* can also go with either *ser* or *estar*:
    - *Estoy feliz* — I am happy (state).
    - *Soy feliz* — I am a happy person (characteristic).
12. There's no fixed rule for which adjectives flex; you have to listen and observe. When in doubt, the safe choice is *soy una persona feliz* (the persona-trick from lesson 32).

## Examples / Sentences
- *Estoy listo / lista.* — I am ready.
- *Estamos listos / listas.* — We are ready.
- *Nosotros estamos listos.* — We are ready (emphatic).
- *Soy listo / lista.* — I am smart.
- *Es listo / lista.* — He / she is smart.
- *Eres lista.* — You (informal, female) are smart.
- *Eres muy lista.* — You are very smart.
- *Estoy tan cansado / cansada.* — I am so tired.
- *Estoy tan aburrida.* — I am so bored.
- *Él está tan aburrido.* — He is so bored.
- *Es tan aburrido.* — It is so boring.
- *Son buenos.* — They are good (people / at something).
- *Es bueno.* — He is good (person / at something).
- *Está bueno.* — He is attractive / in good form.
- *Quiero saber cómo está.* — I want to know how he's doing.
- *Quiero saber cómo es.* — I want to know what he's like.
- *Estoy feliz.* — I am happy (state).
- *Soy feliz.* — I am a happy person.
- *Soy una persona feliz.* — I am a happy person.
