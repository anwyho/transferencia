# Lesson 03

## Summary
More conversion rules: English `-ant`/`-ent` → Spanish `-ante`/`-ente`, English `-ly` → Spanish `-mente`, and the trick that English `-able`/`-ible` words usually work as-is. Also: how the English /j/ sound becomes a throaty /kh/ in Spanish.

## Rules
1. **English `-ant` or `-ent` → Spanish, just stick an `-e` on the end.** *important → importante, different → diferente, constant → constante.*
2. **The stress lands on the second-to-last syllable** in these `-ante`/`-ente` words: im-por-TAN-te. Just observe where the stress falls — don't memorize.
3. **English `-ly` → Spanish `-mente`** (one word, glued on). *constantly → constantemente, legally → legalmente, normally → normalmente.* Means "in this manner."
4. **An *r* at the start of a word** is rolled, like a little engine: *real, realmente.*
5. ***Real* in Spanish also means "royal."** *Real Madrid* = "Royal Madrid," not "real Madrid."
6. ***Normalmente* doubles as "usually."** Like English "normally I do X" = "usually I do X."
7. **English /j/ sound → Spanish /kh/ (throaty, friction in the throat).** This is *not* the letter g — it's the *sound*. *general → general* (pronounced /khe-ne-RAL/), *original → original* (/o-ri-khi-NAL/), *digital → digital* (/di-khi-TAL/). Memory hook: *Argentina* — that's the sound.
8. **English `-ible` / `-able` words** often work in Spanish: just say them syllable by syllable with stress on the second-to-last syllable. *possible → posible, probable → probable, flexible → flexible.*
9. **Don't push the rule too hard.** "Doable" wouldn't convert because the -able isn't really part of a Latin root. Some rules have exceptions — feel free to experiment, feel free to be wrong. That's how you find the words that work.

## Vocabulary
- *importante* — important
- *diferente* — different
- *constante* — constant
- *constantemente* — constantly
- *legalmente* — legally
- *ilegalmente* — illegally
- *real* — real / royal
- *realmente* — really
- *normalmente* — normally / usually
- *global* — global

- *original* — original

- *digital* — digital

## Examples / Sentences
- *Es importante.* — It's important.
- *No es importante.* — It's not important.
- *No es diferente.* — It's not different.
- *Es constante.* — It is constant.
- *Normalmente es importante.* — Normally / usually it's important.
- *Naturalmente es original.* — Naturally / obviously it's original.
- *Posiblemente es original.* — Possibly it's original.
