# Lesson 50

## Summary
Spanish has TWO words for "for": *para* (something is for somebody / destined for) and *por* (because of, in exchange for, periods of time). We hook this onto *gracias por venir* (thanks for coming) and meet *me gustaría* as the hook for "would".

## Rules
1. Spanish has TWO words for "for": *para* and *por*. You have to think about which meaning you want.
2. *Para* = "for" in the sense that something is destined for somebody. *Para mí, para ti, para él, para ella* = for me/you/him/her.
3. *Mí* and *ti* show up after prepositions. After other prepositions too: *de mí* (from me), *de ti* (from you).
4. For *él, ella, nosotros* etc., the regular pronoun stays the same — only "me" and "you-informal" have those special after-preposition shapes.
5. For the demonstratives, you find the right form by looking at one you already know. Tonight = *esta noche* → for "this" feminine, use *esta*. Then for masculine pluck the matching: *este*. (This is the "method": look at a known anchor to find the new form.)
6. *Este es para ti* = "this one is for you" (masculine). *Estos son para ti* = "these are for you" (masc/mixed).
7. *Por* = much more flexible "for". Means "because of": *por qué* = "because of what" = why. *Porque* = because of that = because.
8. *Razón* = reason (in Spain pronounced /rathón/, elsewhere /rasón/). *Razón* is feminine. *Por esta razón, no quiero ir* = "For this reason / because of this reason, I don't want to go".
9. Compare *para* vs *por* for "for you": *Lo compro para ti* = I'm buying it for you (it's destined for you). *Lo compro por ti* = I'm buying it because of you (your influence — doesn't mean it's yours).
10. (Mnemonic) Buy an ashtray "para ti" → he'll take it home; "por ti" → just because you smoke and visit.
11. *Por* is ALSO used for periods of time: "for two weeks, for two days" → *por*, never *para*. *Me quedo por tres días* = I'm staying for three days.
12. *Día* (day) is one of those rare *-a* words that's masculine: *el día, los días*. (Connected to an Indo-European root meaning "light" — same root as deity, Jupiter.)
13. *Todos los días* = "every day" (literally "all the days"). Use *todos* (plural) not *todo*.
14. To push a verb into the simple future, ADD *-é* (or *-ás, -á, -emos, -án*) onto the FULL "to" form: *quedaré* = I will stay. *Me quedaré por tres días*.
15. The future tells the listener it's more certain. *Me quedo por tres días* (saying it as if scheduled) vs *me quedaré por tres días* (will stay — slightly more committed).
16. The hook for "would" = *me gustaría* (I would like / it would please me). Pluck the *-ía* off — that's your would-ending.
17. *Me quedaría por tres días* = I would stay for three days. *Pero tengo que trabajar* = but I have to work. (*Trabajar* — *jar* sounds like /har/.)
18. The "method" itself is half the work: when you can't recall a form, walk through what you DO know — find the anchor — derive it. After a few times it'll just stick.

## Vocabulary
- *para* — for (destined for); also "in order to"
- *por* — for (because of, in exchange for, period of time)
- *para mí / para ti / para él / para ella* — for me / you / him / her
- *de mí, de ti* — from me, from you
- *este / esto* — this (m), generic neutral "this"
- *por qué* — why (because of what)
- *porque* — because
- *razón* — reason (feminine)
- *quiero / no quiero* — I want / I don't want
- *comprar* — to buy
- *día* — day (masculine despite ending in -a)
- *los días* — the days
- *todos los días* — every day
- *tres* — three
- *me quedo / me quedaré / me quedaría* — I stay / I will stay / I would stay
- *me gustaría* — I would like (hook for "would")
- *trabajar* — to work (*jar* = /har/)
- *pero* — but
- *tengo que trabajar* — I have to work

## Examples / Sentences
- *Gracias por venir* — Thanks for coming
- *Para mí / Para ti* — For me / For you
- *Para él / Para ella* — For him / For her
- *Esta noche* — Tonight
- *Este es para ti* — This (masc) is for you
- *Estos son para ti* — These are for you
- *¿Por qué?* — Why?
- *Porque…* — Because…
- *Esta razón* — This reason
- *Por esta razón, no quiero ir* — Because of this reason, I don't want to go
- *Lo compro para ti* — I'm buying it for you
- *Lo compro por ti* — I'm buying it because of you
- *Lo he comprado por ti* — I have bought it because of you
- *El día / Los días* — The day / The days
- *Todos los días* — Every day
- *Me quedo por tres días* — I'm staying for three days
- *Me quedaré por tres días* — I will stay for three days
- *Me quedaría por tres días* — I would stay for three days
- *Me quedaría por tres días, pero tengo que trabajar* — I would stay for three days, but I have to work
