# Lesson 79

## Summary
A recap with new verb *dejar* (to leave a message), more mood-tense triggers (*es esencial que*, *ojalá*), and the story of *ojalá* hidden in Spanish from Arabic.

## Rules
1. *Dejar* = to leave (in the sense of "leave a message" — not to go out, that's *salir*).
2. *Mensaje* = message (masculine: *un mensaje*).
3. **Formal positive command + me** attaches to the verb. *Déjeme un mensaje* = leave me a message (formal).
4. **Informal "I want you to..." → mood tense.** *Quiero que me dejes un mensaje* = I want you to leave me a message.
5. **"I want to tell him to leave me a message" — but Spanish phrases it as "I want to tell him *that he leaves* me a message"**, with mood tense to convey the order. *Quiero decirle que me deje un mensaje*.
6. If you used the normal present (*me deja*) it would mean "I want to tell him that he is (currently) leaving me a message" — not an order.
7. **Cuando + future event → mood tense** (review). *Cuando te deje el mensaje, llámame* = when he leaves you the message, call me.
8. *Llámame* = call me (informal positive command + me).
9. **English "-tial / -tion" → Spanish "-cial / -ción"**: *tradition → tradición*; *essential → esencial*; *spatial → espacial*.
10. **Es esencial que + mood tense.** *Es esencial que vea mi mensaje* = it's essential that he sees my message.
11. *Vea* = mood form of *ver* (based on irregular *veo*).
12. *Ojalá* = hopefully. Comes from Arabic — likely a contraction of "ojo-Allah" ("eye-God"); Spanish was shaped by ~8 centuries of Arabic influence in southern Spain.
13. *Ojo* = eye, also used like "watch out!" / "be careful!"
14. **Ojalá + mood tense** to say what you hope happens. *Ojalá deje un mensaje* = hopefully she leaves a message.
15. *Ojalá sea pronto* = hopefully it's/will be soon (using *sea*, the irregular mood root of *ser*).
16. *Pronto* = soon.
17. The most interesting uses of the mood tense are the **optional** ones — they let you add humor, sarcasm, sincerity, irritation, etc.

## Vocabulary
- *dejar* — to leave (a thing or message)
- *salir* — to go out (different "leave")
- *mensaje (m.)* — message
- *un mensaje* — a message
- *déjeme* — leave me (formal command + me)
- *dejes / deje / dejen* — mood forms
- *decir* — to say / to tell
- *decirle* — to tell him/her/(formal)you
- *cuando* — when
- *llámame* — call me (informal command + me)
- *tradición* — tradition
- *esencial* — essential
- *espacial* — spatial
- *ver* — to see
- *veo / vea* — I see / (mood) he sees
- *ojalá* — hopefully (from Arabic ojo-Allah)
- *ojo* — eye / watch out!
- *Allah* — Arabic for God
- *sea* — (mood) be
- *pronto* — soon

## Examples / Sentences
- *Déjeme un mensaje.* — Leave me a message. (formal)
- *Quiero que me dejes un mensaje.* — I want you to leave me a message. (informal)
- *Quiero decirle que me deje un mensaje.* — I want to tell him to leave me a message.
- *Cuando te deje el mensaje, llámame.* — When he leaves you the message, call me.
- *Es esencial que vea mi mensaje.* — It's essential that he see my message.
- *Ojalá deje un mensaje.* — Hopefully she leaves a message.
- *Ojalá sea pronto.* — Hopefully it'll be soon.
