# Lesson 59

## Summary
We learn the point-in-the-past endings for *-er/-ir* verbs (*-í* for I, *-ió* for he/she). We meet *nada* (nothing), *nadie* (nobody), the double negative, *durar* (to last), *durante* (during/for), *sin* (without), and *parar* (to stop).

## Rules
1. Recap *-ar* point-in-past: I = */e/* with accent (*hablé*), he/she = */o/* with accent (*habló*).
2. Point-in-past for *-er/-ir*:
   - I = *-í* (accent on i). *Comí* = I ate.
   - he/she/it/you-formal = *-ió* (accent on o). *Comió* = he ate.
3. Both endings are accented. Stress matters.
4. *Salir* (irregular only in the present "I" form) is regular here: *salí* (I left), *salió* (he left).
5. *Qué* means "what" or "that" (the connector "that" — like "I hope that..."). Use *espero que* = "I hope that".
6. *Que* also means "than" for comparisons: *Comí más que él* = "I ate more than him". Note: in Spanish use *él* / *yo* (he / I) — NOT *mí*. English uses "him / me" but Spanish stays literal: *más que él*, *más que yo*.
7. Cut the English habit of using "me" for "I". When asked "Who wants to go?" don't say "Me!" — it should be "I!". This bad habit can leak into your Spanish.
8. *Ver* (to see) is regular here: *vi* (I saw), *vio* (he saw). Use *comer* as your hook.
9. When contrasting (I vs he), you may want to throw in *yo* / *él* even though you don't have to — context already tells. *Lo vi, pero no me vio* (I saw him but he didn't see me).
10. *Nada* = nothing. Spanish uses DOUBLE NEGATIVES happily. *No vi nada* = "I didn't see nothing" = I didn't see anything.
11. *Nadie* = nobody. *Nadie vio nada* = "Nobody saw nothing" = nobody saw anything.
12. *Durar* = to last (English "endure", "duration"). Mnemonic: Duracell batteries.
13. *Durante* = during (also = "for" with time). Made like an *-ing* adjective with *-ante/-iente*. *Comió durante media hora* = "He ate during half an hour" = "He ate for half an hour".
14. With time you can use *por* OR *durante* for "for": *por dos minutos* / *durante dos minutos*.
15. *Sin* = without. After prepositions, use the unchanged ("to") form, NOT the *-ing* form: *sin parar* = "without stopping" (literally "without to stop").
16. *Parar* = to stop. Mnemonic city: *par* in Spanish means "pair". *Un par* = a pair. *Parar* might be "the pairing" (two things meeting and stopping).
17. Using the "pair" mnemonic for word-family:
    - *comparar* (to compare) = "with-pairing" (the *n* of *con* softens to *m*).
    - *preparar* (to prepare) = "pre-pairing".
    - *reparar* (to repair) = "re-pair".
    - *separar* (to separate) = "pair apart" (*se-* meaning apart).

## Examples / Sentences
- *Comí. / Comió.* — I ate. / He ate.
- *Salí. / Salió.* — I left. / He left.
- *No salió todavía.* — She didn't leave yet.
- *No sé por qué no salió todavía.* — I don't know why she didn't leave yet.
- *Comí más que él.* — I ate more than he (did).
- *Comió más que yo.* — He ate more than I (did).
- *Vi. / Vio. / Lo vi.* — I saw. / He saw. / I saw it.
- *No lo vi.* — I didn't see it / him.
- *Lo vi, pero no me vio.* — I saw him, but he didn't see me.
- *No vi nada.* — I didn't see anything.
- *No comí nada.* — I didn't eat anything.
- *¿No comió nada?* — Didn't you (formal) eat anything?
- *¿Por qué no comió nada?* — Why didn't you eat anything?
- *Nadie vio nada.* — Nobody saw anything.
- *Duró dos minutos. / Duré dos minutos.* — It lasted two minutes / I lasted two minutes.
- *Durante dos minutos.* — During / for two minutes.
- *Comió durante media hora.* — He ate for half an hour.
- *Habló durante media hora.* — He spoke for half an hour.
- *Habló durante media hora sin parar.* — He spoke for half an hour without stopping.
- *Un par de zapatos.* — A pair of shoes.
