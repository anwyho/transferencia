# Lesson 62

## Summary
We learn how to give it to him / her / them — and the trick that "le lo" never works in Spanish (Spanish hates two L sounds together), so it becomes *se lo*. We also meet *dar* (to give), which weirdly behaves like an *-er/-ir* verb in the past.

## Rules
1. *Dar* = to give. It's the ONLY verb that behaves weirdly in the point-in-past — although it's *-ar*, it uses *-er/-ir* endings.
2. *Dar* point-in-past:
   - I gave = *di* (no accent — short word).
   - he/she/it/you-formal gave = *dio* (no accent on this either; one syllable).
   - we gave = *dimos*.
3. To say "I gave it" = *Lo di*. "He gave me it" = *Me lo dio*. "He gave us it" = *Nos lo dio*. "He didn't give it to us" = *No nos lo dio*.
4. Building rule (intuition trap): if you want *Te lo di* (I gave you it), the FIRST thought must be "I gave" (the verb form). Don't try to assemble all the bits; build the verb first, the rest flows.
5. The big rule: Spanish HATES two /l/ sounds together. *Le lo* sounds like "le-lo" — Spanish doesn't like it. So *le lo*, *les lo*, *le los*, *les los* etc. all become *se lo* / *se los* / etc.
6. This *se* has NO meaning change — it's purely a sound fix. Different *se* from "himself / herself".
7. So "I gave it to him" = *Se lo di* (would be "le lo di"). "I gave it to them" = *Se lo di* too. Ambiguous!
8. Clarify with *a + person*: *Se lo di a él* / *a ella* / *a usted* / *a ellos* / *a ellas*. The *a*-clarification is added at the end like an afterthought.
9. With this clarifying *a*, you ALWAYS repeat the little word. *Le di algo a él* (you keep *le* even though you said *a él*).
10. This is different from *para* / other prepositions, where you DON'T repeat. *Me lo compra* / *Lo compra para mí* — no repetition.
11. Why? The *a* is special — it can mean *to*, but also flexibly stand in for other prepositions. *Me lo compra a mí* could mean "from me" or "for me" (literally "to me"). Because of this flexibility, repetition is needed.
12. *Vender* = to sell. *Vendí* = I sold. *Lo vendí* = I sold it. *Se lo vendí* = I sold it to him (would be "le lo vendí" but the L-L crash forces *se*).
13. Object pronouns can have feminine + plural forms: *la* (her/it-fem), *las* (them-fem), *los* (them-masc/mixed). Used same way.
14. *Se las vendí a ella* = "I sold them (fem) to her". *Se los vendí a ella* = "I sold them (masc) to her". *Se las vendí a ellos* = "I sold them (fem) to them (masc/mixed)".
15. We can layer further clarifications: *Él se lo envió a ella* = "He sent it to her" (clarifying the sender too).
16. Re-cap: with the clarification-*a* you ALWAYS repeat (*se lo envié a él*, *se lo compré a ella*). With other prepositions (like *para*) you don't (*lo compré para usted*, *lo compré para ti*).
17. Don't worry about memorizing every detail — focus on UNDERSTANDING. With listening and analyzing, you'll absorb it.

## Vocabulary
- *dar* — to give
- *di / dio / dimos* — I/he/we gave
- *me lo dio* — he gave me it
- *nos lo dio* — he gave us it
- *te lo di* — I gave you it
- *se lo di* — I gave it to him/her/them/you-formal (the L-L fix)
- *vender* — to sell
- *vendí / vendió / vendimos* — I/he/we sold
- *enviar* — to send
- *envió* — he sent
- *me lo envió / nos lo envió / te lo envió / se lo envió* — he sent it to me/us/you/him etc.
- *a él / a ella / a ellos / a ellas / a usted / a ustedes* — to him / her / them (m) / them (f) / you (formal) / you guys
- *algo* — something
- *le di algo a él / a ella* — I gave something to him/her
- *los / las* — them (masc/fem)
- *compré / compra* — I bought / he buys
- *para mí / para ti / para usted* — for me / you / you-formal
- *todavía* — yet
- *no sé si* — I don't know if

## Examples / Sentences
- *Di. / Dio. / Dimos.* — I gave / he gave / we gave.
- *Me dio. / Me lo dio.* — He gave me. / He gave me it.
- *Nos lo dio. / No nos lo dio.* — He gave us it. / He didn't give us it.
- *Te lo di.* — I gave you it (informal).
- *Se lo di.* — I gave it to him/her/them/you-formal.
- *Se los di.* — I gave them to him/her/them/you-formal.
- *Le di algo. / Le di algo a él. / Le di algo a ella. / Le di algo a usted.* — I gave him/her/you-formal something.
- *Lo vendí. / Se lo vendí. / Se lo vendí a él. / Se lo vendí a ella.* — I sold it. / I sold it to him/her.
- *Se los vendí a ella.* — I sold them (masc) to her.
- *Se las vendí a ella.* — I sold them (fem) to her.
- *Se las vendí a ellos.* — I sold them (fem) to them (masc).
- *Me lo envió.* — He sent it to me.
- *Nos lo envió.* — He sent it to us.
- *Te lo envió.* — He sent it to you.
- *Se lo envió. / Se lo envió a ella. / Él se lo envió a ella.* — He sent it to her (with clarifications).
- *Se lo envió a ellos.* — He sent it to them.
- *Se lo envió a ustedes.* — He sent it to you guys.
- *No se lo envió a ustedes todavía.* — He didn't send it to you guys yet.
- *No sé si se lo envié.* — I don't know if I sent it to him.
- *Lo compré para usted. / Lo compré para ti.* — I bought it for you (formal/informal).
