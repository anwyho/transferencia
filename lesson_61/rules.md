# Lesson 61

## Summary
We learn how the "line in the past" (was -ing) and the "point in the past" (one shot) work together in one sentence — like "I was cooking when the phone rang". We also see *gustar* (to please) and *parecer* (to seem), which take a bit of mind-flipping.

## Rules
1. The line and the point in the past go together. *I was cooking* = the LINE (ongoing). *The phone rang* = the POINT (one shot).
2. Build it: line first uses *-aba* / *-ía*; point uses *-é/-ó* or *-í/-ió*.
3. *Cocinar* = to cook. *Cocinaba* = I was cooking.
4. *El teléfono* = the phone. From Greek *tele-* (far/distant) + *phone* (voice) = "the distant voice".
5. *Sonar* = to sound. So instead of "the phone rang", Spanish says "the phone sounded": *El teléfono sonó*.
6. Word order is flexible: *Cocinaba cuando el teléfono sonó* / *Cocinaba cuando sonó el teléfono*. Verb-then-subject is often more natural in Spanish.
7. *Llegar* = to arrive. Spelled *l-l-e-g-a-r*; the *ll* sounds like an English /y/.
8. Past forms of *llegar*: *llegué* (I arrived), *llegó* (he arrived), *llegamos* (we arrived).
9. "On the phone" in Spanish = *por teléfono* ("by phone"). *Por* can mean "by".
10. *Quedarse* = to stay. The dictionary form has *-se*; you must turn that *se* into the right pronoun: *me quedé* (I stayed), *se quedó* (he stayed), *nos quedamos* (we stayed).
11. *Gustar* = "to please" — NOT "to like". You must mind-flip: where English says "I like it", Spanish says "It pleases me" → *Me gusta*.
12. So *Me gusta* = "It pleases me" = "I like it". *Me gustó* = "It pleased me" = "I liked it".
13. Don't add *lo*. There is no "it" — *gusta* already means "it pleases".
14. Negative: *No me gustó* = "I didn't like it" / "It didn't please me".
15. Line-in-past with *gustar*: *No me gustaba* = "It wasn't pleasing me / I wasn't liking it" — used when you mean an ongoing feeling. Often interchangeable with *no me gustó*; the difference is in your mental framing (event vs. ongoing).
16. *Parecer* = to seem. Related to "apparent", "appear" (*aparecer*), "transparent".
17. *Me parece* = "It seems to me". *Me parece bien* = "It seems good (well) to me".
18. *Me pareció bien* = "It seemed good to me" (point in past).
19. *Me parecía bien* = "It was seeming good to me" (line in past) — useful even though English doesn't usually say it that way; in Spanish you choose based on point-vs-line meaning.
20. Point and line interact in real sentences: *Me parecía bien, pero cuando lo vi, no me gustó* = "It was seeming fine, BUT when I saw it (point), I didn't like it (point)".
21. With these mind-flip verbs, focus on the structure (point or line?) not the literal English translation.

## Vocabulary
- *llamar* — to call (link to: phone, name, call myself)
- *cocinar* — to cook
- *cocino / cocinaba* — I cook / I was cooking
- *cuando* — when
- *el teléfono* — the phone (distant voice)
- *sonar* — to sound (= "ring")
- *sonó* — it sounded / rang
- *llegar* — to arrive (-ar verb, double l)
- *llegué / llegó / llegamos* — I/he/we arrived
- *por teléfono* — by phone
- *por* — by / for / through
- *hablaba* — I was talking (line past of *hablar*)
- *quedarse* — to stay
- *me quedé / se quedó / nos quedamos* — I/he/we stayed
- *gustar* — to please (= English "to like" flipped)
- *me gusta / me gustó / me gustaba* — it pleases me / pleased me / was pleasing me
- *no me gusta / gustó / gustaba* — I don't / didn't like / wasn't liking
- *parecer* — to seem
- *aparecer* — to appear
- *me parece / me pareció / me parecía* — it seems / seemed / was seeming to me
- *bien* — well / good
- *pero* — but
- *cuando lo vi* — when I saw it
- *María* — Maria

## Examples / Sentences
- *Cocinaba cuando el teléfono sonó. / Cocinaba cuando sonó el teléfono.* — I was cooking when the phone rang.
- *Hablaba por teléfono cuando María llegó. / Hablaba por teléfono cuando llegó María.* — I was speaking on the phone when Maria arrived.
- *Me quedé. / Se quedó. / Nos quedamos.* — I/he/we stayed.
- *Me gusta.* — I like it (it pleases me).
- *Me gustó.* — I liked it.
- *No me gustó.* — I didn't like it.
- *No me gustaba.* — I wasn't liking it / It wasn't pleasing me.
- *Me parece. / Me parece bien.* — It seems (good) to me.
- *Me pareció bien.* — It seemed good to me.
- *Me parecía bien.* — It was seeming good to me.
- *Me parecía bien, pero cuando lo vi, no me gustó.* — It was seeming good, but when I saw it, I didn't like it.
