# Lesson 87

## Summary
We tidy up the past tenses. The line-in-the-past is super regular — only *ser* (era), *ir* (iba), and *ver* (veía) are irregular. The point-in-the-past has a bigger irregular family: their root changes, they mix endings from *-ar* and *-er/-ir*, and the endings don't take the accent.

## Rules
1. **Line-in-the-past** is the safest, most regular tense. Just take off *-ar* and add *-aba*, or take off *-er/-ir* and add *-ía*. Only three irregulars worth memorizing: *ser*, *ir*, *ver*.
2. *Ser* line-in-the-past: *era, eras, era, éramos, eran*. The accent stays on the first *e* — it doesn't move (*éramos*, not *eramós*). Mnemonic: think English "an era" → *era*.
3. *Ir* line-in-the-past: *iba, ibas, iba, íbamos, iban*. It's a mix of the *-aba* sound (from *-ar* verbs) and the *-ía* sound (because *ir* is *-ir*) — but it landed on *-aba*.
4. *Ver* line-in-the-past: *veía, veías, veía, veíamos, veían*. Why? Because *ver* keeps an extra *e* in the *I* form (*veo*), and that extra *e* carries over: *veía* (instead of just *vía*).
5. **Point-in-the-past** is less regular. There's a group of mostly-*-go*-verbs (but not all) that share three irregularity habits:
   - **(a) Their root/base changes.** Sometimes a little, sometimes a lot. *poder → pud-* (pudieron). *tener → tuv-* (tuve). *estar → estuv-* (estuve). *decir → dij-* (dije).
   - **(b) They MIX endings from *-ar* and *-er/-ir*.** The *I* and *he/she/you-formal* endings come from *-ar* (*-e* and *-o*). The *we / you-informal / they* endings come from *-er/-ir* (*-imos*, *-iste*, *-ieron*).
   - **(c) The endings DO NOT take the written accent.** Compare regular *hablé* (accented) with irregular *tuve* (no accent). Regular *habló* vs irregular *tuvo*.
6. Quick recap of regular point-in-past endings:
   - *-ar*: *-é, -ó, -amos, -aron, -aste* (e.g. *hablé, habló, hablamos, hablaron, hablaste*)
   - *-er/-ir*: *-í, -ió, -imos, -ieron, -iste* (e.g. *comí, comió, comimos, comieron, comiste*)
7. *Tener* point-in-past: base is *tuv-*. Endings: *tuve, tuviste, tuvo, tuvimos, tuvieron*. Note *-e* and *-o* (no accent), then *-imos/-iste/-ieron*.
8. *Estar* point-in-past: base is *estuv-*. *Estuve, estuviste, estuvo, estuvimos, estuvieron*. Memory pair: *tuve / estuve* — "I had / I was" go together.
9. **Avoidance trick**: many times you can dodge the irregular point-in-past and just use the line-in-the-past. *¿Dónde estabas?* (line) instead of *¿Dónde estuviste?* (point). *Tenía que ir* (line) instead of *Tuve que ir* (point). The meaning shifts a touch — *tuve que ir* feels like one specific moment "I had to go (right then)" — but for getting the meaning across, it works.
10. Teacher's caution: don't AVOID it forever. If you don't take control of a tense, it'll control you. Use the line-in-past as a fallback in the moment, then go back and learn the irregular form properly.
11. *Decir* point-in-past: base is *dij-* (d-i-j). *Dije* (I said), *dijo* (he said), *dijimos* (we said). Same pattern: *-e/-o* (no accent) then *-imos/-iste/-ieron*.
12. Summary line: this irregular group "uses *-e* and *-o* from the *-ar* group, and the rest of the endings from the *-er/-ir* group, for the point in the past."

## Vocabulary
- *ser / era / eras / era / éramos / eran* — to be / line-in-past forms
- *ir / iba / ibas / iba / íbamos / iban* — to go / line-in-past forms
- *ver / veo / veía / veías / veía / veíamos / veían* — to see / I see / line-in-past forms
- *hablar / hablé / habló / hablamos / hablaron / hablaste* — to speak (regular point-in-past)
- *comer / comí / comió / comimos / comieron / comiste* — to eat (regular point-in-past)
- *poder / pud- / pudieron* — to be able / point-in-past base
- *tener / tuv- / tuve / tuviste / tuvo / tuvimos / tuvieron* — to have / point-in-past
- *estar / estuv- / estuve / estuviste / estuvo / estuvimos / estuvieron* — to be (states)
- *decir / dij- / dije / dijo / dijimos* — to say/tell / point-in-past
- *contar* — alternative to *decir* for "to tell"
- *dónde* — where

## Examples / Sentences
- *¿Dónde estuviste?* — Where were you? (point in past)
- *¿Dónde estuvieron?* — Where were you guys? / Where were they?
- *¿Dónde estabas?* — Where were you? (line in past — easier alternative)
- *No estuve.* — I wasn't (there).
- *Tuve que ir.* — I had to go. (specific moment)
- *Tenía que ir.* — I had to go / I needed to go. (line-in-past, regular)
