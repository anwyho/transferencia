# Lesson 48

## Summary
Now we get the line-past for *-er/-ir* verbs (chop, add *-ía*) — same sound as "would". We also see *se* in lots of useful spots: *se vende* (for sale), *¿cómo se dice?* (how do you say it?), and *me queda* (I have left).

## Rules
1. *-er/-ir* verbs in the line-past: chop the *-er/-ir* and add *-ía*. *Comía* = I was eating / used to eat. *Vendía* = I/he/she was selling.
2. The *-ía* ending sounds the same as the "would" ending! The difference: for "would" we ADD *-ía* to the full "to" form (*comer* → *comería* = I would eat); for the past we CHOP the ending first (*comer* → *comía* = I was eating).
3. So you get pairs: *comería* (would eat) vs *comía* (was eating); *rompería* (would break) vs *rompía* (was breaking).
4. Same endings as *-aba* but with *-ía*: *-ía, -ías, -ían, -íamos*. (*Comía, comías, comían, comíamos*.)
5. *Se vende* = "it sells itself" = "for sale". You see it on houses and cars in Spanish-speaking places. Same idea: *se vendía* = it was for sale; *se vendería* = it would be for sale.
6. To say "let's go" or "we are going" from *ir* (to go) is *vamos*. To say "it's going to break" = *se va a romper* or *va a romperse*.
7. *¿Cómo se dice?* literally = "how does it say itself?" = "how do you say…?" This is how you ask about a word in Spanish. Don't say *¿cómo dices?* — that means "how do YOU personally say…?".
8. *Decir* = to say. Slightly irregular: *dice* (he/she/it/you-formal says), *dices* (you say), *dicen* (they say), *decimos* (we say — accent moves so we go back to *decir* form).
9. The "we" form often escapes irregularity because the accent lands somewhere else.
10. *Se* does many jobs: (a) wraps up the sentence when no object follows (it breaks, it transforms), (b) changes meaning between verb pairs (*quedar* = to remain vs *quedarse* = to stay), (c) creative uses (*se vende* = for sale, *se habla español* = Spanish is spoken).
11. *Quedar* alone = to remain / be left over. Use *me queda X* to mean "I have X left" (literally "X remains to me").
12. *Me queda uno* = "I have one left". *Me quedan dos* = "I have two left" (verb agrees with the thing remaining: it/they remain to me).
13. Switch the indirect object pronoun for who has it left: *le queda dos* = he/she/you-formal has two left; *no me quedan / no me queda* = "I don't have any left" (both versions are heard).
14. *Quedarse* (with reflexive *se*) = to stay. *Me quedo* = I stay. *Nos quedamos* = we stay. *Se queda* = he/she stays. *Se quedan* = they stay.
15. ALL the *l-* words (*lo, la, los, las*) become *se* in the "self" version. So *quedan* → *se quedan*, not *los quedan*.
16. *Hablaba* (I was speaking) and other *-aba* forms work the same: *no se quedaban aquí* = they weren't staying here. *Nos quedábamos* = we were staying (accent stays on *-aba*).

## Vocabulary
- *comer* / *comía* / *comería* — to eat / I was eating / I would eat
- *vender* — to sell
- *se vende* — for sale (it sells itself)
- *romper* / *rompía* / *rompería* — to break / was breaking / would break
- *ir* — to go
- *vamos* — we go / let's go
- *va a romperse / se va a romper* — it's going to break
- *cómo* — how
- *decir* — to say (*dice, dices, dicen, decimos*)
- *¿Cómo se dice?* — How do you say it? (How does it say itself?)
- *quedar* — to remain, be left over
- *quedarse* — to stay (reflexive)
- *uno / una* — one (m/f)
- *dos* — two (related to "duo")
- *aquí* — here
- *español* — Spanish
- *se habla español* — Spanish is spoken / Spanish spoken

## Examples / Sentences
- *Comería* — I would eat
- *Comía* — I was eating
- *Lo vendería* — I would sell it
- *Lo vendía* — I was selling it
- *Se vende* — for sale
- *Se vendía* — it was for sale
- *Se vendería* — it would be for sale
- *Lo rompía* — I/he was breaking it
- *Se rompía* — it was breaking
- *Se rompían* — they were breaking
- *Lo rompería* — I would break it
- *Lo romperían* — they would break it
- *Se rompe* — it breaks
- *Se va a romper* — it's going to break
- *¿Cómo se dice?* — How do you say it?
- *Decimos* — we say
- *Se habla español* — Spanish is spoken
- *Me queda uno* — I have one left
- *Me quedan dos* — I have two left
- *Le queda dos* — he/she has two left
- *No me queda(n)* — I don't have any left
- *Me quedo* — I stay
- *Nos quedamos* — we stay
- *Se queda* — he/she stays
- *Se quedan* — they stay
- *No se quedaban aquí* — they weren't staying here
- *No me quedaba* — I wasn't staying
- *Nos quedábamos aquí* — we were staying here
- *No nos queda(n)* — we don't have any left
