# Lesson 24

## Summary
We learn the verb *dar* (to give) and stack two little words together — like *te lo doy mañana* ("I'll give it to you tomorrow"). We also meet *ustedes* (you guys, plural).

## Rules
1. **The verb *dar* (to give)** is short — just *d-a-r*.
2. **"I give" = *doy*** — like English "boy" with /d/. Mnemonic: *dar* + *-oy* (same *-oy* ending we saw in *voy* and *soy*).
3. **Full *dar* set**: *doy* (I), *das* (you informal), *da* (he/she/it/you formal), *damos* (we), *dan* (they/you guys).
4. **Two little words together — order**: when stacking, the **personal one comes first**, then the thing.
   - *me lo dan* = they give it to me. (*me* before *lo*.)
   - *te lo doy* = I give it to you.
5. **Spanish skips "to" / "for" a lot**: in English we say "give it TO me" or "buy it FOR me". In Spanish, the little word *me* already carries that meaning — no extra *a* or *para* needed.
6. **Future via context — drill**: *te lo doy mañana* = "I'll give it to you tomorrow" (literally "I give you it tomorrow"). The word *mañana* makes it future; the verb stays present.
7. ***Algo* = "something"**.
8. **Word order is flexible for emphasis**: subject pronouns *yo, tú, él, ella, usted, ustedes* can sit at the start, the end, or in the middle. *¿Usted me lo da mañana?* / *¿Me lo da usted mañana?* / *¿Me lo da mañana usted?* — all OK. Voice tone and gestures tell which is emphatic; you'll pick this up by ear.
9. ***Ustedes* = "you guys"** = plural of *usted*. Rule for plurals: if a word ends in a consonant, add *-es* (not just *-s*). So *usted → ustedes*.
10. **Object words *lo, me, te, nos* are NOT flexible** — they sit in front of the changed verb (or stuck on the end of the "to" form). Only the big subject pronouns move around.
11. **Two changed verbs together (e.g., "I want to give it to you")**: in *quiero dar* the second verb is "to" form, so the little words can either (a) stick to the end of *dar*: *quiero dártelo*, or (b) sit before the changed verb: *te lo quiero dar*.
12. **Order when stacking**: personal word first, thing second. So "to give it to you" = *te* (you) + *lo* (it) → *te lo*.
13. **Build verbs first, little words after**: when constructing a sentence, work out the verb pieces first ("I want to give"), then attach the little words ("it to you").

## Vocabulary
- *dar* — to give
- *doy* — I give
- *das* — you (informal) give
- *da* — he/she/it gives, you (formal) give
- *damos* — we give
- *dan* — they/you guys give
- *algo* — something
- *ustedes* — you guys / plural formal you
- *quiero dar* — I want to give

## Examples / Sentences
- *Doy.* — I give.
- *Lo doy.* — I give it.
- *Los doy.* — I give them.
- *Las doy.* — I give them (feminine).
- *Da.* — He/she gives.
- *Él da.* — HE gives.
- *Ella da.* — SHE gives.
- *Ellos dan.* / *Ellas dan.* — They give.
- *Das.* / *Tú das.* — You give.
- *Él me da algo.* — He's giving me something.
- *Me dan algo.* — They give me something.
- *Me lo dan.* — They give it to me.
- *Ellos me lo dan.* — THEY give it to me.
- *Te lo doy mañana.* — I'll give it to you tomorrow.
- *¿Me lo da mañana?* — Are you (formal) giving it to me tomorrow?
- *¿Usted me lo da mañana?* — Are YOU (formal) giving it to me tomorrow?
- *¿Nos lo dan mañana?* — Are you guys / they giving it to us tomorrow?
- *¿Ustedes nos lo dan mañana?* — Are YOU GUYS giving it to us tomorrow?
- *Quiero dar.* — I want to give.
- *Te lo quiero dar.* — I want to give it to you.
