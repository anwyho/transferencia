# Lesson 70

## Summary
We practice the mood tense (subjunctive) more. Things that trigger it: wanting, needing, preferring, "it's necessary." We also meet *lo que* (what, when not a question), *faltar / falta / hace falta* (to lack / it's necessary), and *preferir* (to prefer).

## Rules
1. *Qué* (with accent) = what, but only as a question. When "what" is NOT a question, use *lo que*.
2. Examples: *Vi lo que estás haciendo* = I saw what you're doing. *Yo cociné lo que comen* = I cooked what they eat.
3. "About" can be *de* or *sobre*. *No quiero que hables de / sobre lo que hablamos.* = I don't want you to speak about what we spoke about.
4. *Hablamos* can be present or past — same form. To make it clearly past, either add a time word (*el otro día*) or use the past-line form *hablábamos* (what we were speaking about).
5. *-ity* English words become *-idad* in Spanish: *necessity → necesidad*. *Necesitar* = to need.
6. After *necesito que*, the next verb goes in mood tense: *Necesito que lo prepare* = I need you (formal) to prepare it. With *usted* added for clarity: *Necesito que usted lo prepare.*
7. Pattern emerging: a subjective verb (*quiero, necesito*…) + *que* + mood-tense verb.
8. *Faltar* = to lack / to be missing / to be short of. The noun *falta* = lack. *Falta de respeto* = lack of respect. *Falta de consideración* = lack of consideration.
9. *Hace falta* = "it makes lack" = it's necessary. Just another way to say *es necesario*. Followed by *que* + mood tense.
10. *Pronto* in Spanish = soon. (In Italian, *pronto* means "ready" — that's why Italians say it on the phone. In British slang, "pronto" means soon.)
11. Example: *Hace falta que comamos pronto* = It's necessary that we eat soon.
12. *Preferir* = to prefer. *Prefiero* = I prefer (the *e* splits to *ie*).
13. *Prefiero que se quede* = I prefer that he stays. *Prefiero que se queden* = I prefer that they stay. *Prefiero que no se queden aquí* = I prefer that they don't stay here.
14. With mood tense, *se lo des* = you (informal) give it to him/her (mood tense of *das*). To clarify, add *a él* or *a ella*: *Prefiero que se lo des a él / a ella.*
15. The mood tense isn't just about structure — it's about FEELING. Train your ear to recognize the feeling of subjectivity / wishes / preferences.

## Vocabulary
- *qué* — what (question)
- *lo que* — what (non-question)
- *de / sobre* — about
- *necesidad* — necessity
- *necesitar* — to need
- *necesito* — I need
- *preparar* — to prepare
- *faltar* — to lack / be missing / be short of
- *falta* — lack (noun)
- *falta de respeto / consideración* — lack of respect / consideration
- *hace falta* — it's necessary ("it makes lack")
- *pronto* — soon (in Spanish — note: ready in Italian)
- *preferir* — to prefer
- *prefiero* — I prefer (e → ie split)
- *quedarse* — to stay (reflexive)
- *se queda / se queden* — he stays / they stay (and mood-tense versions)
- *aquí* — here
- *dar* — to give
- *des* — you give (mood tense)
- *cocinar* — to cook
- *cociné* — I cooked

## Examples / Sentences
- *Vi lo que estás haciendo.* — I saw what you're doing.
- *Yo cociné lo que comen.* — I cooked what they eat.
- *No quiero que hables sobre lo que hablamos.* — I don't want you to speak about what we spoke about.
- *No quiero que hables sobre lo que hablábamos.* — I don't want you to speak about what we were speaking about.
- *Necesito que lo prepare.* — I need you (formal) to prepare it.
- *Necesito que usted lo prepare.* — Same, clarified to "you."
- *Necesito que prepare lo que falta.* — I need you to prepare what's missing.
- *Falta de consideración / falta de respeto.* — Lack of consideration / respect.
- *Hace falta que comamos pronto.* — It's necessary that we eat soon.
- *Prefiero que se quede.* — I prefer that he stays.
- *Prefiero que se queden.* — I prefer that they stay.
- *Prefiero que no se queden aquí.* — I prefer that they don't stay here.
- *Prefiero que se lo des.* — I prefer that you give it to him/her.
- *Prefiero que se lo des a él / a ella.* — I prefer that you give it to him / to her.
