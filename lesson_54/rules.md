# Lesson 54

## Summary
The *-ado/-ido* form that comes after *haber* can ALSO be used as an adjective — exactly like English ("sold, lost, finished"). This unlocks hundreds of adjectives, and most of them go with *estar* (state).

## Rules
1. The participle after *haber* (*cerrado, perdido, vendido, terminado, cansado*) can also be used as an adjective: "is closed", "are sold", "is lost".
2. After *haber*, the participle NEVER changes (always *cerrado*: *lo he cerrado, la he cerrado, lo hemos cerrado*).
3. But when used AS an adjective, it changes for gender and number like any adjective: *cerrado* (m), *cerrada* (f), *cerrados* (m pl), *cerradas* (f pl).
4. These *-ado/-ido* adjectives almost always pair with *estar*, not *ser*. With this type of adjective don't even worry about state-vs-characteristic — just use *estar*. *La puerta está cerrada* (the door is closed).
5. Even *me gustaría haber ido* = "I would like to have gone" — after the "to" form *haber*, we still use one participle (*ido*).
6. *Lo he cerrado* = I have closed it. *La he cerrado* = I have closed it (la puerta).
7. "It is closed" (door): *Está cerrada*. NOT *Es cerrada*.
8. The exception: when describing a PERSON. "He is a closed person" = *Es una persona cerrada* — because you're really saying "he is a PERSON", and the noun (*persona*) is the most characteristic thing. Adjective is secondary, so use *ser*.
9. *Persona* is feminine even when describing a male — the word itself is feminine.
10. So: *Es un señor cerrado* feels wrong; *Es una persona cerrada* is fine. Or for "she is lost (her way)": *está perdida*. For "she is a lost person (lost her way in life)": *es una persona perdida*.
11. The general rule: when you say "it IS a [noun]", you use *ser* — even if there's an adjective tacked on, because the NOUN dominates. *Es una silla* (it's a chair). *Es una silla mojada* (it's a wet chair) — still *ser* because we're naming it.
12. But *La silla está mojada* (the chair is wet) — now we're talking about the chair's STATE, so *estar*.
13. *Cansar* = to tire (verb). *Me canso* = I get tired / I tire myself / I get fed up. *Me he cansado* = I've gotten tired. *Cansado/cansada* (the after-*haber* form) is also the adjective tired. *Estoy cansada* = I'm tired.
14. So one verb gives you THREE useful things: *me canso* (I tire myself), *me he cansado* (I have tired myself / gotten tired), *cansado* (the adjective: tired).
15. To make adjectives from any *-ar/-er/-ir* verb, just take the *-ado/-ido* form. *Vender* → *vendido* (sold). *Terminar* → *terminado* (finished). *Perder* → *perdido* (lost). *Cerrar* → *cerrado* (closed). *Mojar* → *mojado* (wet).
16. They agree with the noun: *Están vendidos* (they are sold, masc) / *Están vendidas* (the houses are sold, fem).
17. *Idea* — looks feminine, IS feminine: *la idea*. *Está cerrado a la idea* = he is closed to the idea (uses *a la* — the *a* + *la* combine: but here the teacher accepts *a la idea*).
18. *Película* (feminine) = movie. *Está terminada* = it is finished (the movie).

## Vocabulary
- *haber* — to have (past helper)
- *cerrar / cerrado* — to close / closed
- *abrir / abierto* — (implied set) to open / opened
- *perder / perdido* — to lose / lost
- *vender / vendido* — to sell / sold
- *terminar / terminado* — to finish / finished
- *cansar / cansado* — to tire / tired
- *mojar / mojado* — to wet / wet (adjective)
- *la puerta* — the door
- *la idea* — the idea
- *la película* — the movie
- *la silla* — the chair, seat
- *una persona* — a person (always feminine word)
- *un señor* — a sir / man
- *me canso / me he cansado* — I get tired / I have gotten tired
- *me gustaría haber ido* — I would like to have gone
- *un ser humano feliz* — a happy human being (*feliz* = happy)

## Examples / Sentences
- *Lo habré perdido* — I will have lost it
- *Me gustaría haber ido* — I would like to have gone
- *Lo he cerrado* — I have closed it
- *La he cerrado* — I have closed it (the door)
- *Está cerrada* — It is closed (the door)
- *Está perdida* — She is lost / it is lost (fem)
- *Estoy perdido / Estoy perdida* — I am lost (m/f)
- *Es una persona cerrada* — He/she is a closed person
- *Es una persona perdida* — He/she is a lost person
- *Está cerrado a la idea* — He is closed to the idea
- *Están vendidos* — They are sold (m)
- *Están vendidas* — They are sold (f, e.g. las casas)
- *He terminado* — I have finished
- *Está terminado / Está terminada* — It is finished (m/f, e.g. la película)
- *Me canso* — I get tired / fed up
- *Me he cansado* — I have gotten tired
- *Estoy cansada* — I am tired (fem)
- *Está cerrado* — It is closed
- *Es una silla* — It is a chair
- *Es una silla mojada* — It is a wet chair (ser, because naming)
- *La silla está mojada* — The chair is wet (estar, state)
- *Es una persona feliz* — He/she is a happy person
