# Lesson 23

## Summary
We meet "fake feminine" words ending in *-ma* that are actually masculine (because they came from Greek), and we use *me, te, nos* with *haber* to say things like "I have got lost" (literally "I have lost myself").

## Rules
1. **Words ending in *-ma* are masculine**, even though *-a* usually means feminine. Common ones: *el problema, el sistema, el programa, el aroma, el panorama, el clima* (climate).
2. **Why *-ma* words are masculine**: they came from Greek, where they were neuter. Greek neuter looks like masculine, so they got picked up as masculine in Spanish/Latin.
3. **Avoid the common error**: do NOT say *la problema* — say *el problema*.
4. **Greek-origin pattern**:
   - *problema* (Greek *próvlima*)
   - *sistema* (Greek *sýstima*)
   - *aroma, programa, panorama, clima*
5. ***Sentir* = to feel (related to "sensation")**, also "to perceive" (e.g., to smell). *¿Has sentido el aroma?* = "Have you smelt the aroma?"
6. **Reflexive past with *-me/te/se/nos***: to say "I have got lost" (English: I got lost), Spanish says "I have lost myself" — *me he perdido*.
7. **You can't say *me preparo* meaning "I get ready" by guessing**: Spanish actually says "I ready myself" — same logic. So "I get lost" → "I lose myself" → *me pierdo*; "I have got lost" → *me he perdido*.
8. **The /e/ of *me* + the /e/ of *he* blend together** when spoken fluently: *me he perdido* sounds like one smooth /me-perdido/. Don't worry — say it slowly to start.
9. **Where do little words go with *haber***: BEFORE *haber*, never between *haber* and the participle.
   - *Me he perdido.* — I've got lost.
   - *Te has perdido.* — You've got lost.
   - *Se ha perdido.* — He/she has got lost. (We'll meet *se* later — this lesson uses *me, te, nos*.)
   - *Nos hemos perdido.* — We've got lost.
10. **Emphatic + reflexive**: stack subject pronoun + reflexive: *yo me he perdido* (I, emphatic, have got lost), *tú te has perdido* (YOU have got lost), *nosotros nos hemos perdido* (WE have got lost).
11. **"You have" vs "you have lost yourself"**: *tú has perdido* = you have lost (something); *te has perdido* = you have lost yourself / got lost. The little word *te* is what makes it reflexive.
12. ***Comprender* and *entender* both = "to understand"**. Use either.

## Vocabulary
- *el problema* — the problem (masculine despite *-a*)
- *el sistema* — the system
- *el programa* — the program
- *el aroma* — the aroma
- *el panorama* — the panorama
- *el clima* — the climate
- *la casa* — the house (feminine, regular *-a*)
- *comprender* — to understand
- *entender* — to understand
- *entendido* — understood
- *sentir* — to feel / perceive
- *sentido* — felt / smelt
- *perder* — to lose
- *perdido* — lost
- *me he perdido* — I have got lost ("I have lost myself")
- *me* — me / myself
- *te* — you / yourself
- *nos* — us / ourselves
- *me preparo* — I get ready ("I ready myself")

## Examples / Sentences
- *El problema.* — The problem.
- *¿Ha entendido el problema?* — Have you (formal) understood the problem?
- *El sistema.* — The system.
- *¿Has entendido el sistema?* — Have you understood the system?
- *¿Has sentido el aroma?* — Have you felt/smelt the aroma?
- *¿Has entendido el programa?* — Have you understood the program?
- *He perdido.* — I have lost.
- *Yo he perdido.* — I have lost (emphatic).
- *Me he perdido.* — I have got lost.
- *Yo me he perdido.* — I have got lost (emphatic).
- *Has perdido.* — You have lost.
- *Tú has perdido.* — YOU have lost.
- *Te has perdido.* — You have got lost.
- *Tú te has perdido.* — YOU have got lost.
- *Lo hemos perdido.* — We have lost it.
- *Nos hemos perdido.* — We have got lost.
- *Nosotros nos hemos perdido.* — WE have got lost.
