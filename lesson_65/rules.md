# Lesson 65

## Summary
We learn how to say "else / nothing else / nobody else," fill in the past-point of *ver* (to see), meet the word *vez* (occasion / time), and learn the possessive words *mi, tu, nuestro, su* and the special word *propio* for "my own."

## Rules
1. Add *más* to *nada* or *nadie* and you get "else": *nadie más* = nobody else, *nada más* = nothing else.
2. Spanish uses double negatives — that's normal, not wrong. *No comí nada más.* = I didn't eat anything else (literally "I didn't eat nothing more").
3. Mnemonic for *comiste*: the *s* and the *t* both remind you of "you informal" — they're both in there.
4. *Ver* (to see) past-point: *vi, vio, viste, vimos, vieron*. The accent on *vieron* sits on the /e/.
5. *Vez* (V-e-z, in Spain pronounced /bes/) means "time" in the sense of an *occasion* (like "one time" or "another time"). It's feminine: *una vez, otra vez*.
6. *Una vez* = once. *Una vez más* = one more time. *Otra vez* = another time / again.
7. *Nosotros* = we. It's literally *nos* (us) + *otros* (others) — "us-others." So *otra vez* uses that same *otros* root, going backwards: *otra* = other.
8. You don't say "an other" in Spanish, just "other": *otra vez*, not "una otra vez."
9. With verbs of motion (going/coming), use *a* before another verb. *¿Saliste a bailar otra vez?* = Did you go out to dance again?
10. Hablar past-point review: *hablé, habló, hablamos, hablaste, hablaron*.
11. To say "wanting to do X" with an *-ing* feel, use the *-ndo* form: *queriendo hablar conmigo* = wanting to speak with me. Spanish uses *-ing* less than English, but here it does want it.
12. *Mi* = my, *tu* = your (informal), *nuestro* = our, *su* = his/her/their/your (formal). All four cover everyone.
13. These possessives describe the *thing being possessed*, not the possessor: *mi amigo / mis amigos*, *nuestro amigo / nuestra amiga / nuestros amigos / nuestras amigas*.
14. Possessives that end in *-o* (like *nuestro*) change for gender too: *nuestro / nuestra*. *Mi, tu, su* don't change for gender, only for number (singular/plural).
15. *Su* never becomes *sus* unless the *thing* is plural. So "I want to go to their house" = *Quiero ir a su casa*. "I want to go to their houses" = *Quiero ir a sus casas*. Don't say *sus* just because there are many "theys."
16. *Propio/propia* (related to "property") = "own." *Mi propia casa* = my own house. It agrees with the noun: *mis propios ojos* = my own eyes.
17. Common saying: *Mi casa es tu casa* = my house is your house.
18. To ask "at what time?" Spanish says "at what hour?": *¿A qué hora?* (use *hora*, not *vez*).
19. *A la vez* = "at the same time" (literally "at the time").
20. *Todo* = all. Plural *todos* = everybody. You can also say *todo el mundo* (all the world).
21. *Encontrar* = to find / encounter. *Encontré* = I found.

## Vocabulary
- *nada* — nothing
- *nadie* — nobody
- *más* — more / else
- *nada más* — nothing else
- *nadie más* — nobody else
- *ver* — to see
- *vez* — time/occasion (feminine)
- *una vez* — once / one time
- *otra vez* — again / another time
- *nosotros* — we (= nos + otros)
- *otros / otra* — others / other
- *salir* — to leave / go out
- *bailar* — to dance (related to *ballerina*)
- *querer* — to want
- *queriendo* — wanting (-ing form)
- *conmigo* — with me
- *casa* — house
- *mi casa / tu casa / su casa / nuestra casa* — my/your/his-her-their/our house
- *llegar* — to arrive
- *tarde* — late
- *hora* — hour / time (clock time)
- *a qué hora* — at what time
- *a la vez* — at the same time
- *todo* — all
- *todos* — everybody
- *todo el mundo* — everybody (all the world)
- *encontrar* — to find / to encounter
- *visitar* — to visit
- *visitación* — visitation
- *estar* — to be (location/state)
- *amigo / amiga* — friend (m/f)
- *propio / propia* — own (like "property")
- *ojo* — eye (feminine-looking but masculine; "ojo" not "I" in your)
- *auto* — car

## Examples / Sentences
- *No comí nada más.* — I didn't eat anything else.
- *¿No comiste nada más?* — Didn't you eat anything else?
- *Vi / vio / viste / vimos / vieron.* — saw forms.
- *¿No me viste anoche?* — Didn't you see me last night?
- *Lo vi una vez.* — I saw him once.
- *Los vieron una vez.* — They saw them once.
- *Quiero verlos una vez más.* — I want to see them one more time.
- *Quiero verlos otra vez.* — I want to see them again.
- *¿Saliste a bailar otra vez?* — Did you go out to dance again?
- *¿Llegaste tarde otra vez?* — Did you arrive late again?
- *Llegó tarde a mi casa otra vez.* — He arrived late to my house again.
- *Llegó a mi casa otra vez queriendo hablar conmigo.* — He arrived at my house again wanting to speak with me.
- *Mi casa es tu casa.* — My house is your house.
- *¿A qué hora llegaron a tu casa?* — At what time did they arrive at your house?
- *Todos llegaron a mi casa a la vez.* — Everybody arrived at my house at the same time.
- *No encontré nada más en tu casa.* — I didn't find anything else in your house.
- *¿Visitaste nuestra casa?* — Did you visit our house?
- *Estamos con nuestros amigos / nuestras amigas.* — We are with our friends.
- *Estoy con mis amigos.* — I am with my friends.
- *Quiero ir a su casa / a sus casas.* — I want to go to his house / to his houses.
- *Mi propia casa / mi propio auto.* — My own house / my own car.
- *Lo vi con mis propios ojos.* — I saw it with my own eyes.
- *Quiero vivir en mi propia casa.* — I want to live in my own house.
