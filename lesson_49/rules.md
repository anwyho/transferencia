# Lesson 49

## Summary
We use *llamar* (to call) for names — *me llamo* (I call myself). We meet the *tener* family of verbs (*-tain* English verbs), and learn three irregular line-past verbs: *ver* (*veía*), *ser* (*era*), *ir* (*iba*).

## Rules
1. *Llamar* = to call. *Ll* sounds like /y/ in most dialects. To introduce yourself, say *me llamo* (I call myself), not "my name is".
2. *¿Cómo te llamas?* (informal) / *¿Cómo se llama?* (formal) = What's your name? / What's his/her name?
3. Past forms work the same with *llamar*: *¿Cómo se llamaba?* = What was his/her/your-formal name? *¿Cómo se llamaban?* = What were their names?
4. *Tener* = to have (for possession, NOT for past helpers — that's *haber*).
5. The *-tain* in English verbs maps to *tener*: contain → *contener*, obtain → *obtener*, maintain → *mantener*, sustain → *sostener*. The English changes a little; the Spanish does too.
6. (Mnemonic) *man-* in *mantener* is like *mano* (hand) — to "hand-hold" / maintain.
7. *Tener* line-past: *tenía* (I/he/she/you-formal used to have), *teníamos* (we used to have). The accent stays on *-ía*.
8. Adjectives normally come after the noun in Spanish: *una casa grande* (a big house) — NOT *una grande casa*.
9. *Tengo que* + verb = "I have to" (in the sense of "I must"). *Tengo que verte* = I have to see you.
10. Use the line-past for "had to": *tenía que verte* = I had to see you / I was having to see you. English uses "had" not "having to", but Spanish uses the line-past. *¿No tenías que hacerlo ayer?* = "Didn't you have to do it yesterday? / Weren't you supposed to do it yesterday?"
11. The line-past is very regular — only a few irregular verbs.
12. *Ver* (to see) — irregular: *veo* in present (extra *e*), *veía* in line-past (we keep that extra *e*). Forms: *veía, veíamos, veían, veías*.
13. *Ser* (to be — for characteristics) — irregular line-past: *era, eras, era, éramos, eran*. Mnemonic: an "era" is a line of time in the past — easy to remember!
14. *Era estudiante* = I/he/she used to be a student. We don't need the word for "a" — *era* covers it.
15. *Ser* characteristic check: in line-past tense forms the accent doesn't move around — it's *éramos* (accent on *é*).
16. *Ir* (to go) — irregular line-past: *iba, ibas, iba, íbamos, iban*. It's a blend of *-aba* and *-ía* sounds.
17. *Iba a* + verb = "I was going to (do something)". Useful like English: *iba a llamarte* = I was going to call you. Don't forget the *a* (to) — verbs of motion need it.
18. In fluent speech *iba a hacerlo* sounds like /ibacerlo/ — three /a/'s blend together.

## Vocabulary
- *llamar* — to call
- *me llamo* — I call myself / my name is
- *cómo* — how
- *tener* — to have (possession)
- *contener* — to contain
- *obtener* — to obtain
- *mantener* — to maintain (man- like *mano*, hand)
- *sostener* — to sustain
- *tenía / teníamos* — used to have
- *una casa grande* — a big house
- *cuatro* — four (related to "quarter")
- *pero ahora* — but now
- *no me queda(n)* — I don't have any left
- *tengo / tengo que* — I have / I have to (must)
- *ayer* — yesterday
- *hacer* — to do, to make
- *ver* — to see (*veo, veía*)
- *ser* — to be (characteristics) — line-past *era, éramos*
- *estudiante* — student
- *ir* — to go — line-past *iba, íbamos, iban, ibas*

## Examples / Sentences
- *Me llamo…* — My name is / I call myself
- *¿Cómo te llamas?* — What's your name? (informal)
- *¿Cómo se llama?* — What's his/her/your-formal name?
- *¿Cómo se llamaba?* — What was his/her/your-formal name?
- *¿Cómo se llamaban?* — What were their names?
- *Tenía una casa grande* — I used to have a big house
- *Teníamos una casa grande* — We used to have a big house
- *Tenía cuatro, pero ahora no me queda(n)* — I used to have four, but now I don't have any left
- *Tengo que verte* — I have to see you
- *Tenía que verte* — I had to / was having to see you
- *Tenía que verte ayer* — I had to see you yesterday
- *¿Tenías que hacerlo ayer?* — Did you have to do it yesterday?
- *¿No tenías que hacerlo ayer?* — Didn't you have to do it yesterday? / Weren't you supposed to do it?
- *Veo / Veía / Veíamos / Veían / Veías* — I see / was seeing / we were seeing / they were seeing / you were seeing
- *Era estudiante* — I used to be a student
- *¿Eras estudiante?* — Were you a student?
- *Éramos estudiantes* — We were students
- *¿Qué eran?* — What were they?
- *Iba a llamarte* — I was going to call you
- *Iba a hacerlo* — I was going to do it
- *Íbamos a hacerlo / hacerlos* — we were going to do it / them
