# Lesson 84

## Summary
We start tidying up irregular verbs. The big group today is the **"go verbs"**: a family of verbs whose *I*-form in the present ends in *-go* (like *tengo*, *vengo*, *salgo*). In the mood tense, that *-go* swaps for *-ga* and that *-ga* spreads to ALL persons.

## Rules
1. Spanish has lots of irregularities because it's basically modern Latin — older languages tend to have more irregular bits. But the irregularities cluster into groups, so they're learnable.
2. **"Go verbs"** are verbs whose *I*-form in the present ends in *-go* instead of just *-o*. Only the *I*-form has the *-go*; the rest of the present tense looks normal.
3. *Tener* (to have, possession): *tengo, tienes, tiene, tenemos, tienen*. Only *tengo* has the *-go*.
4. *Venir* (to come): *vengo, vienes, viene, venimos, vienen*. Only *vengo* has the *-go*.
5. *Salir* (to go out / leave): I-form is *salgo*.
6. *Poner* (to put): I-form is *pongo*.
7. *Decir* (to say/tell): even more irregular — *digo*, but still has *-go*.
8. *Hacer* (to make/do): I-form is *hago*.
9. *Oír* (to hear): I-form is *oigo*.
10. *Traer* (to bring): I-form is *traigo* — takes an extra *i* before the *go*. (Spelling: t-r-a-e-r → t-r-a-i-g-o.)
11. When *traer* is part of another verb (Latin "tract"), translate it as *-tract* in English: *extraer* = extract, *atraer* = attract, *contraer* = contract, *distraer* = distract. *Me distraigo* = "I get distracted."
12. **Mood-tense rule for go-verbs:** instead of swapping vowels like normal verbs, the *-go* changes to *-ga*, and that *-ga* base is used for ALL persons (not just *I*). So *tengo → tenga* (I/he/she/you-formal mood), *tengas* (you informal mood), *tengamos*, *tengan*.
13. Memory hook for *venga*: in Spain people say *venga, vale!* (come on, ok!) all the time — especially hanging up the phone — *venga vale, vale venga*. That *venga* shows you the *-go → -ga* shift.
14. Another phone hook: when answering the phone in Spain you'll hear *diga* or *dígame* (literally "tell me / speak"). The mood tense by itself works as a command/order.
15. For positive commands, attach the object pronoun to the END of the verb: *dígame* = "tell me." (We saw this with *cómelo* etc. earlier — same trick.)
16. To say "I want you to do X" we use *quiero que* + mood-tense verb. Example: *Quiero que vengas* (I want you to come). *Quiero que lo traigas* (I want you to bring it). *No quiero que me distraigas* (I don't want you to distract me).
17. Don't confuse *traes* (you bring — normal present) with *traigas* (mood: that you bring). After *quiero que* you need the mood form.

## Vocabulary
- *tener* — to have (possession)
- *tengo / tienes / tiene / tenemos / tienen* — present of *tener*
- *venir* — to come
- *vengo / vienes / viene / venimos / vienen* — present of *venir*
- *salir* — to leave / to go out
- *salgo* — I leave / I go out
- *poner* — to put
- *pongo* — I put
- *decir* — to say / to tell
- *digo* — I say / I tell
- *hacer* — to do / to make
- *hago* — I do / I make
- *oír* — to hear
- *oigo* — I hear
- *traer* — to bring
- *traigo* — I bring
- *traes / traigas* — you bring / that you bring
- *extraer* — to extract
- *atraer* — to attract
- *contraer* — to contract
- *distraer* — to distract
- *distraigo / me distraigo* — I distract / I get distracted
- *tenga / tengas* — that I-he-she-you-formal/you-informal have (mood)
- *venga / vengas* — mood form of *venir*
- *vale* — ok (Spain)
- *diga / dígame* — tell me (formal command)
- *quiero que* — I want that... (followed by mood)

## Examples / Sentences
- *Quiero que vengas.* — I want you to come.
- *Venga, vale.* / *Vale, venga.* — Come on, ok. (Spain, very common phone sign-off.)
- *Diga.* / *Dígame.* — Tell me. (Spanish way of answering the phone.)
- *Quiero que lo traigas.* — I want you to bring it.
- *No quiero que me distraigas.* — I don't want you to distract me.
