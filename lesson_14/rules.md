# Lesson 14

## Summary
We learn *yo, él, ella* (I, he, she) and the *poner* / *-pose* verb family. We also get *tener que* = "have to," meet the rest of *tener* (*tienes, tiene, tienen*), and learn the "must" trio: *quiero / voy a / tengo que / puedo / debo* + "to" form.

## Rules
1. *Saber* is irregular: I know is *sé* (with an accent), not *sabo*. People often say *lo sé* (I know it) because *sé* alone is too short.
2. The rest of *saber* is regular: *sabe* (he/she/it/you-formal knows), *sabes* (you-informal), *saben* (they). *No sé* = I don't know.
3. You don't need to say "he" — the verb already contains it. *Quiere* = he/she wants. But you CAN add *él* / *ella* for emphasis or contrast.
4. *Él* = he. *Ella* = she. The double-l in *ella* is pronounced /yuh/ → "ey-yah."
5. Word order is flexible for emphasis: *no sé qué él quiere* and *no sé qué quiere él* are both fine — emphasis sits in voice, hands, face, not just word order.
6. To contrast: "I don't want to invite him, but she does" → *no quiero invitarlo, pero ella sí*. *Sí* (yes) does the work of "she does."
7. *Yo* = I. Use *yo* for emphasis: *yo no quiero invitarlo, pero ella sí*.
8. *Salir* (to leave / go out) is irregular like the *-go* family: I leave = *salgo*. The rest is regular: *sale* (he/she leaves), *salen* (they), *sales* (you-informal).
9. With verbs of motion like *salir*, you often want *a* before the next verb: *salgo a comer* = I'm going out to eat. (You don't need a word for "out" — it's in the verb.)
10. The *-go* irregularity is shared by *tener (tengo)*, *venir (vengo)*, *salir (salgo)*, *poner (pongo)*. You can generalize this irregularity.
11. *Tener* in the present: *tengo* (I), *tiene* (he/she/it/you-formal — note *e* split into *ie* — we'll see this trick fully later), *tienen* (they), *tienes* (you-informal).
12. *Tener que* + "to" form = "have to" (in the sense of "must"): *tengo que comer* = I have to eat. *Tengo que hacerlo* / *lo tengo que hacer* = I have to do it.
13. *Poner* = to put. I put = *pongo* (same *-go* trick). Inside other English verbs, *-pose* swaps to *-poner*: compose → *componer*, suppose → *suponer*, impose → *imponer*, expose → *exponer*, oppose → *oponer*. Generalize the *-go*: *supongo*, *opongo*.
14. *Que* can mean "what" or "that": *supongo que sí* = "I suppose so" (literally "I suppose that yes").
15. *Venir*: *vengo* (I), *viene* (he/she/it/you-formal — note *ie* split), *vienen* (they), *vienes* (you-informal).
16. Don't let English "are you coming?" trip you. In Spanish it's just *¿vienes?* — one word does it all.
17. *Cuándo* = when. *¿Cuándo vienes?* = when are you coming?
18. *Tengo que* (have to) vs *debo* (must) — same as English: *debo* is more serious/severe; *tengo que* is everyday. *Tengo que comer ahora* = I have to eat now.
19. The "spine" verbs (you can put any "to" form right after them): *quiero* (I want), *voy a* (I'm going to), *tengo que* (I have to), *puedo* (I can), *debo* (I must). *Puedo* is related to power; when you "can," you have the power to.
20. When you hear *-ar* on the end of an unknown word, suspect it's a verb. Don't translate it as a noun — *intentar* = to try (verb), not "the intention" (which would be *la intención*).

## Vocabulary
- *saber* — to know
- *sé* — I know (irregular); *lo sé* — I know it
- *sabe / sabes / saben* — knows / you know / they know
- *él* — he
- *ella* — she (ll = /yuh/)
- *yo* — I
- *querer* — to want; *quiero / quiere / quieren / quieres* — I want / he wants / they want / you want
- *ver* — to see; *ve* — he/she sees; *verla* — to see her
- *invitar* — to invite; *invitarlo* — to invite him
- *pero* — but
- *sí* — yes
- *salir* — to leave / go out; *salgo / sale / salen / sales*
- *tener* — to have; *tengo / tiene / tienen / tienes*
- *poner* — to put; *pongo*
- *componer* — to compose; *suponer* — to suppose (*supongo*); *imponer* — to impose; *exponer* — to expose; *oponer* — to oppose (*opongo*)
- *que* — what / that
- *cuándo* — when
- *hacer* — to do/make (silent *h*); *tengo que hacerlo*
- *debo* — I must
- *ahora* — now
- *intentar* — to try (verb, not "intention")
- *más tarde* — later

## Examples / Sentences
- *No sé qué quiere* — I don't know what he/she wants
- *No sé qué él quiere* / *No sé qué quiere él* — I don't know what HE wants (emphatic)
- *Quiero verla* — I want to see her
- *Ella ve* — she sees
- *No quiero invitarlo, pero ella sí* — I don't want to invite him, but she does
- *Yo no quiero invitarlo, pero ella sí* — emphasis on I
- *Salgo a comer* — I'm going out to eat
- *Tengo que comer* — I have to eat
- *Tengo que hacerlo* / *Lo tengo que hacer* — I have to do it
- *Tengo que comer ahora* — I have to eat now
- *Lo obtengo* — I get/obtain it
- *Pongo* — I put; *supongo que sí* — I suppose so; *lo opongo* — I oppose it
- *Tengo que verla* / *La tengo que ver* — I have to see her
- *Debo verla* / *La debo ver* — I must see her
- *¿Cuándo vienes?* — when are you coming?
- *¿Cuándo vienes a comer?* — when are you coming to eat?
- *Quiero saber qué tiene* — I want to know what he/she/you has
- *Voy a intentar comer más tarde* — I'm going to try to eat later
- *Puedo comer* — I can eat
- *Quiero comer* — I want to eat
