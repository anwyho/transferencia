# Lesson 22

## Summary
We extend the *haber* past to *-er* and *-ir* verbs (drop ending, add *-ido*), introduce the people-words (*yo, tú, él, ella, nosotros, ellos, ellas, usted, ustedes*) and contrast them with the little object-words (*me, te, lo, la, los, las, nos*).

## Rules
1. **Past participle for *-er* / *-ir* verbs**: drop *-er* or *-ir*, add *-ido*.
   - *vender* → *vendido* (sold)
   - *venir* → *venido* (come) — explains *bien-venido* = well-come!
   - *perder* → *perdido* (lost)
2. **Important rule**: in the WHOLE Spanish language there is only ONE difference between *-er* and *-ir* verbs — the "we" form (*-emos* vs *-imos*). Everywhere else they're identical. For the past participle, both go to *-ido*.
3. **"Little words" position rule (refined)**: little words like *lo, la, me, te, nos* go AFTER the "to" form OR BEFORE the changed verb. The simple "beginning or end" rule is misleading because nouns can come along.
4. **With *haber* + participle, the little word goes BEFORE *haber***: *lo he terminado* (I have finished it). This is rare — two changed verbs (*he* + *terminado*) sit together untouched.
5. **Subject-words ("big" pronouns)**: *yo* (I), *tú* (you informal), *él* (he), *ella* (she), *usted* (you formal), *nosotros/nosotras* (we), *ellos/ellas* (they), *ustedes* (you guys).
6. **Object-words ("small" pronouns)**: *me, te, lo, la, los, las, nos*. These are different from the subject set.
7. **English doesn't change "you"**, but Spanish does: *tú ves* (you see) vs *te ve* (he sees you). Same idea as he/him, she/her — but applied to "you" too.
8. ***Nosotros* is "we" for males/mixed**; *nosotras* for all-female. **Pronounce with /s/, not /z/** — *nosotros*, never *nozotros*.
9. ***Usted* = formal you**, contraction of *vuestra merced* ("your honor"). Plural *ustedes* = "you guys" (formal in Spain, all-purpose in Latin America). Plural ending: when a word ends in a consonant, add *-es* not just *-s*.
10. ***Usted* is flexible* in word order**: *usted lo ve* or *lo ve usted*. *Lo*, however, is NOT flexible — it stays before the verb.
11. ***Ellos* = they (male/mixed)**, *ellas* = they (all female). Spelled *e-l-l-o-s*. The double *l* is pronounced /y/ in most dialects: *eyos*. In Argentina/Uruguay it's /zh/ (*ezhos*); in Colombia /j/ (*ejos*).
12. ***Lo* is also the "neuter" object-word**: when something has no gender (a situation, an idea), use *lo*. *Lo* is the base — change *o → a* for feminine, add *-s* for plural.
13. **Building "she has lost"**: don't just feel it — pick the right word. *Ella* if "she" (subject), *la* if "her" (object). *Ella ha perdido* (she has lost) vs *la ha perdido* (he has lost her).
14. **Emphatic subjects**: add the subject pronoun for emphasis or clarity: *yo he perdido* (I have lost), *tú has perdido* (YOU have lost). Otherwise it's optional.

## Vocabulary
- *terminar* — to finish / terminate
- *terminado* — finished
- *vender* — to sell
- *vendemos* — we sell
- *vendido* — sold
- *venir* — to come
- *venimos* — we come
- *venido* — come
- *bienvenido* — welcome (well-come)
- *perder* — to lose
- *perdido* — lost
- *yo* — I
- *tú* — you (informal)
- *él* — he
- *ella* — she
- *usted* — you (formal)
- *ustedes* — you guys / you (formal plural)
- *nosotros / nosotras* — we (masc/mixed / fem)
- *ellos / ellas* — they (masc/mixed / fem)
- *me, te, lo, la, los, las, nos* — object words (me, you, him/it, her/it, them, us)

## Examples / Sentences
- *He terminado.* — I have finished.
- *Lo he terminado.* — I have finished it.
- *Los he terminado.* — I have finished them.
- *He vendido.* — I have sold.
- *Hemos vendido.* — We have sold.
- *He perdido.* — I have lost.
- *Yo he perdido.* — I (emphatically) have lost.
- *Hemos perdido.* — We have lost.
- *Nosotros hemos perdido.* — WE have lost.
- *Él ha perdido.* — He has lost.
- *Ella ha perdido.* — She has lost.
- *Has perdido.* — You have lost.
- *Tú has perdido.* — YOU have lost.
- *Te quiero.* — I love you (literally "I want you").
- *Lo veo.* — I see him/it.
- *La veo.* — I see her/it.
- *Las veo.* — I see them (fem).
- *Los veo.* — I see them (masc).
- *Él ve.* — He sees.
- *Ella ve.* — She sees.
- *Yo veo.* — I see.
- *Nosotros vemos.* — We see.
- *Tú ves.* — You see.
- *Usted ve.* — You (formal) see.
- *Lo ve usted.* / *Usted lo ve.* — You (formal) see it.
- *Usted los ve.* / *Los ve usted.* — You see them.
- *Ellos ven.* — They see.
- *Ellos los ven.* — They see them.
- *Ellos me ven.* — They see me.
