# Lesson 40

## Summary
Prepositions can be different in each language — Spanish says "fall in love OF" (*enamorarse de*), "think IN" (*pensar en*), "dream WITH" (*soñar con*). With "me" and "you informal," *con* makes funny one-word forms *conmigo* and *contigo*.

## Rules
1. Prepositions love to switch between languages. English "fall in love WITH"; Spanish "fall in love OF" (*enamorarse de*). Take an interest in the difference — it tells you something about how speakers see the world, and that helps memory.
2. *Enamorarse* = to fall in love (literally "to enamor oneself"). In the dictionary it's written as one word, but it's really a verb *enamorar* (-ar) with *se* hooked on. So: *me enamoro* = I fall in love.
3. *Me enamoro DE ti* = I fall in love with you (Spanish: "of you").
4. To say "I'm falling in love right now," use *estar* + *-ando*: *Estoy enamorándome* OR *Me estoy enamorando* — both fine. The little word can come either after the *-ando* or before *estoy*.
5. *Pensar* = to think (related to "pensive" = thoughtful). Spanish doesn't say "think about you" — it says "I think IN you" (*pienso en ti*). Possibly Arabic-influenced (Spanish + Arabic shared 800 years).
6. *En* = in, on. *Pienso en ti* = I think about you. *Pensamos en ella* = we think about her.
7. *Soñar* = to dream. The /ny/ sound is written *ñ* (n with a squiggly line) — the only letter Spanish has that English doesn't. *Ñ* is basically n-y, like in *mañana*.
8. *Soñar* is a verb where the *o* might split when stressed: *sueño* (I dream), *sueña, sueñas, sueñan*. But *soñamos* (we dream) — no split, because the stress moves to the *a*.
9. Rule: if a verb splits the vowel for one stressed form, it splits for ALL stressed forms (I, he/she/it, you informal, they/you guys), but NOT for "we" (where the stress moves to the ending).
10. Spanish doesn't say "dream OF/ABOUT" — it says "dream WITH" (*soñar con*). *Sueño con ella* = I dream of/about her. (Maybe holds an old belief that the dreamed-of person is participating with you.)
11. *Para mí* = for me. *Para ti* = for you. *De mí* = from me. *De ti* = from you. (After preposition rule.)
12. EXCEPTION with *con*: "with me" = *conmigo* (one word: c-o-n-m-i-g-o). "With you (informal)" = *contigo* (c-o-n-t-i-g-o). The *-go* comes from nowhere — just memorize.
13. *Venir* = to come. *¿Quieres venir conmigo?* = Do you want to come with me?
14. *No* before the verb makes a negative; combined with question intonation = "Don't you...?". *¿No quieres venir conmigo?* = Don't you want to come with me?
15. *¿Por qué no...?* = Why don't...? *¿Por qué no quieres venir conmigo?*
16. *Tengo* = I have. *Tengo que* + verb = I have to + verb. *Tienes que venir conmigo* = You have to come with me.
17. *Puedo* = I can. *¿Puedes venir conmigo?* = Can you come with me?
18. Voy = I go. *Voy contigo* = I'm going with you. (Related to "voyage.")
19. *Depender* = to depend (-er verb). *Depende* = it depends. Always check the verb type before changing it!
20. Spanish says "depends OF" (not "on"): *Depende de ella* = it depends on her.
21. *Sí* = yes; *si* (no accent) = if. Same word, different meaning. So *Depende de si quiere ir* = It depends on if she wants to go.

## Vocabulary
- *enamorarse* — to fall in love (with oneself)
- *me enamoro* — I fall in love
- *enamorándome* — falling in love (gerund + me)
- *pensar* — to think
- *pienso, pensamos* — I think, we think
- *en* — in, on
- *soñar* — to dream
- *sueño, sueña, soñamos* — I dream, he/she dreams, we dream
- *ñ* — n with squiggly (n-y sound)
- *mañana* — morning, tomorrow
- *con* — with
- *conmigo* — with me
- *contigo* — with you (informal)
- *venir* — to come
- *tengo, tienes* — I/you have
- *tengo que* — I have to
- *puedo, puedes* — I/you can
- *voy* — I go
- *depender* — to depend
- *depende* — it depends
- *sí* — yes
- *si* — if

## Examples / Sentences
- *Me enamoro.* — I fall in love.
- *Me enamoro de ti.* — I fall in love with you.
- *Me enamoro de él.* — I fall in love with him.
- *Estoy enamorándome.* / *Me estoy enamorando.* — I'm falling in love.
- *Pienso en ti.* — I'm thinking about you.
- *Pensamos en ti / en ella.* — We think about you / her.
- *Sueño.* — I dream.
- *Soñamos.* — We dream.
- *Sueño con ella.* — I dream about her.
- *Sueña con él.* — He/she dreams about him.
- *Para mí / para ti.* — For me / you.
- *De mí / de ti.* — From me / you.
- *Conmigo / contigo.* — With me / with you.
- *¿Quieres venir conmigo?* — Do you want to come with me?
- *¿No quieres venir conmigo?* — Don't you want to come with me?
- *¿Por qué no quieres venir conmigo?* — Why don't you want to come with me?
- *Tienes que venir conmigo.* — You have to come with me.
- *¿Puedes venir conmigo?* — Can you come with me?
- *Sueño contigo.* — I dream about you.
- *Voy contigo.* — I'm going with you.
- *Depende.* — It depends.
- *Depende de ella.* — It depends on her.
- *Si quiere ir.* — If she wants to go.
- *Depende de si quiere ir.* — It depends on if she wants to go.
