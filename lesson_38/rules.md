# Lesson 38

## Summary
We meet *se* — the magic word that means "himself, herself, itself, themselves." It's how we say "stay" (*quedarse*), "realize" (*darse cuenta*), and lots more "do-it-to-yourself" verbs.

## Rules
1. Recap: *lo, la, los, las* (4 options) collapse to *le, les* (2 options) when the meaning is "to/for him/her/you-formal/them."
2. *Me, te, nos* don't change — they already include myself, yourself, ourselves, each other.
3. To say "himself, herself, itself, themselves" we use *se*. Mnemonic: it's the start of "self." So *lo/la/los/las* all become *se* for this self-meaning.
4. *Me, te, nos* do not need *se* — they already carry the self-meaning.
5. *Se habla* = he/she/it speaks to himself/herself/itself. *Se hablan* = they speak to themselves.
6. *Nos hablamos* most naturally means "we speak to each other" (since *nos* also means "each other").
7. To clarify subject, add the pronoun: *Él se habla*, *Ella se habla*.
8. The accent on *hablan* sits on the first syllable: *HA-blan*.
9. *Quedar* = to stay/remain. To get the meaning "stay," you literally say "I remain myself": *me quedo* = I stay. *Nos quedamos* = we stay. *Te quedas* = you stay. *Se queda* = he/she stays. *Se quedan* = they stay.
10. To make a question, just intonation: *¿Te quedas?* = Are you staying?
11. To negate, put *no* before: *¿No te quedas?* = Aren't you staying? *¿Por qué no te quedas?* = Why don't you stay?
12. Pronouns for emphasis: *yo* (I), *tú* (you informal), *él/ella* (he/she), *nosotros/nosotras* (we — feminine if all-female group), *ustedes* (you guys).
13. *Querer* = to want. *Quiero* = I want. The /e/ of *querer* splits when stressed (*quiero*) — that's why we say *quiero*, not "quero."
14. When a second verb follows *querer*, it stays in the "to" form (infinitive). To attach the self-word, stick it on the end: *quiero quedarme* = I want to stay (I want to remain myself).
15. With "self" verbs in the infinitive: *quedarme* (me), *quedarte* (you), *quedarse* (himself/herself/yourself-formal/themselves), *quedarnos* (us).
16. *Quedarse* is the form you'll find in the dictionary — written as ONE word (entry: "to stay/remain oneself"). All the small word endings on the "to" form are written together: *quedarme, darse cuenta*, etc.
17. Why is the dictionary entry *quedarse* (not *quedarme*)? Because *se* is the most general "oneself" form.
18. *Quedar* without the self has other meanings too — like "to fit/suit": *Me queda bien* = it fits/suits me well.
19. With *darse cuenta*, you can build all forms: *me doy cuenta, te das cuenta, se da cuenta, nos damos cuenta, se dan cuenta*. Negative: *no me doy cuenta*.
20. *Se da cuenta* / *se dan cuenta* are ambiguous (he, she, they, you-formal, you-guys) — clarify with the pronoun: *ella se da cuenta*.

## Vocabulary
- *se* — himself, herself, itself, themselves, yourself (formal), yourselves
- *él* — he
- *ella* — she
- *yo* — I
- *tú* — you (informal)
- *nosotros / nosotras* — we (masc/fem)
- *ustedes* — you guys
- *quedar* — to stay, remain, fit, suit
- *quedarse* — to stay (oneself)
- *querer* — to want
- *quiero, quiere, quieren* — I/he-she/they-you-guys want
- *darse cuenta* — to realize

## Examples / Sentences
- *Me hablo.* — I speak to myself.
- *Nos hablamos.* — We speak to each other (or to ourselves).
- *Se habla.* — He/she speaks to him/herself.
- *Él se habla.* — He speaks to himself.
- *Ella se habla.* — She speaks to herself.
- *Se hablan.* — They speak to themselves.
- *Les hablan.* — They speak to them (different group).
- *Me quedo.* — I stay.
- *Nos quedamos.* — We stay.
- *Te quedas.* — You stay.
- *¿Te quedas?* — Are you staying?
- *¿No te quedas?* — Aren't you staying?
- *¿Por qué no te quedas?* — Why don't you stay?
- *Yo me quedo.* — I'm staying (emphasis).
- *Nosotros nos quedamos.* — We stay.
- *Nosotras nos quedamos.* — We (all-female) stay.
- *Tú te quedas.* — You stay (emphasis).
- *Se queda.* — He/she/it/you (formal) stays.
- *Se quedan.* — They/you guys stay.
- *Quiero quedarme.* — I want to stay.
- *Quiere quedarse.* — He/she wants to stay.
- *Quieren quedarse.* — They/you guys want to stay.
- *Ellos / Ellas / Ustedes quieren quedarse.* — They (m) / they (f) / you guys want to stay.
- *Me queda bien.* — It fits/suits me well.
- *Me doy cuenta.* — I realize.
- *No me doy cuenta.* — I don't realize.
- *Nos damos cuenta.* — We realize.
- *No nos damos cuenta.* — We don't realize.
- *Se da cuenta.* — He/she realizes.
- *Ella se da cuenta.* — She realizes.
- *Se dan cuenta.* — They/you guys realize.
- *No se dan cuenta.* — They don't realize.
