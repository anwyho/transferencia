# Lesson 08

## Summary
Words have gender (not objects — *words*). Feminine = *-a*, masculine = *-o*. We meet *quiere* ("he/she/it/you-formal wants"), learn how to ask questions (just lift your voice), and meet *venir* ("to come") and *debo* ("I owe / I must").

## Rules
1. **Make a word feminine: change *-o* to *-a*.*** *lo → la.* *La* = "her / it (feminine) / you (formal female)."
2. **Important: gender is on the *word*, not the object.*** Two different words for the same object can have different genders. Don't think "the house is feminine" — think "the *word casa* is feminine."
3. **Feminine words usually end in *-a*.*** *casa* (house) → *la casa.*
4. **Verb form for "he/she/it/you-formal wants" = *quiere*** (change *-o* to *-e*). The same four meanings band together — just like *es* covered "is/he is/she is/you (formal) are."
5. **The verb form already tells you the subject** — you don't need to say "he" or "she" separately.
6. **Object pronouns recap:** *me* (me), *te* (you informal), *lo* (him/it/you-formal-masc), *la* (her/it-fem/you-formal-fem). Stick on end of the to-verb.
7. **Formal vs informal *you* depends on the place.*** Spain often starts formal with strangers; Argentina almost never uses it (and when they do, it can sound sarcastic). Safe default: start formal, then mirror what locals do. Formal = *lo/la*, informal = *te*.
8. **Asking a question: just raise your voice.*** No "do" / "does" needed. *¿Quiere visitarme?* = "Do you want to visit me?" In writing, an upside-down ¿ goes at the start.
9. **Negative question — same: ¿No quiere visitarme?*** = "Don't you want to visit me?" English gets twisty; Spanish doesn't.
10. ***Por qué* = "why" (also "because").** Two words, accent on the *é*. *¿Por qué no quiere visitarme?* = "Why don't you want to visit me?"
11. ***Venir* = "to come."*** Often heard with soft *b* (/be-NIR/), sometimes /v/. Memory hooks: *conveniente* = "with coming," *bienvenido/bienvenida* = "welcome" (literally "well-come").
12. ***La casa* = "the house."*** *La* (the, fem) + *casa.*
13. ***A* = "to" — *a la casa* = "to the house."***
14. **Verbs of coming/going (*voy*, *venir*) take an *a* before connecting to another verb.*** *¿Quiere venir a visitarme?* = "Do you want to come to visit me?"
15. ***Debo* = "I owe" / "I must."*** Memory hook: English "debt" (silent b) comes from this Latin root. Use it like "must": *debo continuar* = "I must continue."
16. **Plural rule recap: add *-s*.*** *Lo → los* (masculine them); *la → las* (feminine them). *Debo identificarlas* = "I must identify them (feminine)."

## Vocabulary
- *lo* — him / it / you (formal, masculine)
- *la* — her / it (feminine) / you (formal, feminine) / "the" (feminine)
- *los* — them (masculine plural)
- *las* — them (feminine plural)
- *casa* — house (feminine)
- *la casa* — the house
- *quiere* — he/she/it wants, you (formal) want
- *visitarme* — to visit me
- *por qué* — why
- *porque* — because (same word, written together)
- *venir* — to come
- *conveniente* — convenient (memory: "with coming")
- *bienvenido / bienvenida* — welcome (well-come)
- *debo* — I owe / I must
- *identificación* — identification
- *identificar* — to identify
- *identificarlo* — to identify him / it
- *identificarlos* — to identify them (masculine)
- *identificarlas* — to identify them (feminine)

## Examples / Sentences
- *Quiero verla.* — I want to see it (fem) / her / you (formal female).
- *Quiere visitarme.* — He/she/you-formal wants to visit me.
- *¿Quiere visitarme?* — Do you want to visit me?
- *No quiere visitarme.* — He/she doesn't want to visit me.
- *¿No quiere visitarme?* — Don't you want to visit me?
- *¿Por qué no quiere visitarme?* — Why don't you want to visit me?
- *¿Quiere venir?* — Do you want to come?
- *¿Quiere venir a la casa?* — Do you want to come to the house?
- *¿Quiere venir a visitarme?* — Do you want to come to visit me?
- *¿No quiere venir a visitarme?* — Don't you want to come to visit me?
- *¿Por qué no quiere venir a visitarme?* — Why don't you want to come to visit me?
- *Debo continuar.* — I must continue.
- *Debo identificarlo.* — I must identify it / him.
- *Debo identificarlos.* — I must identify them (masc).
- *Debo identificarlas.* — I must identify them (fem).
