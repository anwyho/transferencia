# Lesson 30

## Summary
We practice *estar* by asking "how?" and "where?" questions, learn the full set of subject pronouns, and build sentences with *tener que* (have to) and *deber* (must / probably) and the reflexive *quedarse* (to stay).

## Rules
1. *Cómo* = how. Use it with *estar* to ask about someone's STATE: *¿Cómo estás?* (informal "how are you?"), *¿Cómo está?* (formal), *¿Cómo están?* (more than one person).
2. *Dónde* = where. Where someone IS located is a state, so use *estar*: *¿Dónde estás?*
3. *No sé* = I don't know. *No sé dónde estamos* = "I don't know where we are."
4. *Aquí* and *acá* both mean "here" — interchangeable in many places.
5. *Quiero estar aquí* = "I want to be here" — *quiero* + infinitive *estar*.
6. Future-by-going: *voy a estar aquí más tarde* = "I'm going to be here later." (*voy a* + verb).
7. *¿Dónde vas a estar?* = "Where are you going to be?" — break it down: *dónde* + *vas a* + *estar*.
8. Subject pronouns with *estar* (for emphasis):
   - *yo estoy* — I am
   - *tú estás* — you (informal) are
   - *él / ella está* — he / she is
   - *usted está* — you (formal) are
   - *nosotros / nosotras estamos* — we are (m / f)
   - *ellos / ellas están* — they (m / f) are
9. *Nosotros* changes to *nosotras* for an all-female group (just like adjectives).
10. *Tener* = "to have". Spanish *-tener* shows up in English as *-tain*: *maintain–mantener*, *contain–contener*, *sustain–sostener*, *entertain–entretener*, *obtain–obtener*. *Tienen* = "they have / you guys have".
11. To say "have TO" (obligation), Spanish needs an extra word: *tener QUE* + verb. Just *tienen estar* is wrong — must be *tienen QUE estar*. So "where do you guys have to be later?" = *¿Dónde tienen que estar más tarde?*
12. *Deber* = to owe / must (related to debt). It also expresses probability — just like English "he must be here by now". Conjugates regularly: *debe estar aquí* = "he must be here". The *e* in *deber* doesn't split.
13. *Deben estar cansados / cansadas* = "they must be tired" (m / f). The adjective still agrees with subject.
14. *Quedar* = to remain. To say "I stay" you literally say "I remain MYSELF" — that's a reflexive verb. So:
    - *me quedo* — I stay (lit. I remain myself)
    - *nos quedamos* — we stay (we remain ourselves)
15. The reflexive piece (*me, nos*) can ride on the front of the conjugated verb OR get attached to the end of an infinitive. Both are fine:
    - *No vamos a quedarnos* = *No nos vamos a quedar* — "we are not going to stay".
16. *Porque* = because (same as the "why" word *por qué*, but written together with no accent).

## Vocabulary
- *cómo* — how
- *dónde* — where
- *aquí* — here
- *acá* — here (interchangeable)
- *no sé* — I don't know
- *más tarde* — later (more late)
- *tarde* — late
- *vas* — you (inf) go
- *yo, tú, él, ella, usted, nosotros, nosotras, ellos, ellas* — subject pronouns
- *tener* — to have
- *tienen* — they / you guys have
- *tener que* + verb — to have to
- *deber* — to owe / must / probably
- *debe* — he/she must / owes
- *deben* — they must / owe
- *quedar* — to remain
- *quedarse* — to stay (remain oneself)
- *me quedo / nos quedamos* — I stay / we stay
- *porque* — because

## Examples / Sentences
- *¿Cómo estás?* / *¿Cómo está?* / *¿Cómo están?* — How are you? (inf / formal / plural)
- *¿Dónde estás?* — Where are you?
- *No sé dónde estamos.* — I don't know where we are.
- *Estoy aquí.* / *Estoy acá.* — I am here.
- *Quiero estar aquí.* — I want to be here.
- *Voy a estar aquí más tarde.* — I'm going to be here later.
- *¿Dónde vas a estar?* — Where are you going to be?
- *¿Dónde tienen que estar más tarde?* — Where do you guys have to be later?
- *Debe estar aquí.* — He must be here.
- *Debe estar cansado.* — He must be tired.
- *Deben estar cansadas.* — They (women) must be tired.
- *No vamos a quedarnos porque estamos muy cansados.* — We're not going to stay because we are very tired.
