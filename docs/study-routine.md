# Study Routine

Daily practice plan. Designed to fit between work and life — not a full course, but enough volume to make steady progress.

## Daily target

- **5-20 minutes of flashcards** (Anki, any subdeck or whole deck)
- **20-40 minutes of audio**, one of:
  - Cumulative drill MP3 for current bundle
  - Story MP3 for current bundle
  - Re-listen of an earlier bundle's story (consolidation)

Total daily commitment: ~30-60 minutes. Audio time is mostly during the drive — it doesn't compete with desk hours.

## Suggested weekly rhythm

| Day | Audio | Why |
|-----|-------|-----|
| Mon | Cumulative drill MP3 (current bundle) | Active production, set the week's tone |
| Tue | Story MP3 (current bundle, story 1 or 2) | Immersion, paced |
| Wed | Cumulative drill MP3 | Reinforce |
| Thu | Story MP3 (story 3 or 4) | Variety in same bundle |
| Fri | Cumulative drill MP3 | Production push before weekend |
| Sat | Catch-up Anki (longer session if backlog) | Bring SRS queue current |
| Sun | Re-listen earlier bundle's story | Consolidation, low-effort |

Anki happens every day. Audio rotates.

## Pace across bundles

Rough target: **one bundle per week**.

- Day 1-2: hear cumulative drill MP3 cold; flag cards that hit you wrong
- Day 3-5: drill cards in Anki; re-listen drill MP3 to validate
- Day 6-7: stories 1-3
- Following week: stories 4-5 + roll forward to next bundle

Don't move forward until the *current* bundle's story 5 feels comfortable on cold listen. If you're guessing more than half the words, you're not ready — re-loop.

## When to skip ahead vs slow down

Slow down if:
- Cumulative drill MP3 has cards that surprise you more than half the time
- Stories feel like a wall of unfamiliar grammar even on second listen
- You're fatiguing in Anki (review queue > 30 min/day)

Speed up (consider 2 bundles/week) if:
- Story 5 feels easy on cold listen
- Anki ease is high (Anki is rating most cards "Easy")
- You're hungry for new grammar

There's no race. Bundles aren't gates — they're checkpoints.

## What flashcards do vs what stories do

| | Flashcards | Stories |
|---|------------|---------|
| Pace | Active, fast — one card every few seconds | Passive, natural pace |
| Direction | Production-heavy (EN→ES forced) | Comprehension-heavy (listen, parse) |
| Granularity | Sentence / word level | Paragraph / narrative level |
| Failure mode | "I can recall the word" but no flow | "I follow the gist" but slow to produce |
| Best for | Building automaticity on specific patterns | Hearing patterns in connected speech |

You need both. Production without comprehension = textbook Spanish. Comprehension without production = passive learner. The card system + story system are deliberately complementary.

## Re-listening matters

Stories aren't one-and-done. A story you understood 70% the first time becomes 90% the second time, 99% the third. The *same* MP3 keeps giving — and you don't have to study it, just play it. This is why the daily routine includes re-listens.

## Flashcards: what to study

If unsure what to drill in Anki:

- **In a focused mood?** Filter to current bundle's tag (`tag:topic::04_05_verb_unlock`). Push hard on what's new.
- **In a maintenance mood?** Whole deck, let SRS pick. Reviews a mix of new and old.
- **Anxious about a specific rule?** Filter by `tag:rule::L4-big-rule`. Drill that pattern across all cards that touch it.
- **Stuck on a card type?** Filter `tag:type::transformation` or `tag:type::sentence`.

## Audio: what to listen to

- **Just got out of a lesson?** Cumulative drill MP3 for the bundle that contains it.
- **Long drive?** Story MP3 for current bundle, in order, story 1 → 5. Or queue all 5 stories of an earlier bundle for re-listen.
- **Short errand?** Cumulative drill MP3 — these tolerate interruption since they're segment-shuffled, not narrative.

## Sync to phone

Pre-rendered MP3s land in `audio/`. To get them on your iPhone:

1. **Easiest:** drag `audio/lesson_*.mp3` and `audio/stories/*.mp3` into an iCloud Drive folder. Files app on iPhone plays them directly. Bluetooth/CarPlay: yes.
2. **Cleaner:** generate a private podcast `feed.xml` from the audio folder, host on a static server, subscribe in Apple Podcasts. Auto-syncs new tracks.
3. **Manual:** AirDrop one mp3 at a time.
