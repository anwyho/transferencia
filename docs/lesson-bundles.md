# Lesson Bundles

Lessons are grouped into bundles of ≤4 to support topical cards that span more than one lesson. Boundaries follow the theme threads in `../CROSS_REFERENCES.md`.

Initial scope: lessons 1-22, 8 bundles.

## Why these boundaries

Each bundle wraps either one tight thematic unit (E: -go irregulars; F: vowel splitting; H: haber-past) or one accumulating thread that feels incomplete if split mid-lesson (C: pronunciation rules + spine verbs both close out at L9; D: I-form rule introduced L10, drilled and confirmed L11, fully conjugated L12).

A few notes on the close calls:

- **B (L4-5) is only 2 lessons.** L4 is THE BIG derivation rule (-ación → -ar) and L5 extends it. Folding them into either neighbor would dilute the unlock-moment.
- **E (L13-14) is only 2 lessons.** -go irregulars get introduced and consolidated in this pair. L15 changes topic (vowel splitting), so extending to L15 would muddy E.
- **F ends at L17, not L18,** even though L17 introduces the *we* form. The *we* form gets fully drilled in L18, and L18-19-20 belong together as the future-builders. Splitting *we* across F and G is acceptable because L17 introduces it briefly and L18 is the real teaching.
- **H (L21-22) is its own bundle, not appended to G.** G builds the future. H opens the past. Different tense families, different mental models — keep them separate.

## Beyond L22

Future bundles will be defined when content past L22 enters scope. The CROSS_REFERENCES theme threads make natural cut points around L25 (pronunciation + qu recap), L29-31 (-idad family + *-oy* verbs + *st-/sp- → e-*), L36 (real future tense), L40 (*ñ*, reflexive *enamorarse*, spine recap), etc.
