## Why these boundaries

Each bundle wraps either one tight thematic unit or one accumulating thread that feels incomplete if split mid-lesson.

A few notes on the close calls:

- **B (L4-5) is only 2 lessons.** L4 is THE BIG derivation rule (-ación → -ar) and L5 extends it. Folding them into either neighbor would dilute the unlock-moment.
- **E (L13-14) is only 2 lessons.** -go irregulars get introduced and consolidated in this pair. L15 changes topic (vowel splitting), so extending to L15 would muddy E.
- **F ends at L17, not L18,** even though L17 introduces the *we* form. The *we* form gets fully drilled in L18, and L18-19-20 belong together as the future-builders. Splitting *we* across F and G is acceptable because L17 introduces it briefly and L18 is the real teaching.
- **H (L21-22) is its own bundle, not appended to G.** G builds the future. H opens the past. Different tense families, different mental models — keep them separate.
- **K (L29-31) is the ser/estar arc.** L29 introduces the split + estoy; L30 cómo estás + full subject pronouns; L31 closes with full *ser* + *st-/sp- → est-/esp-* + *ustedes*. The three lessons read as one unit.
- **L (L32-33) is only 2 lessons.** Flexible-meaning adjectives need their own focus once the ser/estar split is set up; gerund (L34-35) is a separate mental model.
- **N (L36-37) is only 2 lessons.** Real future + the *lo/la → le/les* pronoun migration are paired by the teacher (L36 is future, L37 future of *dar* + the *le/les* shift).
- **Q (L44-45) is only 2 lessons.** Closing out gender exceptions and the gustar-style verb family before the line-past arc starts at L46.
- **R (L46-49) is 4 lessons** because the three-pasts overview (L46), *-aba* (L47), *-ía* (L48), and the irregular line-pasts (L49) all need to land together for the line-past idea to make sense.
- **S (L50-53) is 4 lessons** for the same reason: *para vs por* (L50), *estar para/por* + no -ing after preps (L51), *para = in order to* (L52), and *podía vs podría* + pluperfect (L53) form a single preposition+past block.
- **T (L54-57) is 4 lessons** because irregular participles (L54-55), relatives (L56), and the *-emos/-imos* recap (L57) all close out groundwork before point-past starts at L58.

## Beyond L57

Future bundles will be defined when content past L57 enters scope. Expected cuts:

- **U. Point-past intro** (L58-61): *-ar* point-past (-é/-ó), *-er/-ir* point-past (-í/-ió), "we" point-past anomaly, line + point together.
- **V. Point-past expansion + L-L fix + accents** (L62-68): *dar* past, *le → se* before *lo/la*, *hace + time = ago*, possessives *mi/tu/su/nuestro*, written-accent rules, *este/ese/esto* family.
- **W. Mood tense intro** (L69-73): subjunctive base — swap groups; *quiero que / necesito que / espero que*; commands; *cuando + future*.
- **X. Mood tense expansion** (L74-77): *para que* + mood; *donde/como/que/cuando + mood = wherever/etc.*; *hay/había* + mood; *es + adj + que + mood*.
- **Y. Mood tense closeout + past mood** (L78-83): *seguir + -ing*; *empezar a / terminar de*; *ojalá*; all uses of *se*; *se me + mood*; PAST MOOD TENSE; past mood after *si* (hypothetical past).
- **Z. Irregularity cleanup** (L84-88): "go verbs" mood (*-go → -ga*); go-verb commands; future contractions (*tendré/saldré*); irregular line-past + irregular point-past family; past mood from they-form.
- **(Closing)** L89-90: rule-discovery puzzle + dialects pep talk — likely a small wrap-bundle or absorbed into Y/Z.

These cuts are pencilled in based on the CROSS_REFERENCES theme threads, not yet built.
