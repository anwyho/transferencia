# Lesson 12

## Summary
We finish conjugating *-ar* verbs in the present (key vowel /a/), and start *-er/-ir* verbs (key vowel /e/). We also learn that gender lives in the WORD, not the thing — and we get reliable feminine endings (*-ión*, *-idad*, plus *-a*).

## Rules
1. *-ar* verbs use /a/ as their key vowel for he/she/it/you-formal: *contamina*, *organiza*, *vende*… wait, that last one is *-er*. For *-ar*: add *-a*.
2. Add *-n* to that key-vowel form to get they / you (plural): *contaminan*, *organizan*, *venden*.
3. Add *-s* to that key-vowel form to get you (informal singular): *contaminas*, *organizas*, *vendes*.
4. *-er/-ir* verbs use /e/ as their key vowel. So he/she/it/you-formal of *vender* (to sell) = *vende*; +n → *venden* (they/you-pl); +s → *vendes* (you-informal).
5. When building a sentence with a verb + little word, do it in TWO bites: first work out the verb, then the object — don't translate the whole English chunk at once or you'll mix up who's doing what (e.g. "she has them" vs "they have her").
6. Little words (*lo, la, los, las, me, te, nos*) go BEFORE the changed verb (e.g. *lo vendo* = I'm selling it).
7. *La* is the feminine "the" and also "her/it (feminine)." Plural: *las*. So *vendo las casas* / *las vendo* = I'm selling the houses / I'm selling them.
8. Practice using *la/las* for things, not just for people — many learners overuse *lo/los* for objects.
9. Gender lives in the WORD, not the thing. *Persona* is feminine even when it refers to a man.
10. Words ending in *-a* are usually feminine (with a few exceptions): *la casa*, *la persona*.
11. *-ión* is a feminine ending — always *la*. (e.g. *la opinión*, *la asociación*, *la celebración*, *la administración*).
12. English *-tion* often becomes Spanish *-ción* (e.g. *administración*, *asociación*).
13. English *-ity* usually becomes Spanish *-idad*, also a feminine ending: *la diversidad*, *la nacionalidad*, *la creatividad*, *la electricidad*, *la generosidad*, *la comunidad*, *la oportunidad*, *la claridad*, *la personalidad*, *la sinceridad*.
14. The *-ción* sound becomes /s/ or /th/ in most of Spain (so *nacionalidad* → /na/s/ionalidad/ or /na/th/ionalidad/).
15. Once you spot the *-idad* / *-ión* ending, you can guess the related adjective by ripping it off and tweaking. Keep -arity → -ario (cf. *vocabulario*). Examples: *electricidad* → *eléctrico*, *generosidad* → *generoso*, *oportunidad* → *oportuno*, *claridad* → *claro*, *personalidad* → *persona*, *sinceridad* → *sincero*.
16. Adjectives change ending to match: *-o* (masc.), *-a* (fem.), *-os* (masc/mixed plural), *-as* (fem. plural). E.g. *generoso / generosa / generosos / generosas*.
17. Some words break all rules: *común* (common) keeps its *-ún* and has the accent on the end. There's no rule for it — you just have to try and listen.
18. *Claro* is also conversational filler meaning "of course" — you'll hear it a lot.
19. When two little words pile up, the personal one (me, te, nos) comes FIRST, then *lo/la/los/las*. E.g. *me lo venden* = "they're selling it to me"; *nos lo venden* = "they're selling it to us."
20. Word order with negation and questions: *No me lo venden* = they're not selling it to me. *¿Por qué no me lo venden?* = why aren't they selling it to me?
21. *Algo* (something) is NOT a little word — it goes after the verb: *vende algo* = he's selling something. Compare *nos venden algo* = they're selling us something vs *nos lo venden* = they're selling it to us.

## Vocabulary
- *contaminar* — to contaminate
- *vender* — to sell (think "vending machine"); *vendo*, *vende*, *venden*, *vendes*
- *casa* — house (la, fem.)
- *opinión* — opinion (la)
- *asociación* — association (la)
- *celebración* — celebration (la)
- *administración* — administration (la)
- *diversidad* — diversity (la)
- *nacionalidad* — nationality (la)
- *creatividad* — creativity (la)
- *electricidad* — electricity (la); *eléctrico/a* — electric
- *generosidad* — generosity; *generoso/a* — generous
- *comunidad* — community (la); *común* — common (irregular!)
- *oportunidad* — opportunity; *oportuno/a* — opportune
- *claridad* — clarity; *claro/a* — clear / "of course"
- *personalidad* — personality; *persona* — person (always feminine, even for men)
- *sinceridad* — sincerity; *sincero/a* — sincere
- *algo* — something
- *nos* — us
- *me* — me / to me
- *por qué* — why

## Examples / Sentences
- *Vendo* — I sell / I'm selling
- *Lo vendo* — I'm selling it
- *Lo vendas* — wait, error — student tried to attach to "to" form alone; the option only exists with a verb
- *Las vendo* / *Vendo las casas* — I'm selling them / I'm selling the houses
- *Vende algo* — he's/she's selling something
- *No venden* — they're not selling
- *No lo venden* — they're not selling it
- *¿Por qué no lo venden?* — Why aren't they selling it?
- *Me lo venden* — they're selling it to me
- *No me lo venden* — they don't sell it to me
- *¿Por qué no me lo venden?* — Why don't they sell it to me?
- *Nos venden algo* — they're selling us something
- *Nos lo venden* — they're selling it to us
- *No nos lo venden* — they're not selling it to us
- *¿Por qué no nos lo venden?* — Why don't they sell it to us?
- *Quiero vender* — I want to sell; *Quiero venderlo* / *Lo quiero vender* — I want to sell it
