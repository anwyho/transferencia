# Lesson 01

## Summary
This is the welcome and introduction to the course. No Spanish is taught yet — instead the teacher explains *how* to learn with the "thinking method," which is mostly about going slowly and never memorizing.

## Rules
1. **Don't try to memorize anything.** Memorization gets in the way. Just understand the explanations and build sentences.
2. **Don't write anything down.** Carry the language in your head, not in a notebook. If you forget something, that's fine — that's by design.
3. **Pause the audio every time the teacher asks the volunteer student a question.** Work out your own answer *before* you hear hers, otherwise you're just watching someone else learn.
4. **Take your time. Build the answer piece by piece.** Don't rush. Think slowly to learn quickly.
5. **These are exercises, not tests.** Getting it "wrong" doesn't matter. The thinking *is* the learning.
6. **Find a calm, quiet place. Use headphones.** You need to be focused, not multitasking.
7. **Be well rested.** A tired brain takes shortcuts and makes careless mistakes.
8. **Do a little, often.** 20 minutes a day beats hours crammed on the weekend.
9. **If you tune out, take a break.** More energy = more absorption.
10. **You can always repeat the course.** That should kill any anxiety about forgetting.
