# Lesson 34

## Summary
We learn the Spanish *-ing* form (the gerund: *-ando* / *-iendo*), used ONLY for "right now in this moment" — not for future or general activities like English *-ing*.

## Rules
1. Recap: *hablar* (to speak) → *hablo* (I speak / I'm speaking). *Dormir* (to sleep, related to "dormant") → *duermo* (I sleep / I'm sleeping). The o-to-ue split happens in *dormir*, but NOT in *comer* (so *como* stays *como*).
2. *Me duermo* = "I fall asleep" (literally "I sleep myself" — reflexive twist gives a slightly different meaning).
3. *Desayunar* = to have breakfast. Trick: Spanish *des-* = "un-", *ayunar* = "to fast". So *desayunar* = "un-fast" = "break-fast". *Desayuno* is also the noun: *el desayuno* = the breakfast.
4. *Esperar* = to wait OR to hope. Same trick: *des-esperar* / *desesperado* (desperate) = "un-hopeful".
5. Up to now we've been giving present-tense verbs TWO English translations: *hablo* = "I speak" or "I'm speaking". That's because English overuses *-ing*. Spanish present tense covers both — and even covers the future when there's a future word: *lo hace mañana* = "he's doing it tomorrow".
6. There IS a true Spanish *-ing* form, but you ONLY use it for "what I'm doing RIGHT NOW, in this moment". Not for the future, not for general activities.
7. To build it: *estoy* (I am) + the *-ando/-iendo* form. We use *estar* because what you're doing IS a state.
8. The *-ing* rule:
   - *-ar* verbs: drop *-ar*, add *-ando*. *hablar* → *hablando*, *preparar* → *preparando*, *desayunar* → *desayunando*.
   - *-er* and *-ir* verbs: drop *-er*/*-ir*, add *-iendo*. *comer* → *comiendo*, *vender* → *vendiendo*.
9. So *estoy hablando* = "I'm speaking (right now)". *Estoy esperando aquí* = "I'm waiting here right now".
10. Both *espero aquí* and *estoy esperando aquí* are valid, with a slight nuance: *espero aquí* sounds more like "I'll wait here", *estoy esperando aquí* is firmly "I'm waiting here right now".
11. What you CAN'T do is use the Spanish *-ing* for the future. "I am eating with her tomorrow" CANNOT be *estoy comiendo con ella mañana* — say *como con ella mañana* instead.
12. Object pronouns (*lo*, *la*, *los*, *las*) can either go before *estoy* OR get tacked onto the end of the *-ando/-iendo* word (with an accent added):
    - *Lo estoy preparando* — I'm preparing it.
    - *Estoy preparándolo* — same meaning, pronoun glued on.
    - *Están vendiéndolo* / *Lo están vendiendo* — they are selling it.
13. *Vender* = to sell. Memory trick: vending machine.

## Vocabulary
- *hablar* — to speak
- *hablo / hablando* — I speak / speaking
- *dormir* — to sleep / be dormant
- *duermo / me duermo* — I sleep / I fall asleep
- *comer* — to eat
- *como / comiendo* — I eat / eating
- *desayunar* — to have breakfast (un-fast)
- *desayuno* — I have breakfast / the breakfast (noun)
- *ayunar* — to fast
- *des-* — un-
- *esperar* — to wait / to hope
- *espero / esperando* — I wait/hope / waiting/hoping
- *desesperado* — desperate (un-hopeful)
- *preparar / preparación* — to prepare / preparation
- *preparo / preparando* — I prepare / preparing
- *vender* — to sell
- *vendo / vendiendo* — I sell / selling
- *mañana* — tomorrow

## Examples / Sentences
- *Hablo.* / *Estoy hablando.* — I speak / I'm speaking (right now).
- *Estoy esperando aquí.* — I'm waiting here right now.
- *Espero aquí.* — I'll wait here / I wait here.
- *Lo preparo mañana.* — I'm preparing it tomorrow (future — use present, not -ing).
- *Lo estoy preparando.* / *Estoy preparándolo.* — I'm preparing it (right now).
- *Estamos desayunando.* — We're having breakfast (right now).
- *Estoy comiendo.* — I'm eating.
- *Está comiendo.* — He / she is eating.
- *Lo venden mañana.* — They are selling it tomorrow.
- *Están vendiéndolo.* / *Lo están vendiendo.* — They are selling it right now.
