# Lesson 18

## Summary
We learn the only place *-er* and *-ir* verbs differ in the entire language: the *we* form. *-ar* → *-amos*, *-er* → *-emos*, *-ir* → *-imos*. We meet *preferir, venir, vivir* and learn *aquí / acá* (here), *dónde* (where), and *siempre* (always).

## Rules
1. *we* form endings: *-ar* verbs → *-amos*; *-er* verbs → *-emos*; *-ir* verbs → *-imos*. This is the ONLY place in the entire language where *-er* and *-ir* verbs split apart — everywhere else they're identical.
2. *Preferir* (to prefer) is *-ir*. So: *prefiero* (I prefer — *e* splits), *prefiere*, *prefieren*, *prefieres*, *preferimos* (we — no split, stress moved off root; also *-imos* shows the *-ir* identity).
3. *Venir* (to come, also *-ir*): *vengo, viene, vienen, vienes, venimos* (we come / we're coming).
4. *Vivir* = to live (cf. *viva la revolución!*, *viva Las Vegas!*). *Vivo, vive, viven, vives, vivimos*. *-ir* verb so *we* form gets *-imos*.
5. The Spanish letter *v* sounds like a soft *b*. Don't be afraid to say it that way. (E.g. *verano* = summer, often heard /berano/.) Pronounce like the people around you for best understanding.
6. *Saber* — note it's spelled with *b* (not *v*); pronounced as a soft b regardless.
7. Match the local accent: in Colombia speak Colombian; in Buenos Aires, Rio de la Plata; in Madrid, Madrileño. The closer you sound, the better you understand.
8. Vowel-harmony trap returns: when starting *vivir* you might say "veve" because the *e* of the ending invites a matching *e* at the start. Stop, go back to *vivir*, peel the *-ir*, you have *viv-*, add *-e* → *vive*. Take control.
9. *Aquí* = here (Spain). *Acá* = here (Latin America). Both are understood everywhere.
10. *Dónde* = where. *¿Dónde viven?* = where do they live? *Quiero saber dónde viven* = I want to know where they live.
11. *Pero él vive aquí* — for emphasis, drop in *él* (he) — this is the "but HE does" pattern again.
12. *Entender* (to understand) — recap: *entiendo, entiende, entienden, entendemos*. Note we vs they have different forms (*entendemos* vs *entienden*).
13. Recap *-er we*: *entendemos, perdemos, vendemos*, etc. *-ir we*: *vivimos, venimos, preferimos*.
14. *Siempre* = always. Word order is flexible: *siempre perdemos* OR *perdemos siempre* both mean "we always lose."

## Vocabulary
- *preferir* — to prefer (-ir)
- *prefiero / prefiere / prefieren / prefieres / preferimos* — prefer forms
- *venir* — to come; *vengo / viene / vienen / vienes / venimos*
- *vivir* — to live
- *vivo / vive / viven / vives / vivimos* — live forms
- *aquí* — here (Spain)
- *acá* — here (Latin America)
- *saber* — to know (with *b*); soft b sound
- *verano* — summer (often heard /berano/)
- *dónde* — where
- *entender* — to understand; *entiendo / entiende / entienden / entendemos*
- *perder* — to lose; *perdemos* — we lose
- *siempre* — always
- *viva* — long live

## Examples / Sentences
- *Prefiero / Prefiere / Prefieren / Preferimos* — I/he/they/we prefer
- *Venimos* — we come / we're coming
- *Viva la revolución* — long live the revolution
- *Vivo / Vive / Viven / Vives* — live forms
- *Vivimos* — we live (note *-imos* for *-ir*)
- *Vivo aquí* / *Vivo acá* — I live here
- *No vivo aquí* — I don't live here
- *Pero él vive aquí* — but he lives here
- *No vivo aquí, pero él vive aquí* — I don't live here, but he does
- *Viven aquí* — they live here
- *¿Dónde viven?* — where do they live?
- *Quiero saber dónde viven* — I want to know where they live
- *Entiendo / Entiende / Entienden / Entendemos* — understand forms
- *No entiende* — he doesn't understand
- *¿Por qué no entiende?* — why doesn't he understand?
- *Quiero saber por qué no entiende* — I want to know why he doesn't understand
- *Perdemos* — we lose
- *Siempre perdemos* / *Perdemos siempre* — we always lose
