# Lesson 53

## Summary
We pin down two different "could's" — *podía* (past, "I was able to / I couldn't") vs *podría* (future, "I would be able to / could tomorrow"). And we meet "I had…" using *había* + participle for past-of-past.

## Rules
1. *Poder* = to be able, can. There are TWO English "could's", and Spanish has two too.
2. Future "could" / "would be able to" = *podría* (with the would-ending *-ía* added to *poder*; the *e* falls out: *podería* → *podría*). Used for "I could do it tomorrow".
3. Past "could" / "I was able to" = *podía* (line-past *-ía* on the chopped stem). Used for "I couldn't do it yesterday".
4. We make the line-past for *-er* verbs by chopping the *-er* and adding *-ía*. *Poder* → *podía, podías, podía, podíamos, podían*.
5. Most of the time, when "could" refers to the past, you'll use *podía*. The "dot in the past" form (preterite, learned later) is sometimes interchangeable, sometimes not.
6. *Porque* = because. *No tenía el tiempo* = I didn't have the time.
7. The choice between line-past and dot-past in Spanish is NOT just translating English — it's a context decision. We can already start practicing this even before we've formally met the dot-past.
8. *Ayer* = yesterday. *No podía hacerlo ayer* = I couldn't do it yesterday.
9. To say "I had eaten / I had gone" (past-of-past), always use the LINE-past of *haber* + participle. *Había* = I/he/she/you-formal had. So *había comido* = I had eaten. The line-past is the only choice here for the helper.
10. The participle after *haber* doesn't change — same rule as before: *comido, ido, hablado, etc.*
11. *Ya* = already. It usually goes at the BEGINNING of the sentence — that's the most natural, least emphatic position. *Ya había comido* = I had already eaten.
12. *Querer* = to want. *Quería* = he/she/I wanted. We use the line-past with *querer* because wanting is usually ongoing, not a single instant.
13. With "with me" you DON'T say *con mí* — say *conmigo* (one word, irregular).
14. When two people are contrasted in a sentence, throw in *yo* / *él* to keep them straight: *Él quería cenar conmigo, pero yo ya había cenado* = He wanted to dine with me, but I had already dined.
15. When translating "I had dined", don't try to translate "dined" alone — find the form that comes after *haber* (*cenado*). Same trick saves pain everywhere.
16. *Buscar* = to look for. *Lo buscaba* = he was looking for it.
17. *Perder* = to lose. *Lo había perdido* = I had lost it. *Lo habría perdido* = I would have lost it.
18. *Habría* (with the *e* dropped) is used the same way *había* is — irregularity formalized in writing because that *e* falls so naturally. Only happens with a few verbs.
19. *Me habría perdido* = I would have got lost / I would have lost myself.

## Vocabulary
- *poder* — to be able, can
- *podía / podías / podía / podíamos / podían* — could / was able to (past)
- *podría* — would be able to / could (future)
- *hacer / hacerlo* — to do / to do it
- *porque* — because
- *no tenía el tiempo* — I didn't have the time
- *ayer* — yesterday
- *haber* — to have (past helper)
- *había / habías / había / habíamos / habían* — I/you/he/we/they had (line-past of haber)
- *habría* — would have (irregular contraction of haberí­a)
- *ya* — already
- *querer / quería* — to want / wanted
- *cenar / cenado* — to dine / dined
- *conmigo* — with me (irregular, not *con mí*)
- *buscar / buscaba* — to look for / was looking for
- *perder / perdido* — to lose / lost
- *me habría perdido* — I would have got lost

## Examples / Sentences
- *Podía* — I/he/she could (past)
- *Podríamos* — we could (future)
- *No podía hacerlo* — I couldn't do it
- *No podía hacerlo porque no tenía el tiempo* — I couldn't do it because I didn't have the time
- *No podía hacerlo ayer* — I couldn't do it yesterday
- *Había* — I/he/she had
- *Había comido* — I had eaten
- *Ya había comido* — I had already eaten
- *Quería cenar conmigo* — he wanted to dine with me
- *Pero ya había cenado* — but I had already dined
- *Él quería cenar conmigo, pero yo ya había cenado* — He wanted to dine with me but I had already dined
- *Lo buscaba* — he was looking for it
- *Porque yo lo había perdido* — because I had lost it
- *Lo buscaba porque yo lo había perdido* — he was looking for it because I had lost it
- *Lo habría perdido* — I would have lost it
- *Me habría perdido* — I would have got lost / lost myself
