# Lesson 36

## Summary
We learn the Spanish FUTURE tense — built by taking the whole infinitive ("to" form) and tacking on the *haber* endings (*-é, -ás, -á, -emos, -án*). The accent jumps to the end, "pushing it into the future".

## Rules
1. Quick recap of how to talk about the future without the future tense:
   - With future context (like *mañana*), just use the present: *desayuno con ellos mañana* = "I'm having breakfast with them tomorrow".
   - Or use *voy a* + verb: *voy a desayunar con ellos* = "I'm going to have breakfast with them".
   - Both work even WITH future context: *voy a desayunar con ellos mañana* is also fine.
2. "With them" in Spanish is literally "with they": *con ellos*. (Not *con les* or anything like that.)
3. There IS a true future tense, and luckily we don't learn any new sounds — its endings are just the forms of *haber* (the verb behind "I have eaten / I have gone").
4. Recap of *haber* (very irregular): the trick is the two vowel sounds /a/ and /e/.
   - *he* — I have (h-e, just /e/ sound; *h* is silent)
   - *hemos* — we have
   - *ha* — he/she/it/you (formal) has
   - *han* — they / you guys have
   - *has* — you (informal) have
5. To build the FUTURE: take the verb's "to" form (the infinitive — *-ar*, *-er*, or *-ir* doesn't matter) and add the *haber*-shaped endings on the end. Stress the ending — you're literally pushing the accent into the future.
6. Future endings:
   - *-é* (I) — like *he*
   - *-ás* (you informal) — like *has*
   - *-á* (he/she/it/usted) — like *ha*
   - *-emos* (we) — like *hemos*
   - *-án* (they / you guys) — like *han*
7. Example with *comer*: *comeré* (I will eat), *comerás* (you will eat), *comerá* (he will eat), *comeremos* (we will eat), *comerán* (they will eat).
8. Cool fact: many languages use accent shifts to mark time. Greek pulls the accent BACK for past tense. Spanish PUSHES it forward for future. Accents reflect time.
9. *Ir* (to go) is super-irregular in present (*voy, vas, va, vamos, van*) but TOTALLY regular in the future. Just add the endings to *ir*: *iré, irás, irá, iremos, irán*.
10. *Irán a Irán* = "they will go to Iran" (the country and the verb form sound the same!).
11. Question forms work the same way: *¿Irás?* (Will you go?), *¿No irás?* (Won't you go?), *¿Por qué no irás?* (Why won't you go?).
12. Past-tense recap with *haber*: *he dormido* (I have slept), *hemos dormido* (we have slept), *ha dormido* (he has slept). With *haber*, the *lo / la / los / las* MUST go before *haber* — no option to glue it on the end. *Lo han recomendado* = they have recommended it.
13. *Quedar* (to remain) future: *quedaré* (I will remain). To get "I will stay" you still need the reflexive *me*: *me quedaré* = "I will stay (here)". Without the *me*, *quedaré* just means "I will remain".
14. Common learner mistake: building the future without thinking out the whole infinitive first. Don't say *preparé* for "I will prepare". Think out *preparar* first, then add *-é*: *prepararé*. Same for *esperaré* (I will wait), *organizará* (he will organize), *organizarán* (they will organize).
15. Bonus benefit: because the accent is always on the ending in the future tense, you don't have to think about where the stress goes. It's always on the last syllable.
16. *Cansado* (tired) is a state, so "I will be tired" uses *estar*: *estaré cansada / cansado*.
17. *Será* = "it will be" (from *ser*). Recognize from the song *Que será, será* ("Whatever will be, will be").
18. Reality check: even though the future tense exists and is easy, in everyday Spanish people often use the present tense or *voy a / vamos a* + verb instead. So know the future tense, but don't expect to need it constantly.

## Vocabulary
- *con ellos* — with them
- *desayuno / desayunar* — I have breakfast / to have breakfast
- *voy a + verb* — I'm going to ... (planned future)
- *haber* — to have (auxiliary)
- *he, has, ha, hemos, han* — present forms of *haber*
- *dormir / dormido* — to sleep / slept (past participle)
- *recomendar / recomendación* — to recommend / recommendation
- *comer / comeré / comerá / comerás / comeremos / comerán* — to eat (future tense)
- *ir / iré / irás / irá / iremos / irán* — to go (future tense)
- *Irán* — Iran (the country)
- *encontrar* — to find / to encounter
- *encontraré / encontrará / encontraremos / encontrarán* — find (future)
- *quedar / quedaré / me quedaré* — to remain / I will remain / I will stay
- *preparar / prepararé* — to prepare / I will prepare
- *esperar / esperaré* — to wait/hope / I will wait
- *organizar / organizará / organizarán* — to organize / he will organize / they will organize
- *cansado* — tired
- *estaré* — I will be (state)
- *seré / será* — I will be / it will be (characteristic)

- *Desayuno con ellos mañana.* — I'm having breakfast with them tomorrow.
- *Voy a desayunar con ellos.* — I'm going to have breakfast with them.
- *Voy a desayunar con ellos mañana.* — I'm going to have breakfast with them tomorrow.
- *He dormido.* — I have slept.
- *Hemos dormido.* — We have slept.
- *Ha dormido.* — He has slept.
- *Lo han recomendado.* — They have recommended it.
- *Comeré.* — I will eat.
- *Comerás. Comerá. Comeremos. Comerán.* — Future of *comer* (full set).
- *Iré. Iremos. Irá. Irán. Irás.* — Future of *ir*.
- *Irán a Irán.* — They will go to Iran.
- *¿Irás?* / *¿No irás?* / *¿Por qué no irás?* — Will you go? / Won't you go? / Why won't you go?
- *Lo encontraré. Lo encontraremos. Lo encontrará. Los encontrará. Los encontrarán.* — I/we/he/they will find it/them.
- *Me quedaré aquí.* — I will stay here.
- *Prepararé.* — I will prepare.
- *Esperaré.* — I will wait.
- *Organizará. Organizarán.* — He will organize. They will organize.
- *Estaré cansada / cansado.* — I will be tired.
- *Será.* — It will be.
- *Que será, será.* — Whatever will be, will be.
